    Scope

    Question¶

    How do the New Testament writers reconcile strict Old Testament monotheism ('YHWH alone is God') with attributing divine titles, worship, creation, and judgment to Jesus Christ? What does 'one God' mean in light of the NT's Christological claims? (Capstone synthesis of trin-01 through trin-07.)

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Seven prior studies (trin-01 through trin-07) established: (1) prototokos/arche are sovereignty titles, not createdness markers (trin-01); (2) subordination passages describe voluntary mission, not ontological inferiority — meizon vs. kreitton distinction, Phil 2:5-11 kenosis framework (trin-02); (3) monos+theos doxologies cannot function as absolute exclusion without contradicting NT testimony about Christ; 1 Cor 8:6 distributes the Shema between Father and Christ; three YHWH-exclusive OT texts applied to Jesus (trin-03); (4) the ransom requires divine sacrifice — Psa 49:7-8 excludes merely human ransom; Acts 20:28 'God's own blood' (trin-04); (5) 'my God' language and 'none good but God' are implicit deity claims, not denials — Psa 45:6-7 two-elohim pattern, worship accept/refuse binary (trin-05); (6) Holy Spirit possesses personal faculties and is identified with YHWH in three independent passages (trin-06); (7) six interlocking domains collapse without Christ's deity — worship, forgiveness, creation, savior exclusivity, prayer/omnipresence, atonement (trin-07). Additional studies addressed indirect deity claims (ego eimi matrix), Elohim plural grammar (echad vs. yachid), and Revelation worship patterns. Web research identified three major scholarly frameworks: Bauckham's divine identity model, Hurtado's devotion model, Wright's narrative model — plus dissenting agency/angelomorphic models (Perriman, McClellan, Dunn).

    Johannine Christology: John's Logos theology, ego eimi sayings, and deity confessions - John 1:1-3 — The Word was God (qualitative theos) + all things made through him — Logos Christology - John 1:14 — Word became flesh — incarnation bridge between deity and humanity - John 1:18 — Monogenes theos / monogenes huios — the only-begotten who declares God - John 5:22-23 — All judgment given to Son so that all may honor Son AS they honor Father — equal honor claim - John 8:58 — Ego eimi — absolute 'I AM' echoing Exod 3:14 / Isa 43:10 LXX; triggered stoning attempt - John 10:30-33 — Hen (neuter) = one in essence, not person; Jews understood as deity claim (v.33) - John 14:9-11 — Seeing me = seeing the Father; mutual indwelling claim - John 17:3 — Monos alethinos theos — 'only true God' phrase; must not exclude Christ from immortality/wisdom if monos is absolute - John 17:5 — Pre-incarnate glory shared with the Father 'before the world was' - John 20:28 — Thomas: 'ho Kyrios mou kai ho Theos mou' — full deity confession accepted by Jesus

    Pauline Christology: Paul's Shema reformulation, kenosis hymn, and deity attributions - 1 Cor 8:6 — Shema reformulated: one God the Father (ek) + one Lord Jesus Christ (dia) — Christ placed within the Shema - Phil 2:5-11 — Morphe theou, kenosis, harpagmos, Isa 45:23 quotation applied to Christ — central Christological passage - Col 1:15-17 — Firstborn of creation + all things created by/for him + he is before all things — creation agency - Col 2:9 — Theotetos (not theiotes) dwells bodily — fullness of essential deity, not mere divine quality - Rom 9:5 — 'Christ...who is over all, God blessed forever' — doxology identifying Christ as God - Rom 10:9-13 — Joel 2:32 ('call on the name of YHWH') applied to calling on Jesus as Lord - 1 Cor 15:24-28 — Son's future subjection to the Father — mediatorial mission completion vs. permanent hierarchy (Perriman's objection) - Titus 2:13 — Granville Sharp Rule: 'our great God and Savior Jesus Christ' — single referent

    Hebrews Christology: Son as charakter of God's hypostasis, OT quotation chain, angel superiority - Heb 1:1-4 — Son as heir, creator, radiance of glory, charakter tes hypostaseos — exact imprint of God's substance - Heb 1:6 — God commands angelic worship of the Son — proskynesatosan imperative - Heb 1:8-12 — Psa 45:6-7 and Psa 102:25-27 applied to Christ — 'Thy throne O God' + YHWH creator text transferred

    Revelation Christology: Alpha-Omega titles, co-worship, throne sharing - Rev 1:8 — Alpha and Omega, the Almighty — applied to YHWH/Christ - Rev 1:17-18 — First and Last who was dead and is alive — Isa 44:6 title on the resurrected Christ - Rev 5:8-14 — Co-worship of Lamb and throne-sitter; singular verb for joint reign; every creature gives praise - Rev 22:13 — Alpha-Omega + First-Last + Beginning-End — all three title pairs combined in Christ's final self-identification

    Trinitarian formula and Spirit passages: Matt 28:19 baptismal formula, Spirit-YHWH identifications, triadic patterns - Matt 28:19 — Singular onoma for three persons: Father, Son, Holy Spirit — shared divine name

    Dissenting model test passages: Passages used to argue against Christ's deity — delegation, subordination, agency - Acts 2:36 — 'God made him both Lord and Christ' — bestowed lordship claim (Perriman's delegation argument) - 1 Cor 15:24-28 — Son's future subjection to the Father — mediatorial mission completion vs. permanent hierarchy (Perriman's objection) - 1 Tim 6:15-16 — Monos+sovereign doxology — if applied exclusively to Father, Christ lacks immortality/sovereignty

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The Shema Reformulated: Deut 6:4 + 1 Cor 8:6: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1520-heîs.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2316-theos.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G1223-diá.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G1537-ek.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/H259-echad.md. The research agent should run greek_parser.py --verse on 1CO 8:6 and retrieve Deut 6:4 from the Hebrew parser. The critical question is whether Paul's distribution of Shema vocabulary (heis theos assigned to Father, heis kyrios assigned to Christ) constitutes an inclusion of Christ within the divine identity or merely a functional delegation. Perriman's counter-argument (ek = source, dia = agency = hierarchy) must be tested against Rom 11:36 where Paul uses ex autou kai di autou kai eis auton of the Father alone, demonstrating that ek and dia can describe the same person. Also check Perriman's claim that Malachi 2:10-12 rather than Deut 6:4 is the background text. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on 1CO 8:6. Verses: Deut 6:4, 1 Cor 8:6, Rom 11:36, Isa 44:24, Isa 45:5-6 Strong's: G1520, G2316, G2962, G1223, G1537, H259

    2. YHWH-Title Transfer System Across Multiple Authors: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md and $WORD_STUDIES/H3068-yhwh.md. The research agent should retrieve full text for each OT-NT pair and parse the Greek of the NT quotation to confirm it echoes the LXX rendering of the YHWH text. For Phil 2:10-11, READ the passage analysis at $GRAMMAR_REF/passages/phil-2-6-11.md. For Heb 1:10-12, READ $GRAMMAR_REF/passages/heb-1-8-10.md. The Alpha-Omega / First-Last title sharing across Rev 1:8, 1:17-18, 2:8, 21:6, 22:13 should be mapped against Isa 41:4, 44:6, 48:12. The counter-argument that different persons bear the same titles with different meanings must be addressed — Isaiah 44:6 uses 'First and Last' as an exclusive monotheistic claim ('beside me there is no God'), so if Jesus bears this title, the monotheistic framework demands he share the divine identity. Verses: Isa 45:21-23, Phil 2:9-11, Joel 2:32, Rom 10:9-13, Psa 102:25-27, Heb 1:10-12, Isa 44:6, Rev 1:17-18, Rev 22:13 Strong's: G2962, H3068, G2578, G746, G2078

    3. John 1:1c — Qualitative Theos and Logos Christology: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3056-logos.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2316-theos.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G1510-eimi.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G1096-ginomai.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G4314-pros.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G3439-monogenḗs.md. READ the grammar reference $GRAMMAR_REF/greek/anarthrous-predicate-nominative.md which covers Colwell's Rule, Harner's qualitative correction, and Wallace's spectrum — this entry has parser-verified analysis of John 1:1c specifically. The research agent should run greek_parser.py --verse on JHN 1:1, JHN 1:3, JHN 1:14, JHN 1:18. The en/egeneto verb distinction is critical: the Word 'was' (en, imperfect of eimi = continuous existence) while all created things 'came into being' (egeneto, aorist of ginomai = inception). This places Christ on the uncreated side of the divide. Also check the pros ton theon construction in 1:1b — the Word was 'toward/with' God, indicating relational distinction within shared identity. Verses: John 1:1-3, John 1:14, John 1:18 Strong's: G2316, G3056, G1510, G1096, G4314, G3439

    4. John 10:30 — Neuter hen and the Jewish Response: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1520-heîs.md for the masculine/neuter distinction. The research agent should run greek_parser.py --verse on JHN 10:30 to verify hen is neuter singular and esmen is first-person plural. The plural verb (esmen, 'we are') confirms two distinct persons, while the neuter hen ('one thing/essence') asserts essential unity — a masculine heis ('one person') would have asserted personal identity (modalism). The immediate context strengthens the reading: Jesus claims to give eternal life and hold sheep in his hand (10:28-29), which are YHWH's prerogatives (Deut 32:39). The Jewish response in 10:33 ('You, being a man, make yourself God') constitutes contemporaneous interpretation evidence — trained Torah scholars heard a deity claim. Compare John 14:9-11 mutual indwelling and John 17:5 pre-incarnate glory. Verses: John 10:30-33, John 14:9-11, John 17:5 Strong's: G1520, G1510, G2316

    5. Col 2:9 — Theotetos vs. Theiotes Distinction: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2730-katoikeo.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G4983-soma.md. G2320 (theotes/theotetos) is a hapax in the NT — it denotes the essence/being of deity itself, distinguished from G2305 (theiotes) in Rom 1:20 which denotes divine quality or attributes that creation can display. Trench's distinction: theotes = the being of God, theiotes = the quality of being like God. The research agent should run search_strongs.py --lexicon G2320 and search_strongs.py --lexicon G2305 to verify the lexical distinction. Parse Col 2:9 with greek_parser.py --verse to check katoikei (present tense = permanent dwelling, not temporary visitation) and somatikos (hapax, bodily). Connect to Col 1:19 where pleroma (fullness) also dwells in Christ. This is the strongest single-verse evidence that Paul predicates essential deity of Christ, not merely divine-like qualities. Verses: Col 2:9, Rom 1:20, Col 1:19 Strong's: G2320, G2305, G2730, G4138, G4983

    6. Heb 1:1-14 — Charakter tes Hypostaseos and the Angel Contrast: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5481-charaktēr.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G4352-proskyneo.md.
