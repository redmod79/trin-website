    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    When Jesus says 'the only true God' (John 17:3) and Paul says 'one God, the Father' (1 Corinthians 8:6), do these passages exclude Jesus from deity? Examine the full chapter context of John 17 and the Shema background of 1 Corinthians 8:6.

    G3441 — monos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3441-mónos.md

    Occurrences: 47

    Adjective meaning 'alone, only.' 47 NT occurrences. Two main senses: (1) physical isolation/solitude, (2) exclusivity/restriction. In doxological usage (1 Tim 1:17; 6:15-16; Jude 1:25; Rom 16:27; Rev 15:4), monos ascribes unique divine qualities to God — 'the only wise God,' 'only Potentate,' 'who only hath immortality.' The key question for John 17:3 is whether monos functions exclusively (ruling out the Son) or contrastively (ruling out false gods). The doxological pattern shows monos praises the one true God vs. idols: if monos excluded the Son, Christ could not be immortal (contradicting Rev 1:18), could not be King of kings (contradicting Rev 19:16), and could not be the object of equal honor (contradicting John 5:23).

    Semantic categories: physical isolation, exclusivity/restriction, doxological divine exclusivity

    Key verses: John 17:3, 1 Tim 1:17, 1 Tim 6:15-16, Rev 15:4, Jude 1:25, Rom 16:27

    Relevance: Core to John 17:3 — does 'only' exclude the Son or contrast with false gods? The doxological pattern demonstrates contrastive function.

    G228 — alethinos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md

    Occurrences: 27

    Adjective meaning 'true, genuine, real.' 27 occurrences, 80% in Johannine literature. Semantic categories: (1) genuine vs. copy/shadow (Heb 8:2; 9:24), (2) God as the true God vs. idols (John 17:3; 1 Thess 1:9; 1 John 5:20), (3) divine character: faithful and true (Rev 3:7; 19:11), (4) true judgments/sayings. The alethinos+theos pairing in 1 Thess 1:9 explicitly contrasts with idols ('turned from idols to serve the living and true God'), establishing the semantic pattern for John 17:3.

    Semantic categories: genuine vs. copy, God vs. idols, faithful and true, true judgments

    Key verses: John 17:3, 1 Thess 1:9, 1 John 5:20, Rev 19:11, John 1:9, John 6:32

    Relevance: Core to John 17:3 — 'true' (alethinos) contrasts the genuine God with false gods/idols, not with the Son. 1 John 5:20 uses the same alethinos theos language with Jesus as nearest antecedent.

    G2316 — theos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2316-theos.md

    Occurrences: 1343

    Noun meaning 'God, deity.' The most frequent theological term in the NT (1343 tokens). Primarily refers to the God of Israel/the Father (93.9% as 'God'). Critical Christological uses: John 1:1 (theos en ho logos, qualitative predicate nominative), John 20:28 (ho theos mou, Thomas to Jesus), Heb 1:8 (pros de ton huion... ho theos, Father addresses Son as God), Titus 2:13 and 2 Peter 1:1 (Granville Sharp constructions identifying Jesus as theos), Rom 9:5 (ho on epi panton theos, Christ who is over all God). The LXX uses theos to render elohiym (H430). The NT distribution of theos (primarily for Father) and kyrios (for Jesus) mirrors the OT YHWH/Elohim pairing.

    Semantic categories: supreme deity, God the Father, Son of God / Christ as God, false gods

    Key verses: John 1:1, John 20:28, Heb 1:8, Titus 2:13, 2 Peter 1:1, Rom 9:5, 1 Cor 8:6

    Relevance: Central term — how is theos applied to Jesus? The NT applies it directly to Jesus in at least five passages, demonstrating that 'one God, the Father' (1 Cor 8:6) does not exclude Jesus from deity.

    G2962 — kyrios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2962-kyrios.md

    Occurrences: 748

    Noun meaning 'Lord, master, owner.' 748 tokens in 687 verses. The LXX standard rendering for YHWH (H3068) — over 5,107 co-occurrences. Applied to Jesus as confessional formula (Acts 2:36; Rom 10:9; Phil 2:11; 1 Cor 12:3). Critical for 1 Cor 8:6: Paul's 'one Lord (kyrios) Jesus Christ' takes the kyrios slot from the Shema (Deut 6:4 LXX: kyrios... kyrios heis estin). The NT applies YHWH-kyrios texts to Jesus: Phil 2:10-11 (Isa 45:23), Rom 10:13 (Joel 2:32), Heb 1:10 (Ps 102:25) — demonstrating that the NT's kyrios for Jesus derives from YHWH identity, not merely from Psalm 110:1's adon.

    Semantic categories: YHWH divine name, God the Father as Lord, Jesus Christ as Lord — confessional, Jesus as Lord — authority, earthly master/owner, polite address

    Key verses: 1 Cor 8:6, Phil 2:11, Rom 10:9, Rom 10:13, Acts 2:36, Heb 1:10, Mark 12:36

    Relevance: Central term — does kyrios for Jesus derive from YHWH (deity) or from Ps 110:1's adon (exalted human)? The YHWH-text applications to Jesus (Isa 45:23 → Phil 2:10-11; Joel 2:32 → Rom 10:13; Ps 102:25 → Heb 1:10) demonstrate YHWH-derivation.

    G1520 — heis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1520-heîs.md

    Occurrences: 353

    Primary numeral meaning 'one.' 353 occurrences. Semantic categories: cardinal number, ordinal 'first,' indefinite article, unity/oneness of God (Shema theology), unity between persons, body metaphor. Critical for 1 Cor 8:6: heis theos ho pater (one God, the Father) and heis kyrios Iesous Christos (one Lord, Jesus Christ). Paul distributes the Shema's heis (Deut 6:4 LXX: kyrios heis estin) between theos and kyrios — Jesus is included within the 'one' of the Shema, not excluded by it.

    Semantic categories: numerical/cardinal, ordinal/first, indefinite article, unity of God (Shema), unity between persons, body metaphor, totality

    Key verses: 1 Cor 8:6, Mark 12:29, Mark 12:32, Eph 4:4-6, John 10:30, John 17:21-23

    Relevance: Distributed between theos and kyrios in 1 Cor 8:6, demonstrating Paul's Christological reformulation of the Shema.

    G1223 — dia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1223-diá.md

    Occurrences: 662

    Preposition meaning 'through, by means of, because of.' 662 occurrences. In creation theology: dia + genitive marks the intermediate agent through whom creation occurs (John 1:3; 1 Cor 8:6; Col 1:16; Heb 1:2). Critical finding: Rom 11:36 applies BOTH ek AND dia to the Father (ex autou kai di autou kai eis auton ta panta), collapsing the ek/dia ontological hierarchy argument. Heb 2:10 also uses dia of God. The preposition marks the channel of agency, not inherent inferiority.

    Semantic categories: spatial passage, instrumental agency, prophetic mediation, causal, temporal, soteriological mediation, cosmological agency

    Key verses: 1 Cor 8:6, John 1:3, Col 1:16, Heb 1:2, Rom 11:36, Heb 2:10

    Relevance: The ek/dia distinction in 1 Cor 8:6 does NOT establish ontological hierarchy — Paul uses dia of the Father himself in Rom 11:36.

    G1537 — ek¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1537-ek.md

    Occurrences: 901

    Preposition meaning 'out of, from, by.' 901 occurrences. Marks ultimate source or origin. In 1 Cor 8:6: ek denotes the Father as originating source ('of whom are all things'). In Rom 11:36: ek is applied to God alongside dia and eis, showing all three prepositions describe the Father's relationship to creation. The ek/dia distinction in 1 Cor 8:6 cannot establish an ontological hierarchy because Paul uses all three prepositions interchangeably of the Father.

    Semantic categories: source/origin (ontological), physical separation, material/composition, partitive, causal/instrumental, temporal, resurrection/deliverance

    Key verses: 1 Cor 8:6, Rom 11:36, John 1:13, 1 Cor 11:12

    Relevance: Used of the Father in 1 Cor 8:6 as source/origin. Rom 11:36's parallel usage collapses any ontological hierarchy argument.

    G4990 — soter¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4990-sōtḗr.md

    Occurrences: 24

    Noun meaning 'savior, deliverer.' 24 occurrences. Applied to both God the Father (1 Tim 1:1; 2:3; 4:10; Titus 1:3; 2:10; 3:4; Jude 1:25) and Jesus Christ (Luke 2:11; Acts 5:31; Titus 2:13; 2 Peter 1:1, 1:11, 2:20, 3:2, 3:18). Isaiah 43:11 declares 'beside me there is no saviour' — if YHWH alone is Savior and Jesus is called Savior, this constitutes an implicit deity claim. The Granville Sharp constructions in Titus 2:13 and 2 Peter 1:1 may identify Jesus as both theos and soter under one article.

    Semantic categories: God the Father as Savior, Jesus Christ as Savior, ambiguous/dual referent

    Key verses: Titus 2:13, 2 Peter 1:1, Luke 2:11, Acts 5:31, Isa 43:11, 1 Tim 4:10

    Relevance: Applied to both Father and Son — if YHWH alone saves (Isa 43:11) and Jesus is soter, Jesus must be within YHWH.

    G1391 — doxa¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1391-doxa.md

    Occurrences: 168

    Noun meaning 'glory.' 168 tokens in 151 verses. Semantic categories: divine luminous presence, Christ's inherent glory (pre-existent and revealed), eschatological glory, doxological ascription, human honor. John 17:5 is critical: Jesus claims pre-existent glory (te doxe he eichon para soi — 'the glory which I had with you') — imperfect eichon indicates continuous past possession of shared divine glory before creation. John 17:24 adds: 'thou lovedst me before the foundation of the world.' The Hebrew source is kabod (H3519) — YHWH's manifest presence.

    Semantic categories: divine luminous presence, Christ's inherent glory, eschatological glory, doxological ascription, human honor

    Key verses: John 17:5, John 17:24, John 1:14, Heb 1:3, Rev 21:23

    Relevance: Pre-existent glory in John 17:5 is the same prayer's context for 'the only true God' in 17:3 — Jesus claims shared divine glory before creation.

    H259 — echad¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H259-echad.md

    Occurrences: 980

    Numeral/adjective meaning 'one, united, first.' 980 tokens in 739 verses. Semantic categories: cardinal number, ordinal 'first,' indefinite article, compound/collective unity (Gen 2:24 'one flesh'), absolute/unified 'one' of God (Deut 6:4). Key finding: echad is referent-neutral — it permits composite unity (Gen 2:24; Gen 11:6; Ezek 37:17) without requiring it. Moses avoided yachid (H3173, 'solitary/only') in the Shema — yachid is NEVER applied to YHWH in the OT. The LXX renders echad as heis (G1520), which Paul distributes across Father and Son in 1 Cor 8:6.

    Semantic categories: cardinal number, ordinal first, indefinite article, compound/collective unity, absolute unity of God, emphatic unity of action

    Key verses: Deut 6:4, Gen 2:24, Zech 14:9, Mal 2:10, Ezek 37:17

    Relevance: The Shema's echad permits composite unity within YHWH. The deliberate avoidance of yachid (solitary) is significant.

    H3173 — yachid¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3173-yachiyd.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Adjective meaning 'only, solitary, unique, beloved.' 12 tokens total. Applied to only children (Gen 22:2 'thine only son'), solitary individuals (Judg 11:34), mourning contexts (Zech 12:10). Critical finding: yachid is NEVER applied to YHWH in the Hebrew Bible. If Moses wanted to express that YHWH is solitary/alone, yachid was the available word — yet the Shema uses echad (which permits composite unity) instead of yachid (which expresses solitariness).

    Semantic categories: only child, solitary individual, mourning/loss

    Key verses: Gen 22:2, Judg 11:34, Zech 12:10, Amos 8:10

    Relevance: NEVER applied to YHWH — significant for the composite unity question in the Shema.

    G3056 — logos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3056-logos.md

    Occurrences: 329

    Noun meaning 'word, speech, reason, account.' In John's Prologue: the pre-existent divine Word who was with God and was God (John 1:1), through whom all things were created (1:3), who became flesh (1:14). The Logos theology provides the theological framework for understanding John 17:3 — the same author who writes 'the only true God' also writes 'the Word was God.'

    Semantic categories: divine Logos, spoken word, reason/account

    Key verses: John 1:1, John 1:3, John 1:14, 1 John 1:1, Rev 19:13

    Relevance: The theological framework for John 17:3 — the Word who was God (1:1) is the same person who speaks of 'the only true God' in 17:3.

    G1510 — eimi¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1510-eimi.md

    Occurrences: 155

    Verb meaning 'I am, I exist.' The copulative verb 'to be.' In Christological usage: the imperfect en in John 1:1-2 indicates continuous past existence of the Logos ('the Word was'), contrasting with the aorist egeneto of ginomai in 1:3 ('all things came into being'). The absolute ego eimi in John 8:58 ('Before Abraham was, I am') uses the present tense for existence prior to Abraham — indicating timeless being. See grammar reference: absolute-ego-eimi.md.

    Semantic categories: existence, copulative, absolute ego eimi, timeless being

    Key verses: John 1:1, John 8:58, John 17:5, Exod 3:14

    Relevance: The en/egeneto distinction places Christ on the uncreated side; the absolute ego eimi echoes YHWH's self-identification.

    G1096 — ginomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md

    Occurrences: 702

    Verb meaning 'to become, come into being, happen.' In John 1:3: egeneto (aorist middle indicative) — 'came into being' — used for ALL created things. Contrasts with en (imperfect of eimi) in 1:1-2 used for the Logos. The Logos 'was' (uncreated) while everything else 'became' (created). In John 8:58: genesthai (aorist infinitive) — Abraham 'came into being' — contrasts with ego eimi (present, timeless being) for Jesus.

    Semantic categories: creation/becoming, happening/occurrence, incarnation

    Key verses: John 1:3, John 8:58, John 1:14

    Relevance: The en/egeneto verb distinction is foundational — it places the Logos on the uncreated side of the creator/creation divide.

    G5293 — hupotasso¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5293-hupotasso.md

    Occurrences: 49

    Verb meaning 'to subject, subordinate, submit.' In 1 Cor 15:28: hupotagesetai (future passive indicative) indicates a CHANGE of state — the Son WILL BE subjected, not IS subjected. The hotan...tote construction indicates a future temporal transition. This parallels the Phil 2:5-11 kenosis pattern: equality → voluntary subordination → exaltation. Functional submission does not negate ontological deity, just as Phil 2:7's self-emptying did not eliminate divine nature.

    Semantic categories: military/hierarchical submission, voluntary submission, eschatological subjection

    Key verses: 1 Cor 15:28, Phil 2:7-8, Heb 2:8, Eph 5:24

    Relevance: 1 Cor 15:28's future submission is compatible with deity — it parallels the Phil 2 kenosis pattern.

    G3444 — morphe¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3444-morphe.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Noun meaning 'form, nature, essential character.' In Phil 2:6: en morphe theou hyparchon — 'existing in the form of God.' morphe denotes the essential nature/character, not mere outward appearance (that would be schema, G4976, used in Phil 2:8). Christ existed in God's essential form — sharing the divine nature itself.

    Semantic categories: essential nature/form, divine nature

    Key verses: Phil 2:6, Phil 2:7, Mark 16:12

    Relevance: Phil 2:6's morphe theou asserts Christ's essential divine nature — pre-incarnation equality with God.

    G2470 — isos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2470-ísos.md

    Occurrences: 7

    Adjective meaning 'equal.' In Phil 2:6: isa theo — 'equality with God.' In John 5:18: ison heauton poion to theo — 'making himself equal with God.' The Jews understood Jesus's claims as asserting equality with God. Phil 2:6 confirms this was not a false perception — Christ actually existed in equality with God.

    Semantic categories: equality, equivalent value

    Key verses: Phil 2:6, John 5:18

    Relevance: Confirms the pre-incarnation equality of Christ with God — supports reading John 17:3 as not excluding the Son.

    G2758 — kenoo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2758-kenóō.md

    Occurrences: 7

    Verb meaning 'to empty, make void.' In Phil 2:7: heauton ekenosen — 'he emptied himself.' The reflexive construction (heauton) emphasizes voluntary, self-initiated action. Christ chose to set aside the prerogatives of deity (not the nature of deity) for the incarnation. This framework explains how 'the only true God' language (John 17:3) and subordination language coexist with deity — they describe the incarnational mission, not the eternal nature.

    Semantic categories: self-emptying, voluntary subordination

    Key verses: Phil 2:7

    Relevance: The kenosis framework explains how subordination language is compatible with full deity.

    H430 — elohiym¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H430-elohiym.md

    Occurrences: 2689

    Noun meaning 'God, gods.' Plural form (-im) used with singular verb agreement when referring to the God of Israel. The elohim-plural-trinity study found this plural is referentially under-determined — consistent with plurality within God but not proof of it. The LXX renders elohiym as theos (G2316). The NT distributes theos (primarily for the Father) and kyrios (for Jesus), mirroring the OT YHWH/Elohim pairing.

    Semantic categories: the God of Israel, pagan deities, divine council, human judges

    Key verses: Gen 1:1, Deut 6:4, Ps 45:6-7, Ps 82:1, Ps 82:6

    Relevance: The plural form permits (without requiring) plurality within God; the LXX/NT preserves this through the theos/kyrios distribution.

    H3068 — YHWH¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3068-yhwh.md

    Occurrences: 6534

    The divine name, the Tetragrammaton. The LXX renders YHWH as kyrios (G2962) — over 5,107 co-occurrences. Isaiah's exclusivity texts (43:10-11; 44:6, 24; 45:5-6, 21-23) declare YHWH alone as God, Creator, Savior, first and last. The NT applies each of these titles to Jesus: Creator (John 1:3; 1 Cor 8:6; Col 1:16; Heb 1:2, 10), Savior (Luke 2:11; Titus 2:13), first and last (Rev 1:17; 22:13), YHWH-oath recipient (Phil 2:10-11 citing Isa 45:23). This systematic application of YHWH-exclusive titles to Jesus is the strongest evidence for inclusion of the Son within YHWH's identity.

    Semantic categories: divine name, covenant God, exclusive deity

    Key verses: Deut 6:4, Isa 44:6, Isa 44:24, Isa 45:5-6, Isa 45:21-23, Isa 43:10-11

    Relevance: The Isaiah exclusivity texts create a logical framework: if YHWH alone is X, and Jesus is X, then Jesus must be within YHWH.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    Verb meaning 'to worship, bow down to.' Jesus teaches that worship belongs to God alone (Matt 4:10). Yet Jesus accepts worship: Matt 14:33, John 9:38, Heb 1:6, Rev 5:12-14. If 'the only true God' excludes Jesus from deity, then worship of Jesus would be idolatry — yet the NT consistently presents worship of Jesus as proper and expected.

    Semantic categories: divine worship, reverent homage

    Key verses: Matt 4:10, Matt 14:33, John 9:38, Heb 1:6, Rev 5:12-14

    Relevance: Worship of Jesus is incompatible with exclusion from deity — God alone is worshipped, and Jesus receives worship.

    G225 — aletheia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G225-aletheia.md

    Occurrences: 110

    Noun meaning 'truth.' 110 NT occurrences. Abstract noun form of the aleth- word family. Related to alethinos (G228) — aletheia is the abstract concept, alethinos is the adjective describing what is genuine/real. Jesus claims to be 'the truth' (John 14:6), which is consistent with the 'true God' language.

    Semantic categories: truth as reality, truth as faithfulness

    Key verses: John 14:6, John 17:17, John 8:32

    Relevance: Comparison word — helps distinguish aletheia (abstract truth) from alethinos (genuine/real) in the word family.

    G3962 — pater¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3962-pater.md

    Occurrences: 419

    Noun meaning 'father.' Used for God the Father in 1 Cor 8:6 ('one God, the Father') and throughout John 17 (addressed as 'Father' in vv.1, 5, 11, 21, 24, 25). The designation of the Father as 'one God' in 1 Cor 8:6 does not exclude the Son from deity — it identifies the Father within the Shema framework, just as 'one Lord' identifies the Son within the same framework.

    Semantic categories: God the Father, human father, ancestor

    Key verses: 1 Cor 8:6, John 17:1, John 17:5, Eph 4:6

    Relevance: 'One God, the Father' identifies the Father's role within the Shema, not the Son's exclusion from deity.

    G649 — apostello¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G649-apostellō.md

    Occurrences: 144

    Verb meaning 'to send forth, dispatch.' Used in John 17:3: hon apesteilas Iesoun Christon ('Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent'). The sending language describes mission, not ontological inferiority. In John 5:23, the Son who is 'sent' (pempō) is also the one who receives equal honor with the Father.

    Semantic categories: divine commission, missionary sending

    Key verses: John 17:3, John 3:17, John 5:36, Gal 4:4

    Relevance: The 'sent' language in John 17:3 describes mission, not inferiority — the same sent Son receives equal honor (John 5:23).

    G5481 — charakter¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5481-charaktēr.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Noun meaning 'exact impress, representation.' In Heb 1:3: charakter tes hupostaseos autou — 'the exact representation of his being/substance.' The Son is not merely similar to God but is the precise imprint of God's essential nature.

    Semantic categories: exact representation

    Key verses: Heb 1:3

    Relevance: Supports reading the Son as sharing the divine nature, not excluded from it.

    G4416 — prototokos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4416-prototokos.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Noun meaning 'firstborn.' Title of preeminence, not birth order — as established in trin-01 study. Psalm 89:27 applies it to David (youngest son). Col 1:15's 'firstborn of all creation' indicates supremacy over creation, not membership in it.

    Semantic categories: preeminence title, inheritance rights

    Key verses: Col 1:15, Heb 1:6, Rev 1:5

    Relevance: Title of preeminence over creation, supporting Christ's deity rather than createdness.

    G746 — arche¶

    Noun meaning 'beginning, ruler, origin.' In Rev 3:14: 'the beginning (arche) of the creation of God' — functions as a divine title of origination within Revelation's Alpha-Omega framework, not as evidence of createdness. See trin-01 study.

    Semantic categories: beginning/origin, ruler/authority, divine origination title

    Key verses: Rev 3:14, John 1:1, Col 1:18

    Relevance: Divine origination title, consistent with deity.

    G725 — harpagmos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G725-harpagmos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Noun meaning 'something grasped/exploited.' Only in Phil 2:6: ouch harpagmon hegesato to einai isa theo — 'did not consider equality with God something to be grasped/exploited.' Whether res rapta (already possessed) or res rapienda (something to be seized), the text presupposes Christ's actual equality with God — he either had it and did not cling to it, or could have taken it but did not need to.

    Semantic categories: exploitation, grasping

    Key verses: Phil 2:6

    Relevance: Presupposes Christ's equality with God in Phil 2:6.

    G3187 — meizon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3187-meizōn.md

    Occurrences: 30

    Comparative adjective meaning 'greater.' As established in trin-02: meizon indicates positional/relational rank, not ontological superiority (which would be kreitton, G2909). John 14:28's 'the Father is greater than I' uses meizon — describing incarnational position, not eternal nature.

    Semantic categories: positional rank, comparative size

    Key verses: John 14:28, John 5:20, Matt 12:6

    Relevance: The meizon/kreitton distinction supports the incarnational reading of subordination language.

    G2909 — kreitton¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2909-kreitton.md

    Occurrences: 17

    Comparative adjective meaning 'better, superior.' Indicates qualitative/ontological superiority. Used of Christ's superiority to angels (Heb 1:4), old covenant (Heb 7:22; 8:6), levitical priests (Heb 7:7). NOT used of Father's superiority to Son — John 14:28 uses meizon (positional) instead.

    Semantic categories: qualitative superiority, ontological better-ness

    Key verses: Heb 1:4, Heb 7:22, Heb 8:6

    Relevance: The Father is never called kreitton (ontologically superior) to the Son — only meizon (positionally greater).

    G3439 — monogenes¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3439-monogenḗs.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Adjective meaning 'unique, one-of-a-kind.' In John 1:14, 18; 3:16, 18; 1 John 4:9. The textual variant in John 1:18 reads monogenes theos ('the unique God') in the earliest manuscripts, explicitly calling Jesus 'God.'

    Semantic categories: unique/one-of-a-kind, only child

    Key verses: John 1:14, John 1:18, John 3:16

    Relevance: The monogenes theos variant in John 1:18 is another Johannine text calling Jesus God.

    G5207 — huios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5207-huios.md

    Occurrences: 382

    Noun meaning 'son.' 'Son of God' in John's Gospel carries deity implications — John 5:18 states the Jews understood 'making God his Father' as 'making himself equal with God.' Sonship in John is not merely messianic but implies shared divine nature.

    Semantic categories: divine sonship, human offspring, messianic title

    Key verses: John 5:18, John 1:14, Heb 1:2, Mark 1:1

    Relevance: The Son who is 'sent' in John 17:3 is the same Son who is equal with God in John 5:18.

    G2222 — zoe¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2222-zoe.md

    Occurrences: 134

    Noun meaning 'life.' In John 17:3: haute de estin he aionios zoe — 'this is eternal life.' Eternal life is defined as knowing the Father AND Jesus Christ. The joint-object construction makes both the Father and the Son essential to eternal life — excluding the Son from deity would make him a created mediator of knowledge that constitutes eternal life, which is a divine prerogative.

    Semantic categories: eternal life, spiritual life, biological life

    Key verses: John 17:3, John 1:4, John 14:6, 1 John 5:20

    Relevance: Eternal life requires knowing BOTH the Father and Jesus — making the Son co-essential with the Father for salvation.

    G1097 — ginosko¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1097-ginosko.md

    Occurrences: 246

    Verb meaning 'to know (experientially).' In John 17:3: hina ginoskosi se — 'that they might know you.' Present active subjunctive — ongoing, experiential knowledge. The verb takes two accusative objects: se ton monon alethinon theon (you, the only true God) kai hon apesteilas Iesoun Christon (and Jesus Christ whom you sent). Both are equal objects of saving knowledge.

    Semantic categories: experiential knowledge, relational knowing

    Key verses: John 17:3, John 8:32, Phil 3:10

    Relevance: The dual accusative object structure in John 17:3 pairs the Father and Jesus as joint objects of life-giving knowledge.

    G166 — aionios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G166-aionios.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Adjective meaning 'eternal, everlasting.' Modifies zoe (life) in John 17:3 — 'eternal life.' Also used in 1 John 5:20: zoe aionios ('eternal life'), where 'this is the true God and eternal life' may refer to Jesus as both true God and source of eternal life.

    Semantic categories: eternal duration, age-related

    Key verses: John 17:3, 1 John 5:20, Matt 25:46, Rom 6:23

    Relevance: Connects John 17:3 and 1 John 5:20 — the 'eternal life' that is knowing God is the same 'eternal life' that IS Jesus.

    G989 — blasphemos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G989-blasphemos.md

    Occurrences: 5

    Adjective/noun meaning 'blasphemous, blasphemer.' In John 10:33, the Jews charge Jesus with blasphemy for 'making thyself God.' The blasphemy charge confirms the contemporaneous understanding that Jesus claimed deity — and he did not correct their understanding.

    Semantic categories: blasphemy charge, false accusation

    Key verses: John 10:33, Mark 14:64, Matt 26:65

    Relevance: The blasphemy charge confirms Jesus's deity claims were understood as such by contemporaries.

    G1849 — exousia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1849-exousia.md

    Occurrences: 103

    Noun meaning 'authority, power.' In Matt 28:18: pasa exousia edothe moi en ourano kai epi ges — 'all authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me.' Post-resurrection, Jesus claims universal authority — consistent with deity, inconsistent with exclusion from it.

    Semantic categories: divine authority, delegated authority

    Key verses: Matt 28:18, John 17:2, John 5:27

    Relevance: All authority given to Jesus — consistent with deity, not with exclusion from deity.

    G5547 — christos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5547-christos.md

    Occurrences: 569

    Noun meaning 'Christ, anointed one.' The messianic title. In John 17:3: 'Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent.' In 1 Cor 8:6: 'one Lord Jesus Christ.' The Christ is not merely a human messiah but the divine Lord who shares in creation.

    Semantic categories: messianic title, anointed one

    Key verses: John 17:3, 1 Cor 8:6, Phil 2:11

    Relevance: The title connects the human messianic role with the divine Lord of 1 Cor 8:6.

    G2258 — en (imperfect of eimi)¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2258-en.md

    Occurrences: 426

    Imperfect active indicative of eimi (G1510). In John 1:1: en arche en ho logos — 'in the beginning was the Word.' The imperfect tense indicates continuous past existence — the Logos already existed when 'the beginning' began. Contrasts with egeneto (aorist of ginomai) in 1:3 for created things.

    Semantic categories: continuous past existence

    Key verses: John 1:1, John 1:2

    Relevance: The imperfect en establishes the Logos's uncreated, continuous pre-existence.
