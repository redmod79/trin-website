    Reference Gathering: "The Only True God" — Do John 17:3 and 1 Corinthians 8:6 Exclude Jesus from Deity?¶

    Question¶

    When Jesus says "the only true God" (John 17:3) and Paul says "one God, the Father" (1 Corinthians 8:6), do these passages exclude Jesus from deity? Examine the full chapter context of John 17 and the Shema background of 1 Corinthians 8:6.

    Study Plan Context¶

    This is Study 3 (trin-03) of a 10-study Trinity series. No formal PLAN.md file was found for this series. The prior two studies in the series are: - trin-01-firstborn-created-being — Does "firstborn of all creation" (Col 1:15) or "beginning of the creation of God" (Rev 3:14) make Jesus a created being? - trin-02-subordination-passages — Do John 14:28, John 5:19, Mark 13:32, and 1 Corinthians 15:28 describe Jesus' eternal nature or his voluntary mission?

    Both prior studies establish extensive findings that bear directly on this study's question.

    Prior Studies¶

    From Series Context (trin-01, trin-02)¶

    trin-01-firstborn-created-being: - Question: Does "firstborn of all creation" (prototokos pases ktiseos, Col 1:15) teach that Jesus was the first created being? - Key finding 1: Bekor/prototokos (H1060/G4416) functions as a title of preeminence divorced from birth order — Psalm 89:27 (David = youngest son, made "firstborn" = highest king), Exodus 4:22 (Israel = not first nation), Jeremiah 31:9 (Ephraim = second-born, called firstborn) - Key finding 2: The causal hoti in Col 1:16 explains the firstborn title by creative agency — "He is firstborn BECAUSE by him were all things created" - Key finding 3: The creation paradox — John 1:3, Col 1:16, Heb 1:2, 1 Cor 8:6 all assign ALL creation to Christ. If Christ is created but created ALL things, he created himself (logical impossibility) - Key finding 4: Isaiah 44:24 declares YHWH created alone (lebaddi) — if Christ is creation's agent, he must be within YHWH's identity - Key finding 5: The en/egeneto verb distinction in John 1:1-3 places Christ on the uncreated side — the Word "was" (en, continuous existence) while creation "came into being" (egeneto) - Key finding 6: Arche (G746) in Rev 3:14 functions within Revelation's Alpha-and-Omega formula as a divine title of eternal origination, not createdness (Rev 1:8, 21:6, 22:13) - Key finding 7: 1 Corinthians 8:6 cited as evidence — "one Lord Jesus Christ, by whom are all things" assigns creation agency to Christ - Vocabulary chain: prototokos (G4416) / bekor (H1060) / arche (G746) / proteuo (G4409) / ktizo (G2936) / monogenes (G3439) / charakter (G5481)

    trin-02-subordination-passages: - Question: Do John 14:28, John 5:19, Mark 13:32, and 1 Corinthians 15:28 describe Jesus' eternal nature or his voluntary mission? - Key finding 1: Meizon (G3187, positional rank) vs. kreitton (G2909, qualitative superiority) — John 14:28 uses meizon (positional), not kreitton (ontological). The distinction is the strongest lexical argument for the incarnational reading. - Key finding 2: John 5:19-30 has a concessive-climactic structure — dependence (v.19) frames divine prerogatives (life-giving v.21, all judgment v.22, equal honor v.23, self-existent life v.26) - Key finding 3: 1 Cor 15:28's hotan...tote with future passive hupotagēsetai indicates a CHANGE of state (future transition), not ongoing subordination - Key finding 4: Phil 2:5-11 kenosis hymn provides the controlling framework — pre-incarnation equality (morphe theou, isa theo) → voluntary self-emptying → incarnational subordination → hyper-exaltation - Key finding 5: NO pre-incarnation text describes the Son as subordinate — John 1:1, 17:5, 17:24, Phil 2:6, John 8:58 all describe equality, shared glory, timeless being - Key finding 6: Post-resurrection portrait removes incarnational limitations — Matt 28:18 (all authority), Rev 1:8 (Alpha and Omega/Almighty), Rev 22:1,3 (shared throne), Rev 5:12-13 (identical worship) - Key finding 7: Monos (G3441) in Mark 13:32/Matt 24:36 describes temporary incarnational restriction, not permanent knowledge hierarchy - Key finding 8: Reflexive constructions (heauton ekenosen Phil 2:7, ap emautou John 10:18) consistently present subordination as self-initiated and voluntary - Vocabulary chain: meizon (G3187) / kreitton (G2909) / hupotasso (G5293) / kenoo (G2758) / morphe (G3444) / isos (G2470) / harpagmos (G725) / thelema (G2307) / eimi (G1510) / ginomai (G1096) / doxa (G1391) / monos (G3441) - Open question: Precise nature of eternal Father-Son relationship apart from incarnational mission (John 5:26's "given to the Son to have life in himself" raises but does not resolve this)

    From Semantic Search (additional)¶

    jesus-never-said-i-am-god: (score: 0.470) - Question: Why is there no sentence on Jesus' lips of the form "I am God"? - Relevance: Directly addresses how Jesus claimed deity through an "indirect-with-load matrix" rather than bare predication - Key finding 1: Five disclosure vectors carry the deity claim — absolute ego eimi sayings (echoing LXX YHWH-formulas), divine-prerogative actions, acceptance of worship, reception of YHWH-titles, and post-resurrection open YHWH-formula speech - Key finding 2: John 17:3 is addressed as a difficult passage — "The Father is 'the only true God' as opposed to false gods, not as opposed to the Son who shares his identity. The very next phrase 'and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent' pairs the Son with the Father as the joint-object of life-giving knowledge" - Key finding 3: The Sanhedrin's blasphemy verdict (Mar 14:64) is the contemporaneous proof that Jesus' indirect deity claim was recognized - Key finding 4: John 20:28 (Thomas's "my Lord and my God") is "the crowning christological affirmation of the Fourth Gospel" — Jesus accepts without rebuke - Key finding 5: 1 John 5:20 ("This is the true God, and eternal life") uses the same "true God" language as John 17:3, with Jesus Christ as the nearest antecedent - Vocabulary chain: G2316 theos / G2962 kyrios / G3686 onoma / G4352 proskyneo / G989 blasphemia / G1510 eimi / G1473 ego

    greatest-commandment-shema: (score: 0.462) - Question: What did Jesus say about the greatest commandment in Mark 12? How does it relate to Deuteronomy 6? - Relevance: The Shema (Deut 6:4-5) is the direct OT background for 1 Corinthians 8:6 - Key finding 1: Jesus AFFIRMED the Shema in Mark 12:29 — "Hear, O Israel; The Lord our God is one Lord" - Key finding 2: Jesus expanded the Hebrew to Greek, adding dianoia ("mind") to make explicit what Hebrew levav ("heart") includes - Key finding 3: The Shema contains two elements — monotheistic declaration (v.4) and total-love command (v.5) - Key finding 4: The scribe's affirmation "there is one God; and there is none other but he" (Mark 12:32) — Jesus affirms this scribe is "not far from the kingdom of God"

    elohim-plural-trinity: (score: 0.728) - Question: Does the plural form of Elohim grammatically refer to the Trinity? - Relevance: Establishes the grammatical framework for understanding Hebrew monotheism and plurality-within-unity - Key finding 1: Elohim's plural morphology is "consistent with" Trinity but does not by itself prove it — the -im form is referentially under-determined - Key finding 2: The Shema's echad (H259) is referent-neutral — it permits composite unity (Gen 2:24 "one flesh") without requiring it, but Moses avoided yachid (H3173, "solitary/only") which is NEVER applied to YHWH - Key finding 3: Stronger OT evidence for divine plurality comes from distinct-agent texts — Angel of YHWH, Spirit of YHWH, "two YHWHs" passages (Gen 19:24), Psalm 45:6-7 (elohim-to-elohim address), Psalm 110:1 - Key finding 4: 1 Corinthians 8:6 is cited as evidence that "Paul expands the Shema: the 'one' is distributed across Father and Son" - Key finding 5: Isaiah 48:16 names three distinct agents in one clause: "Adonai YHWH, and his Spirit, hath sent me"

    cmd-02-first-commandment-no-other-gods: (score: 0.430) - Question: What does "no other gods before me" mean? Trace through entire Bible. - Relevance: Establishes the monotheistic framework within which 1 Cor 8:6 operates - Key finding 1: 1 Cor 8:4,6 directly cited — "there is none other God but one... to us there is but one God, the Father" - Key finding 2: Isaiah's exclusive-deity passages (43:10; 44:6; 45:5) form the OT background for NT monotheistic confessions - Key finding 3: Other "gods" are lifeless creations, worthless nothings, and demons (Deu 32:17; 1 Cor 10:20) — the "only true God" contrasts with these, not with the Son

    trin-02-subordination-passages: (score: 0.462 on Shema query) - Already covered above in series context

    psalm-82-gods-identity: (score: 0.431) - Question: What is the identity of the gods in Psalm 82? - Relevance: Jesus cites Psalm 82 in John 10:34-36 during a deity-claim debate, providing context for Johannine Christology - Key finding: The "gods" are human judges; Jesus uses this as an a fortiori argument — if human judges can receive the elohim title, his "Son of God" claim is even more justified (John 10:36)

    yhwh-elohim-usage-pattern: (score: 0.595) - Question: Why does the Bible use YHWH and Elohim? - Relevance: The NT preserves the YHWH/Elohim structure via kyrios/theos — "applying kurios-YHWH to Jesus and theos-Elohim to the Father" - Key finding: This distribution is theologically significant for 1 Cor 8:6, where "one God (theos), the Father" and "one Lord (kyrios), Jesus Christ" may map onto the Shema's YHWH-Elohim pairing

    Claims to verify biblically: 1. EGW reads John 17:3 as establishing the personality (distinct personhood) of both Father and Son — not as excluding the Son from deity. She affirms the Father as "the only true God" while simultaneously affirming Christ as "equal with God" and "one with the eternal Father... one in nature, in character, and in purpose." The scoping agent should verify whether John 17's full chapter context (esp. v.5 pre-existent glory, v.10 mutual ownership, v.21-22 divine unity) supports this "distinct persons sharing divine nature" reading. 2. The phrase "He declared that He had no existence separate from the Father" appears to reference John 10:30 or John 14:10 — the claim of unity-without-separation needs verification against the Greek of these texts. 3. "One with the Father before the foundation of the world" references John 17:5, 24 — these pre-creation relationship claims need verification as evidence that "only true God" does not exclude the Son.

    Notable external historical source found in EGW corpus: - Matthew Henry (HENRY 51624): "God is here called the only true God, to distinguish him from the false gods of the heathen, which were counterfeiters and pretenders, not from the person of the Son, of whom it is expressly said, that he is the true God and eternal life (1 John 5:20) and who in this text is proposed as the object of the same religious regard with the Father." - Uriah Smith (DAR 401.1 / DAR1909 430.1): Notably represents a DIFFERENT position — Smith argued Christ "does not possess a co-eternity of past existence with the Father" and that his existence "had a beginning," though the 1909 edition softened this to "he does not possess a co-eternity of past existence with the Father." This is a lead for understanding the historical development of SDA Christology.

    Web Research Findings¶

    The Phase 0 web research agent found 11 scholarly sources across 3 major interpretive positions. Key findings organized by category:

    Christological Monotheism (dominant scholarly position — Bauckham, Wright, Fee, Hurtado, Gathercole, Witherington): - Bauckham argues Paul produces a "Christian version of the Shema" in 1 Cor 8:6, identifying Jesus as "the Lord whom the Shema affirms to be one" — not adding a second figure but expanding the subject - Wright: Paul "placed Jesus within an explicit statement of the doctrine that Israel's God is the one and only God" (Climax of the Covenant, p.129) - Fee: Paul "divided the Shema into two parts, with theos now referring to the Father, and kyrios referring to Jesus Christ" (First Corinthians, p.90) - The ek/dia preposition distinction in 1 Cor 8:6 is debated: does ek (Father as source) vs. dia (Christ as agent) indicate ontological hierarchy or complementary roles?

    Counter-argument (Perriman): - Paul does NOT quote the Shema verbatim in 8:4 — already drops kyrios. Why quote incompletely if intending deliberate Shema division? - Better OT background may be Malachi 2:10-12 rather than Deut 6:4 - Kyrios applied to Jesus derives from Psalm 110:1 enthronement (human king exalted), not from YHWH's unique identity - 1 Cor 15:24-28 (Son hands kingdom back to Father) seen as incompatible with full inclusion in deity

    John 17:3 analysis (Cross Theology, Robertson, Reymond): - Monos alethinos theos ("only true God") contrasts Father with false/pagan gods, not with the Son — parallels 1 Thess 1:9 - John 17:5: pre-existent glory "with thee before the world was" — imperfect eichon (continuous past possession) + para + dative (personal proximity) - John 17:10: "All mine are thine, and thine are mine" — mutual ownership claim only deity could make - John 20:28: Thomas's "my Lord and my God" (ho kyrios mou kai ho theos mou) — same Gospel, Jesus accepts - 1 John 5:20: "This is the true God and eternal life" — same author uses same "true God" language with Jesus as nearest antecedent

    Additional scholarly leads: - Granville Sharp rule applied to Titus 2:13 and 2 Peter 1:1 — single-article construction may identify Jesus as theos - Romans 9:5 punctuation debate — Metzger's arguments for Christ = "God blessed forever" - Isaiah 45:23 → Philippians 2:10-11 parallel — exclusive YHWH-oath applied to Jesus - John 1:1 grammar — Harner's qualitative reading of anarthrous theos

    Research directives from web research (15 items): 1. Verify Shema mapping: Deut 6:4 LXX word-by-word vs. 1 Cor 8:6 Greek 2. Check ek/dia preposition significance across Pauline usage 3. Investigate 1 Cor 15:24-28 relation to 8:6 4. Verify monos (G3441) usage with theos — contrastive vs. exclusive? 5. Examine John 17 full chapter context (vv.5, 10, 21-22, 24) 6. Check Granville Sharp construction in Titus 2:13 and 2 Peter 1:1 7. Verify Rom 9:5 grammar 8. Examine John 1:1 grammar 9. Verify John 20:28 Greek 10. Check Isaiah 44:6, 45:5-6, 45:21-22 NT applications 11. Examine Philippians 2:6-11 12. Investigate alethinos (G228) in John 17:3 13. Check 1 John 5:20 pronoun reference 14. Verify Perriman's Malachi 2:10 claim 15. Examine John 5:17-23
