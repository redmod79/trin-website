    Question¶

    When Jesus says 'the only true God' (John 17:3) and Paul says 'one God, the Father' (1 Corinthians 8:6), do these passages exclude Jesus from deity? Examine the full chapter context of John 17 and the Shema background of 1 Corinthians 8:6.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Seven prior studies bear directly on this question. trin-01 established that ALL creation is assigned to Christ (John 1:3, Col 1:16, Heb 1:2, 1 Cor 8:6) and that Isaiah 44:24 declares YHWH created alone — if Christ is creation's agent, he must be within YHWH's identity. trin-02 established that monos (G3441) in Mark 13:32 describes temporary incarnational limitation and that NO pre-incarnation text describes the Son as subordinate. The jesus-never-said-i-am-god study already treated John 17:3 as a difficult passage and concluded the 'only true God' contrasts with false gods, not with the Son. The elohim-plural-trinity study identified 1 Cor 8:6 as Paul 'expanding the Shema' and found that echad (H259) permits composite unity without requiring it while yachid (H3173) is NEVER applied to YHWH. The greatest-commandment-shema study confirmed Jesus affirmed the Shema in Mark 12:29. The yhwh-elohim-usage-pattern study found the NT preserves the YHWH/Elohim structure via kyrios/theos. Web research identified the Bauckham/Wright/Fee 'Christological Shema' thesis as the dominant scholarly paradigm, and Perriman's counter-argument (Psalm 110:1 background, ek/dia hierarchy, 1 Cor 15:24-28 subordination) as the strongest challenge.

    Primary Verses (33 — full analysis required)¶

    Primary crux texts: The two passages under direct examination - John 17:1-5 — Primary crux passage — 'the only true God' (v.3) plus pre-existent glory claim (v.5) - John 17:3 — Isolated verse for detailed parsing — monos alethinos theos + kai construction - 1 Corinthians 8:4-6 — Primary crux passage — 'one God, the Father... one Lord, Jesus Christ' with creation language

    John 17 full context: The entire High Priestly Prayer - John 17:10 — Mutual ownership: 'All mine are thine, and thine are mine' — a claim only deity could make - John 17:21-23 — Divine unity: 'that they may be one, as we are one' — the nature of Father-Son unity - John 17:24 — 'Thou lovedst me before the foundation of the world' — pre-creation relationship

    Johannine deity passages: John's Gospel testimony to Christ's deity within the same literary work - John 1:1-3 — Johannine prologue — theos without article (qualitative predicate), creation agency - John 5:17-23 — Equal honor: 'all men should honour the Son, even as they honour the Father' (v.23) - John 8:58 — 'Before Abraham was, I am' — absolute ego eimi, pre-existence claim triggering stoning - John 10:30-33 — 'I and my Father are one' — the Jews understood this as a deity claim (v.33) - John 20:28 — Thomas: 'My Lord and my God' — same Gospel, Jesus accepts deity title

    OT YHWH exclusivity texts: Isaiah's monotheism declarations and their NT applications - Isaiah 44:6 — 'I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God' — compare Rev 1:17; 22:13 - Isaiah 44:24 — YHWH created alone (lebaddi) — if Christ is creation agent in 1 Cor 8:6, he must be within YHWH - Isaiah 45:5-6 — 'I am the LORD, and there is none else' — YHWH exclusivity text - Isaiah 45:21-23 — 'There is no God else beside me' plus 'every knee shall bow' — applied to Jesus in Phil 2:10-11

    Philippians 2 kenosis and eschatological submission: Self-emptying framework and 1 Cor 15 submission counter-argument - Philippians 2:5-11 — Kenosis hymn — morphe theou, isa theo, plus Isa 45:23 application to Jesus - 1 Corinthians 15:24-28 — Counter-argument: Son hands kingdom to Father — tests whether full deity is compatible with eschatological submission

    Focus Areas¶

    1. John 17:3 — monos alethinos theos: exclusive or contrastive?: READ the library entries for G3441 (monos) at $WORD_STUDIES/G3441-mónos.md, G228 (alethinos) at $WORD_STUDIES/G228-alethinos.md, and G2316 (theos) at $WORD_STUDIES/G2316-theos.md. Run greek_parser.py --verse 'JHN 17:3' to get the full morphological parse. The key question is whether monos functions as an exclusive adjective ('only, to the exclusion of all others including the Son') or a contrastive adjective ('the genuine God as opposed to false/pagan gods'). Check 1 Thess 1:9 where 'true God' (theos alethinos) contrasts with idols — the same alethinos+theos pairing. Then examine EVERY NT occurrence where monos modifies or accompanies theos: John 17:3, 1 Tim 1:17, 1 Tim 6:15-16, Jude 1:25, Rom 16:27. Does monos in these doxologies exclude the Son, or do they praise the one true God as opposed to false gods? The kai ('and') connecting 'the only true God' with 'Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent' grammatically pairs them as the joint object of life-giving knowledge — verify this by parsing the accusative structure. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on John 17:3. Verses: John 17:3, 1 Thessalonians 1:9, 1 Timothy 1:17, 1 Timothy 6:15-16, Jude 1:25, Romans 16:27 Strong's: G3441, G228, G2316

    2. John 17 full chapter context — what the same prayer reveals about the speaker: Retrieve the FULL TEXT of John 17:1-26 from kjv.txt. Run greek_parser.py --verse on John 17:5, 17:10, 17:21, 17:22, 17:24. In v.5, the phrase te doxe he eichon pro tou ton kosmon einai para soi uses imperfect eichon (continuous past possession) plus para + dative (personal proximity) — this indicates actual, conscious, personal pre-existence, not merely a plan in God's mind. In v.10, 'All mine are thine, and thine are mine' (panta ta ema sa estin kai ta sa ema) is a mutual ownership claim — verify the possessive pronoun structure. In v.21-22, the unity formula 'that they may be one, as we are one' establishes Father-Son unity as the MODEL for believer unity — what kind of unity is this? In v.24, 'thou lovedst me before the foundation of the world' (pro kataboles kosmou) asserts pre-creation personal relationship. Read the library entry for G1391 (doxa) at $WORD_STUDIES/G1391-doxa.md. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on John 17:5. Verses: John 17:1-5, John 17:10, John 17:21-23, John 17:24, John 17:6-8, John 17:11 Strong's: G1391, G2258, G4253, G2889

    3. 1 Corinthians 8:6 and the Shema — Paul's Christological reformulation of Deuteronomy 6:4: Run greek_parser.py --verse '1CO 8:6' and also parse '1CO 8:4' and '1CO 8:5' for full context. Retrieve Deut 6:4 from kjv.txt and run hebrew_parser.py --verse 'Deu 6:4' to get the Hebrew. The Shema reads: kyrios ho theos hemon kyrios heis estin (LXX). Paul's confession reads: heis theos ho pater... kai heis kyrios Iesous Christos. The mapping is: Shema's 'heis' appears twice in Paul, distributed between theos (Father) and kyrios (Christ). Fee says Paul 'divided the Shema into two parts.' The ek/dia preposition distinction (ex hou ta panta kai hemeis eis auton / di hou ta panta kai hemeis di autou) is debated: does ek (Father as ultimate source) vs. dia (Christ as agent) indicate ontological hierarchy or complementary roles? Check whether dia is ALSO used of God's creative activity elsewhere in Paul (e.g., Rom 11:36 'of him, and through him, and to him'). Read the library entries for G2962 (kyrios) at $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md and H259 (echad) at $WORD_STUDIES/H259-echad.md. Test Perriman's claim that Malachi 2:10 ('Have we not all one father? hath not one God created us?') is a better background — retrieve Malachi 2:10-12 and compare. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on 1 Cor 8:6. Verses: 1 Corinthians 8:4-6, Deuteronomy 6:4, Malachi 2:10, Romans 11:36, Mark 12:29-32 Strong's: G2316, G2962, G1520, G1537, G1223, H259, H3068, H430

    4. The ek/dia preposition distinction — source vs. agent or complementary divine roles?: Run greek_parser.py --verse 'ROM 11:36' to verify the text: 'For of him (ex autou), and through him (di autou), and to him (eis auton) are all things.' If Paul uses BOTH ek AND dia of God the Father in the same verse, the preposition distinction in 1 Cor 8:6 cannot establish an ontological hierarchy. Also check Heb 2:10 ('for whom [di hon] are all things, and by whom [di hou] are all things') — dia applied to God the Father. Check Col 1:16 where 'by him' (en auto), 'through him' (di autou), and 'for him' (eis auton) all apply to Christ — the same prepositions used of the Father in Rom 11:36. READ the library entry for G1096 (ginomai) at $WORD_STUDIES/G1096-ginomai.md for creation terminology. Run a search_strongs.py --verses query on G1223 (dia) to find key creation-agency verses. Verses: 1 Corinthians 8:6, Romans 11:36, Hebrews 2:10, Colossians 1:16, John 1:3 Strong's: G1537, G1223, G1519

    5. The echad (H259) composite unity question and yachid (H3173) avoidance: READ the library entries for H259 (echad) at $WORD_STUDIES/H259-echad.md and H3173 (yachid) at $WORD_STUDIES/H3173-yachiyd.md. The elohim-plural-trinity study already found that echad is 'referent-neutral' — it permits composite unity (Gen 2:24 'one flesh') without requiring it, but Moses avoided yachid (H3173, 'solitary/only') which is NEVER applied to YHWH. Run hebrew_parser.py --verse 'Deu 6:4' to parse the Shema. Run hebrew_parser.py --verse 'Gen 2:24' to verify echad in the 'one flesh' context. Search for yachid occurrences using search_strongs.py --verses H3173 to confirm it is never used of God. Also run hebrew_parser.py --verse 'Zec 14:9' — 'In that day shall there be one LORD, and his name one' — where echad describes YHWH in an eschatological context. Verses: Deuteronomy 6:4, Genesis 2:24, Zechariah 14:9 Strong's: H259, H3173

    6. John 1:1 — the anarthrous theos as qualitative predicate nominative: Run greek_parser.py --verse 'JHN 1:1' to get full morphological parsing. The structure is: (a) En arche en ho logos — 'In the beginning was the Word'; (b) kai ho logos en pros ton theon — 'and the Word was with God' (articular theon = the Father); (c) kai theos en ho logos — 'and the Word was God' (anarthrous theos in predicate position). Harner's statistical analysis (JBL 92, 1973) showed 80% of anarthrous pre-verbal predicate nominatives are QUALITATIVE — meaning 'the Word had the nature/essence of God.' Wallace confirms: 'an anarthrous pre-verbal PN is normally qualitative, sometimes definite, and only rarely indefinite.' Read the library entry for G3056 (logos) at $WORD_STUDIES/G3056-logos.md and the grammar reference for absolute ego eimi at $GRAMMAR_REF/greek/absolute-ego-eimi.md. Also parse John 1:3 ('All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made') — the creation language that connects to 1 Cor 8:6.     7. John 20:28 — Thomas's confession as the climax of Johannine Christology: Run greek_parser.py --verse 'JHN 20:28' to get the morphological parse. The phrase ho kyrios mou kai ho theos mou uses the nominative case for both kyrios and theos (rather than vocative), but 'said to Him' (eipen auto) explicitly indicates Thomas addressed Jesus directly. Both nouns have the article (ho), making them definite — Thomas is not saying 'a god' but 'THE God of me.' Jesus's response in v.29 ('Because thou hast seen me, thou hast believed: blessed are they that have not seen, and yet have believed') affirms rather than corrects the confession. Read the library entry for G2962 (kyrios) at $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md. This is what Harris calls 'the crowning christological affirmation of the Fourth Gospel.' Also note that 1 John 5:20 uses the same 'true God' language — run greek_parser.py --verse '1JN 5:20' to verify whether houtos refers to Jesus Christ (nearest antecedent) or the Father. Verses: John 20:28-29, 1 John 5:20 Strong's: G2316, G2962, G228

    8. Titus 2:13, 2 Peter 1:1, and the Granville Sharp rule — calling Jesus theos: Run greek_parser.py --verse 'TIT 2:13' to parse tou megalou theou kai soteros hemon Iesou Christou. Sharp's rule (1798): when two singular, personal nouns appear under one article linked by kai, they designate the same individual. The article tou appears only once before theou, and per Sharp's rule both theou and soteros refer to the same person: Jesus Christ. Run greek_parser.py --verse '2PE 1:1' for the parallel construction tou theou hemon kai soteros Iesou Christou. Evaluate the counter-arguments: Winer warned against dogmatism; Moulton-Turner noted Koine sometimes lacks a second article for multiple individuals. However, Wallace notes Sharp's rule has NO clear NT exception for two singular, personal, non-proper nouns. Search semantic_grammar.py for 'Granville Sharp' or 'article noun kai noun' in Greek grammars for textbook support.     10. 1 Corinthians 15:24-28 — eschatological submission and deity: Run greek_parser.py --verse '1CO 15:28' to parse hotan de hupotagē auto ta panta tote kai autos ho huios hupotagesetai. The trin-02 study found that hotan...tote with future passive hupotagēsetai indicates a CHANGE of state (future transition), not ongoing subordination. READ the library entry for G5293 (hupotasso) at $WORD_STUDIES/G5293-hupotasso.md. The key question is whether functional/economic submission to the Father in the eschaton negates ontological deity, or whether it parallels the incarnational self-emptying of Phil 2:5-11. If Phil 2 describes the pattern 'equality with God → voluntary self-emptying → exaltation,' then 15:28 may describe the reverse pattern: 'mediatorial kingdom → hand-back → restored equal glory.' Also parse 1 Cor 15:24-25 to understand the structure: the Son destroys all enemies FIRST, then delivers the kingdom. Verses: 1 Corinthians 15:24-28, Philippians 2:5-11, John 17:5 Strong's: G5293, G2758, G3444, G2470

    11. Psalm 110:1 — kyrios for YHWH or for a human king?: Run hebrew_parser.py --verse 'Psa 110:1' to parse ne'um YHWH la'adoni ('The LORD said unto my lord'). The LXX renders this as eipen ho kyrios to kyrio mou — two different uses of kyrios. But the first kyrios = YHWH (the divine name), and the second kyrios = adon (the human lord/master). Perriman argues Jesus's kyrios title derives from the second (adon = exalted human king), not the first (YHWH). But check: does the NT ever apply to Jesus texts where kyrios MUST mean YHWH? Yes — Phil 2:10-11 (citing Isa 45:23 where the speaker is YHWH), Rom 10:13 (citing Joel 2:32 'whosoever shall call upon the name of the LORD [YHWH] shall be saved'), Heb 1:10 (citing Ps 102:25 where the speaker is YHWH). If NT writers apply YHWH-kyrios texts to Jesus, the kyrios of 1 Cor 8:6 is not merely Ps 110:1's adon. READ the library entry for G2962 (kyrios) at $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md. Retrieve Rom 10:13 and Joel 2:32 for comparison. Verses: Psalm 110:1, Philippians 2:10-11, Romans 10:13, Hebrews 1:10-12, Joel 2:32 Strong's: G2962, H3068, H136

    12. The Johannine en/egeneto verb distinction — uncreated existence vs. created becoming: Run greek_parser.py --verse 'JHN 1:1', 'JHN 1:2', 'JHN 1:3'. In v.1-2, the Word 'was' (en, imperfect of eimi G1510 — continuous past existence). In v.3, all things 'were made' (egeneto, aorist of ginomai G1096 — came into being at a point). The Logos 'was' (en) while everything else 'became' (egeneto). The same pattern appears in John 8:58: 'Before Abraham was (genesthai, aorist infinitive — came into being), I am (ego eimi, present tense — timeless being).' READ the library entries for G1510 (eimi) at $WORD_STUDIES/G1510-eimi.md and G1096 (ginomai) at $WORD_STUDIES/G1096-ginomai.md and the grammar reference for absolute ego eimi at $GRAMMAR_REF/greek/absolute-ego-eimi.md. This verb distinction was identified in trin-01 as placing Christ on the 'uncreated side.' Verses: John 1:1-3, John 8:58 Strong's: G1510, G1096, G2258
