    Verses¶

    Question¶

    If Jesus is not God but a created being or merely a perfect human, what logical contradictions arise within the Bible's own framework? Trace the consequences across six domains: (1) worship — Jesus accepts what only God may receive (Ex 20:3-5, Matt 4:10 vs. Matt 14:33, 28:9, John 9:38, 20:28), (2) forgiveness — Jesus exercises what only God can do (Mark 2:5-7), (3) creation — all things made through him yet he is a thing made (John 1:3, Col 1:16), (4) savior exclusivity — YHWH alone is savior yet Jesus saves (Isa 43:11 vs. Titus 2:13, Acts 4:12), (5) prayer/omnipresence — prayer directed to Jesus and his promise to be present everywhere (Acts 7:59, 2 Cor 12:8, Matt 18:20, 28:20), (6) atonement sufficiency — can a finite sacrifice provide eternal redemption (Heb 9:12-14, Heb 7:16, 25)?

    First Commandment: Worship Belongs to YHWH Alone¶

    Thou shalt have no other gods before me. Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness [of any thing] that [is] in heaven above, or that [is] in the earth beneath, or that [is] in the water under the earth: Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them: for I the LORD thy God [am] a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth [generation] of them that hate me;

    Context: The Ten Commandments at Sinai. The first commandment establishes the absolute exclusivity of worship — no bowing down to or serving anything other than YHWH.

    Notes: Domain 1 foundation. The Hebrew shachah (H7812, bow down) and abad (H5647, serve) establish the worship prohibition that Jesus himself cites in Matt 4:10. If Jesus accepts the same worship that YHWH commands exclusively for himself, the first commandment is either violated or Jesus is within YHWH's identity.

    Serve YHWH Exclusively — Quoted by Jesus in the Temptation¶

    Thou shalt fear the LORD thy God, and serve him, and shalt swear by his name.

    Context: Moses' instruction to Israel in the Shema context (Deut 6:4-15). Commands exclusive fear, service, and oath-loyalty to YHWH.

    Notes: Jesus cites this passage in Matt 4:10 to refuse Satan's worship request. The irony is devastating if Jesus is not God: he cites the exclusive-worship command and then accepts the very worship he said belongs only to God.

    Jesus Cites Exclusive Worship Command Against Satan¶

    Then saith Jesus unto him, Get thee hence, Satan: for it is written, Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve.

    Context: The wilderness temptation. Satan offers Jesus the kingdoms of the world in exchange for worship. Jesus refuses by citing the exclusive-worship command.

    Notes: Jesus explicitly affirms that worship belongs to God alone ('him only shalt thou serve'). He then accepts worship from disciples (Matt 14:33, 28:9, 28:17) without any correction. The accept/refuse contrast with this verse is the structural crux of Domain 1.

    Angel Refuses Worship a Second Time — Double Witness¶

    And I John saw these things, and heard [them]. And when I had heard and seen, I fell down to worship before the feet of the angel which shewed me these things. Then saith he unto me, See [thou do it] not: for I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren the prophets, and of them which keep the sayings of this book: worship God.

    Context: End of Revelation. John again falls to worship the angel, and the angel again refuses with nearly identical language to 19:10.

    Notes: The double refusal by an angel — a righteous heavenly being — establishes the pattern: no creature, however exalted, may accept worship. The refusal is immediate, forceful, and redirective. Jesus never exhibits this behavior in any worship acceptance passage.

    Peter Refuses Worship from Cornelius¶

    And as Peter was coming in, Cornelius met him, and fell down at his feet, and worshipped [him]. But Peter took him up, saying, Stand up; I myself also am a man.

    Context: Cornelius, a devout Roman centurion, meets Peter and falls at his feet in worship. Peter immediately refuses.

    Notes: Peter's reason for refusing: 'I myself also am a man.' The implication is that a man (human) must not accept worship. If Jesus is merely a man and accepts worship, Peter's refusal is incoherent.

    And when the people saw what Paul had done, they lifted up their voices, saying in the speech of Lycaonia, The gods are come down to us in the likeness of men. And they called Barnabas, Jupiter; and Paul, Mercurius, because he was the chief speaker.     Context: After Paul heals a lame man at Lystra. The crowds attempt to sacrifice to Paul and Barnabas as gods. The apostles react with horror.

    Notes: The apostles' reaction is visceral — tearing clothes and running into the crowd. Their reason: 'We also are men of like passions with you.' They redirect worship to 'the living God, which made heaven, and earth.' This is the same creation language used of Christ in Col 1:16 and Heb 1:2.

    Post-Resurrection: Women Worship Jesus — Accepted¶

    And as they went to tell his disciples, behold, Jesus met them, saying, All hail. And they came and held him by the feet, and worshipped him.

    Context: After the resurrection. The women encounter the risen Jesus on the way to tell the disciples. They hold his feet and worship.

    Notes: Post-resurrection worship acceptance. Proskyneo used again. Jesus does not redirect this worship to the Father.

    Post-Resurrection: Disciples Worship on the Mountain — Accepted¶

    And when they saw him, they worshipped him: but some doubted.

    Context: The Great Commission scene on the mountain in Galilee. The disciples worship the risen Jesus before he gives the baptismal formula.

    Notes: Immediately before the Trinitarian baptismal formula (Matt 28:19 — 'in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost'). Worship of Jesus is accepted as the context for the Trinitarian commission.

    Healed Blind Man Worships Jesus — Accepted After Faith Conversation¶

    And he said, Lord, I believe. And he worshipped him.

    Context: After Jesus heals the man born blind and reveals himself as the Son of Man. The man confesses faith and worships.

    Notes: Jesus actively initiates the faith conversation (John 9:35-37: 'Dost thou believe on the Son of God?... Thou hast both seen him, and it is he that talketh with thee'). Then the man worships and Jesus accepts. Jesus is not a passive recipient — he elicits worship.

    Co-Worship of God and the Lamb — The Climactic Biblical Worship Scene¶

    And when he had taken the book, the four beasts and four [and] twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one of them harps, and golden vials full of odours, which are the prayers of saints. And they sung a new song, saying, Thou art worthy to take the book, and to open the seals thereof: for thou wast slain, and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood out of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation; And hast made us unto our God kings and priests: and we shall reign on the earth. And I beheld, and I heard the voice of many angels round about the throne and the beasts and the elders: and the number of them was ten thousand times ten thousand, and thousands of thousands; Saying with a loud voice, Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing. And every creature which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as are in the sea, and all that are in them, heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, [be] unto him that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever. And the four beasts said, Amen. And the four [and] twenty elders fell down and worshipped him that liveth for ever and ever.

    Context: Revelation 5 — the heavenly throne room. After the Lamb alone is found worthy to open the scroll, all heaven and creation join in escalating worship directed to both God on the throne and the Lamb.

    Notes: The worship escalation: (1) 4 beasts + 24 elders worship the Lamb (5:8-10), (2) myriads of angels declare the Lamb worthy to receive a sevenfold doxology (5:12: dynamis, ploutos, sophia, ischys, time, doxa, eulogia), (3) every creature gives identical worship formula to God AND the Lamb (5:13: eulogia, time, doxa, kratos — unto both, for ever and ever), (4) the elders fall down and worship (5:14). If the Lamb is a creature, every creature in the universe commits idolatry in heaven, and the elders worship a fellow creature.
