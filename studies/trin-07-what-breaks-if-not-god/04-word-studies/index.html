    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    If Jesus is not God but a created being or merely a perfect human, what logical contradictions arise within the Bible's own framework? Trace the consequences across six domains: (1) worship — Jesus accepts what only God may receive (Ex 20:3-5, Matt 4:10 vs. Matt 14:33, 28:9, John 9:38, 20:28), (2) forgiveness — Jesus exercises what only God can do (Mark 2:5-7), (3) creation — all things made through him yet he is a thing made (John 1:3, Col 1:16), (4) savior exclusivity — YHWH alone is savior yet Jesus saves (Isa 43:11 vs. Titus 2:13, Acts 4:12), (5) prayer/omnipresence — prayer directed to Jesus and his promise to be present everywhere (Acts 7:59, 2 Cor 12:8, Matt 18:20, 28:20), (6) atonement sufficiency — can a finite sacrifice provide eternal redemption (Heb 9:12-14, Heb 7:16, 25)?

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    Proskyneo (worship, do obeisance) — the primary NT worship verb. Used for worship of God (Matt 4:10, Rev 19:10), worship of Jesus (Matt 14:33, 28:9, John 9:38), and worship refused by righteous creatures (Rev 19:10, 22:8-9, Acts 10:25-26). The LXX uses proskyneo to translate the Hebrew shachah (H7812), establishing the semantic connection between OT and NT worship vocabulary.

    Semantic categories: religious worship, prostration/homage, angelic worship

    Key verses: Matt 4:10, Matt 14:33, Rev 19:10, Rev 5:14, Heb 1:6

    Relevance: Central to Domain 1. The identical verb is used in both acceptance passages (Jesus) and refusal passages (angels, apostles), proving the worship offered to Jesus is the same kind that righteous creatures refuse.

    H7812 — shachah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7812-shachah.md

    Occurrences: 194

    Shachah (bow down, worship) — the primary OT worship verb. Used for worship of God (Exo 20:5, Deut 6:13), forbidden worship of idols, and occasionally social obeisance. The LXX consistently translates shachah as proskyneo, creating the OT-NT worship vocabulary chain.

    Semantic categories: divine worship, prostration, social obeisance

    Key verses: Exo 20:5, Deut 6:13, Psa 95:6, Isa 45:23

    Relevance: OT foundation for Domain 1. Establishes that worship exclusivity is rooted in the first commandment and Israel's covenant identity.

    G2316 — theos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2316-theos.md

    Occurrences: 1343

    Theos (God) — applied to God the Father throughout the NT, and to Jesus in key passages: John 1:1 ('the Word was God'), John 20:28 ('my God'), Titus 2:13 ('the great God and our Saviour'), Heb 1:8 ('Thy throne, O God'). The use of theos for Jesus in confessional and doxological contexts establishes his divine identity.

    Semantic categories: divine name/title, deity identification, christological title

    Key verses: John 1:1, John 20:28, Titus 2:13, Heb 1:8, 1 John 5:20

    Relevance: Central to Domains 1-2 and Focus Areas 2, 11, 12. The application of theos to Jesus in Thomas's confession, the Granville Sharp constructions, and the Father's address in Hebrews 1 are direct deity identifications.

    G2962 — kyrios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2962-kyrios.md

    Occurrences: 748

    Kyrios (Lord) — the LXX replacement for YHWH. Applied to Jesus systematically throughout the NT (Phil 2:11, Rom 10:9, 1 Cor 12:3). When NT authors apply OT YHWH-kyrios passages to Jesus (Isa 45:23 → Phil 2:10-11, Joel 2:32 → Rom 10:13), they identify Jesus with YHWH.

    Semantic categories: divine name substitute, YHWH equivalent, christological title, social authority

    Key verses: Phil 2:11, Rom 10:13, 1 Cor 12:3, John 20:28, Acts 7:59

    Relevance: Central to Domains 4-5 and Focus Area 8. The systematic application of kyrios-YHWH passages to Jesus constitutes the YHWH-title transfer system.

    H3068 — YHWH¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3068-yhwh.md

    Occurrences: 6534

    YHWH — the divine name, occurring 6,519 times in the OT. The personal name of Israel's covenant God, associated with exclusive titles: 'first and last' (Isa 44:6), 'alone created' (Isa 44:24), 'no saviour beside me' (Isa 43:11), 'my glory I will not give to another' (Isa 42:8).

    Semantic categories: divine name, covenant identity, exclusive self-identification

    Key verses: Exo 3:14, Isa 42:8, Isa 43:11, Isa 44:6, Isa 44:24

    Relevance: Foundation for Focus Area 8 (YHWH-title transfer). Every exclusive YHWH title that is applied to Jesus in the NT creates a logical contradiction if Jesus is not within YHWH's identity.

    G859 — aphesis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G859-aphesis.md

    Occurrences: 17

    Aphesis (forgiveness, remission) — the specific forgiveness vocabulary used for the release from sins. Jesus pronounces aphesis in Mark 2:5 ('thy sins be forgiven') using the cognate verb aphiemi (G863). The NT connects aphesis with Christ's blood (Eph 1:7, Col 1:14).

    Semantic categories: forgiveness of sins, release/remission, divine prerogative

    Key verses: Mark 2:5, Luke 7:48, Eph 1:7, Col 1:14

    Relevance: Central to Domain 2. The forgiveness vocabulary that the scribes correctly identify as God's exclusive prerogative.

    H5545 — calach¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5545-calach.md

    Occurrences: 49

    Calach (forgive, pardon) — Hebrew verb for divine forgiveness. Exclusively used with God as subject in the OT — no human ever 'calach's' another human. This establishes forgiveness of sins as a distinctly divine action in the OT framework.

    Semantic categories: divine forgiveness, pardon, God-exclusive action

    Key verses: Exo 34:9, Num 14:19, 1 Ki 8:30, Dan 9:19

    Relevance: Central to Domain 2. The Hebrew background establishes that the scribes in Mark 2:7 were drawing on genuine OT theology — forgiveness is God's exclusive prerogative.

    G4990 — soter¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4990-soter.md

    Occurrences: 24

    Soter (savior) — applied to both God the Father (1 Tim 1:1, 2:3, Titus 1:3) and Jesus Christ (Titus 2:13, 2 Pet 1:1, Luke 2:11). The dual application of soter to God and Jesus, combined with the Granville Sharp constructions identifying Jesus as 'God and Savior,' places Jesus within the YHWH-savior identity.

    Semantic categories: savior title, divine attribute, christological title

    Key verses: Luke 2:11, Titus 2:13, 2 Pet 1:1, Acts 4:12

    Relevance: Central to Domain 4. The savior title bridges YHWH's exclusive claim (Isa 43:11) and Jesus' identification as savior.

    H3467 — yasha¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3467-yasha.md

    Occurrences: 238

    Yasha (save, deliver) — the root behind moshia (savior) in Isa 43:11. Used for both divine salvation (Isa 43:11, 45:22) and temporal human deliverance (Neh 9:27, Obad 1:21). The anti-deity counterargument uses the human-savior instances to challenge savior exclusivity.

    Semantic categories: divine salvation, temporal deliverance, military rescue

    Key verses: Isa 43:11, Isa 45:22, Neh 9:27, Judg 3:9

    Relevance: Central to Domain 4 and Focus Area 10. The comparison between moshia in Isa 43:11 (YHWH as exclusive eternal savior) and moshiim in Neh 9:27 (plural human deliverers in temporal contexts) tests the anti-deity counterargument.

    G2936 — ktizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2936-ktizo.md

    Occurrences: 16

    Ktizo (create) — the NT creation verb. Used for God's creation (Rom 1:25, Eph 3:9) and Christ's creation agency (Col 1:16: ta panta... ektistai, 'all things have been created'). The comprehensive scope of 'ta panta' (all things) creates the self-creation paradox if Christ is himself a created being.

    Semantic categories: divine creation, cosmic agency, creation vocabulary

    Key verses: Col 1:16, Eph 3:9, Rev 4:11, Rev 10:6

    Relevance: Central to Domain 3. The verb that assigns creation to Christ in Col 1:16 with comprehensive scope.

    H1254 — bara¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1254-bara.md

    Occurrences: 59

    Bara (create) — the distinctively divine creation verb in the OT. Used exclusively with God as subject. Establishes creation as God's exclusive prerogative, which the NT then assigns to Christ (John 1:3, Col 1:16).

    Semantic categories: divine creation, ex nihilo creation, God-exclusive action

    Key verses: Gen 1:1, Gen 1:27, Isa 43:1, Isa 45:18

    Relevance: Central to Domain 3. The OT creation vocabulary that establishes creation as a divine-only action.

    G166 — aionios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G166-aionios.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Aionios (eternal, everlasting) — used for eternal life, eternal redemption (Heb 9:12), and the eternal Spirit (Heb 9:14). In the atonement context, aionios establishes the non-temporal quality of Christ's redemptive work.

    Semantic categories: eternal duration, divine quality, eschatological permanence

    Key verses: Heb 9:12, Heb 9:14, John 3:16, Rom 6:23

    Relevance: Central to Domain 6. 'Eternal redemption' and 'eternal Spirit' in Heb 9:12-14 establish the infinite quality of Christ's sacrifice that a finite creature cannot provide.

    G179 — akatalytos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G179-akatalytos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Akatalytos (indestructible, indissoluble) — hapax legomenon in Heb 7:16. Describes the 'power of an indestructible life' (dynamis zoes akatalytou) that qualifies Jesus as priest after the order of Melchizedek. Compound: a- (not) + kata- (down) + lytos (loosed) = 'un-dissolvable.' The word places Christ's life outside the category of creaturely mortality — it cannot be dissolved, un-made, or brought to nothing.

    Semantic categories: indestructible quality of life, christological implication — more than human

    Key verses: Heb 7:16

    Relevance: Central to Domain 6. The hapax legomenon that anchors the atonement sufficiency argument. A created being's life is by definition katalytos (dissolvable) — the Creator could un-create it. Akatalytos asserts Jesus' life is inherently beyond destruction.

    G531 — aparabatos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G531-aparabatos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Aparabatos (unchangeable, untransferable) — describes Christ's priesthood in Heb 7:24. Because he continues forever, his priesthood cannot pass to another. The permanence depends on indestructible life (Heb 7:16).

    Semantic categories: unchangeable quality, priesthood permanence, non-transferable office

    Key verses: Heb 7:24

    Relevance: Domain 6 link in the atonement chain. Unchangeable priesthood depends on eternal life; eternal life requires a nature beyond destruction.

    G629 — apolytrosis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G629-apolytrosis.md

    Occurrences: 10

    Apolytrosis (redemption) — the NT redemption noun. Used for the redemption through Christ's blood (Eph 1:7, Col 1:14, Rom 3:24). Heb 9:12 specifies 'eternal redemption' (aionion lutrosin, using the related G3085 lytrosis).

    Semantic categories: redemption, ransom payment, liberation from sin

    Key verses: Rom 3:24, Eph 1:7, Col 1:14, Heb 9:12

    Relevance: Domain 6. The redemption vocabulary that connects to the ransom impossibility of Psa 49:7-8.

    G3083 — lytron¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3083-lytron.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Lytron (ransom) — the price paid for redemption. Jesus gave his life as 'a ransom for many' (Matt 20:28, Mark 10:45). The anti-lytron (1 Tim 2:6) expands the scope to 'all.'

    Semantic categories: ransom price, redemption payment, substitutionary exchange

    Key verses: Matt 20:28, Mark 10:45

    Relevance: Domain 6. The ransom vocabulary that connects to Psa 49:7-8's declaration that no human can pay the redemption price.

    H3724 — kopher¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3724-kopher.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Kopher (ransom-price) — the OT ransom vocabulary. In Psa 49:8, kopher is the ransom that no human can give to God for his brother's soul. The redemption is 'precious' (yaqar) — costly beyond human means.

    Semantic categories: ransom price, atonement payment, human impossibility

    Key verses: Psa 49:7-8, Exo 21:30, Job 33:24

    Relevance: Domain 6. Psa 49:7-8 uses kopher to declare the ransom impossible for any human — only a divine redeemer can pay.

    G2435 — hilasterion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2435-hilasterion.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Hilasterion (propitiation, mercy seat) — Christ as the hilasterion (Rom 3:25). Connects Christ to the OT mercy seat (kapporeth) where atonement was made. The propitiation is directed toward God's justice.

    Semantic categories: propitiation, mercy seat, atonement focus

    Key verses: Rom 3:25, 1 John 2:2, Heb 9:5

    Relevance: Domain 6. Christ as the mercy seat connects his sacrifice to the entire OT atonement system.

    G1391 — doxa¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1391-doxa.md

    Occurrences: 168

    Doxa (glory) — used for God's glory (Isa 42:8 LXX: 'my glory I will not give to another') and Christ's glory (John 1:14, 17:5, Rev 5:12-13). The sharing of doxa between God and the Lamb in Rev 5:13 is a co-worship formula.

    Semantic categories: divine glory, worship ascription, christological co-glory

    Key verses: Isa 42:8, John 1:14, Rev 5:12-13, 2 Pet 1:17

    Relevance: Central to Focus Area 11. YHWH says 'my glory I will not give to another' (Isa 42:8), yet the Lamb receives identical doxa in Rev 5:12-13. Either the Lamb is 'another' (violating Isa 42:8) or the Lamb is within YHWH's identity.

    G4416 — prototokos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4416-prototokos.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Prototokos (firstborn) — title of preeminence, not birth order. In Col 1:15 ('firstborn of every creature'), it functions as a sovereignty title (cf. Psa 89:27 where David is called 'firstborn' though he was the youngest). The very next verse (Col 1:16) assigns all creation to Christ, refuting the 'first created' reading.

    Semantic categories: preeminence title, sovereignty, rank designation

    Key verses: Col 1:15, Heb 1:6, Psa 89:27, Rom 8:29

    Relevance: Supports Domain 3. The prototokos title in context proves rank, not creation — Col 1:16 immediately assigns creation to the one called 'firstborn.'

    H905 — bad¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H905-bad.md

    Occurrences: 203

    Bad (alone, by oneself) — used in Isa 44:24 (lebaddi, 'alone') to describe YHWH's exclusive creation agency. The word eliminates any created assistant in the creation process.

    Semantic categories: solitary agency, exclusivity, unassisted action

    Key verses: Isa 44:24, Neh 9:6

    Relevance: Central to Domain 3. 'Alone' (lebaddi) in Isa 44:24 eliminates any created co-creator, forcing the conclusion that Christ (through whom all things were made) must be within YHWH's identity.

    G5481 — charakter¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5481-charakter.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Charakter (exact imprint) — used in Heb 1:3 to describe the Son as the 'express image' (charakter) of God's person (hypostasis). Like a coin stamped from a die, the Son is an exact reproduction of God's essential being.

    Semantic categories: exact representation, divine image, ontological identity

    Key verses: Heb 1:3

    Relevance: Focus Area 12. Charakter asserts the Son is not merely similar to God but the precise reproduction of God's essential nature — a claim impossible for a created being.

    G5287 — hupostasis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5287-hupostasis.md

    Occurrences: 5

    Hupostasis (substance, being, essence) — in Heb 1:3, the Son is the charakter of God's hupostasis. Hupostasis here means the underlying reality or essential being of God.

    Semantic categories: essential being, substance, underlying reality

    Key verses: Heb 1:3, Heb 3:14, Heb 11:1

    Relevance: Focus Area 12. Combined with charakter, this describes the Son as the exact reproduction of God's essential being.

    G110 — athanasia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G110-athanasia.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Athanasia (immortality) — in 1 Tim 6:16, God 'only hath immortality.' This is an attribute unique to God. The connection to akatalytos (Heb 7:16) is significant: if only God has inherent immortality, the 'indestructible life' of Christ's priesthood implies his divine nature.

    Semantic categories: divine immortality, inherent life, God-exclusive attribute

    Key verses: 1 Tim 6:16, 1 Cor 15:53-54

    Relevance: Domain 6 support. If only God has immortality (1 Tim 6:16) and Christ has 'indestructible life' (Heb 7:16), Christ must share God's nature.

    G1510 — eimi¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1510-eimi.md

    Occurrences: 155

    Eimi (I am) — the verb used in Jesus' 'ego eimi' sayings. The absolute use (without predicate) in John 8:58 ('Before Abraham was, I am') echoes the LXX of Exo 3:14 (ego eimi ho on). The construction claims eternal self-existence.

    Semantic categories: self-existence, divine name echo, eternal being

    Key verses: John 8:58, John 13:19, John 18:5, Exo 3:14

    Relevance: Supports Focus Area 8 (YHWH-title transfer). The ego eimi sayings are indirect deity claims recognized by Jesus' contemporaries (John 8:59 — they took up stones).

    G3444 — morphe¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3444-morphe.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Morphe (form) — in Phil 2:6 ('being in the form of God'). Morphe refers to the essential nature or characteristic attributes, not merely outward appearance. Christ existed in the 'form of God' before taking the 'form of a servant.'

    Semantic categories: essential nature, divine form, incarnational vocabulary

    Key verses: Phil 2:6, Phil 2:7

    Relevance: Supports the kenosis passage in Focus Area 8. 'Form of God' (morphe theou) describes Christ's pre-incarnate divine nature.

    G1941 — epikaleomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1941-epikaleomai.md

    Occurrences: 33

    Epikaleomai (call upon, invoke) — the verb for religious invocation of God's name. In the LXX, it renders the Hebrew qara b'shem YHWH ('to call on the name of the LORD'). In the NT, this invocation is directed to Jesus: Acts 7:59 (Stephen calls upon Jesus), 1 Cor 1:2 (Christians defined as those who 'call upon the name of our Lord Jesus Christ'), Rom 10:13 (Joel 2:32's 'whoever calls on the name of the LORD' applied to Christ).

    Semantic categories: religious invocation, prayer address, YHWH-invocation transferred to Jesus

    Key verses: Acts 7:59, Acts 9:14, Acts 22:16, Rom 10:13, 1 Cor 1:2

    Relevance: Central to Domain 5. The transfer of YHWH-invocation language to Jesus proves prayer to Jesus is not peripheral but definitional for Christian identity (1 Cor 1:2).

    G5092 — time¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5092-timē.md

    Occurrences: 43

    Time (honor, value, price) — used in divine doxologies alongside doxa (glory). In Rev 5:12-13, time appears in the worship formulas directed to both God and the Lamb. The co-attribution of time to God and the Lamb places them in the same worship economy.

    Semantic categories: divine honor, christological honor, worship vocabulary, monetary value

    Key verses: Rev 4:11, Rev 5:12, Rev 5:13, 1 Tim 1:17, 1 Tim 6:16

    Relevance: Focus Area 11 (Rev 5 co-worship). Time in the doxological formulas confirms that the Lamb receives the same categories of divine honor as God on the throne.

    G1849 — exousia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1849-exousia.md

    Occurrences: 103

    Exousia (authority, power) — in Mark 2:10, Jesus claims 'authority on earth to forgive sins.' In Matt 9:8, the crowds note that God 'had given such authority to men.' The delegation counterargument uses Matt 9:8 to argue forgiveness was delegated, but the Greek anthropois (dative plural 'to men') is the crowd's interpretation, not Jesus' own claim.

    Semantic categories: authority, power, divine prerogative

    Key verses: Mark 2:10, Matt 9:8, Matt 28:18

    Relevance: Domain 2 and Focus Area 10. Tests the delegation counterargument: does 'authority given to men' prove forgiveness was delegated, or does the crowd misidentify the nature of Jesus' authority?

    G4982 — sozo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4982-sozo.md

    Occurrences: 120

    Sozo (save) — the primary NT salvation verb. Used for Jesus' saving activity (Matt 1:21, Luke 19:10, Heb 7:25). The LXX uses sozo to translate the Hebrew yasha (H3467), connecting NT salvation vocabulary to the OT YHWH-saves framework.

    Semantic categories: salvation, deliverance, healing, rescue

    Key verses: Matt 1:21, Luke 19:10, Acts 4:12, Heb 7:25

    Relevance: Domain 4. The salvation verb connecting Jesus' saving activity to YHWH's exclusive savior claim through the LXX translation chain.

    G3085 — lytrosis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3085-lytrosis.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Lytrosis (redemption) — used in Heb 9:12 for 'eternal redemption' (aionion lutrosin) that Christ obtained by his own blood. Distinguished from apolytrosis (G629) by its more direct connection to the lytron (ransom) word family.

    Semantic categories: redemption, ransom-based liberation

    Key verses: Heb 9:12, Luke 1:68, Luke 2:38

    Relevance: Domain 6. The specific redemption noun in Heb 9:12 modified by aionios (eternal).
