    Scope

    Question¶

    If Jesus is not God but a created being or merely a perfect human, what logical contradictions arise within the Bible's own framework? Trace the consequences across six domains: (1) worship — Jesus accepts what only God may receive (Ex 20:3-5, Matt 4:10 vs. Matt 14:33, 28:9, John 9:38, 20:28), (2) forgiveness — Jesus exercises what only God can do (Mark 2:5-7), (3) creation — all things made through him yet he is a thing made (John 1:3, Col 1:16), (4) savior exclusivity — YHWH alone is savior yet Jesus saves (Isa 43:11 vs. Titus 2:13, Acts 4:12), (5) prayer/omnipresence — prayer directed to Jesus and his promise to be present everywhere (Acts 7:59, 2 Cor 12:8, Matt 18:20, 28:20), (6) atonement sufficiency — can a finite sacrifice provide eternal redemption (Heb 9:12-14, Heb 7:16, 25)?

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The Worship Accept/Refuse Pattern — Structural Test for Deity: This is the single most structurally decisive test for the entire study. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4352-proskyneo.md and $WORD_STUDIES/H7812-shachah.md and $WORD_STUDIES/TR-shachah-proskyneo.md to establish the semantic range of worship vocabulary. The pattern is binary: every righteous creature who receives proskuneo REFUSES it (angels in Rev 19:10, 22:8-9; apostles in Acts 10:25-26, 14:11-15), while Jesus ALONE accepts it without correction (Matt 2:11, 14:33, 28:9, 28:17, John 9:38, 20:28, Heb 1:6, Rev 5:8-14). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Matt 14:33 and Rev 19:10 to confirm the identical proskuneo in both accept and refuse cases. Then retrieve Rev 5:8-14 in full chapter context — the Lamb receives the SAME worship formula as God on the throne. If Jesus is not God, then God COMMANDS idolatry in Heb 1:6, and the entire worship economy of Revelation is blasphemous. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on Rev 5:13 to find OT worship parallels attributed to the Lamb. Verses: Exo 20:3-5, Deut 6:13, Matt 4:10, Rev 19:10, Rev 22:8-9, Acts 10:25-26, Acts 14:11-15, Matt 14:33, Matt 28:9, Matt 28:17, John 9:38, John 20:28, Heb 1:6, Rev 5:8-14 Strong's: G4352, H7812

    2. Thomas's Confession (John 20:28) — The Explicit Deity Claim Accepted: Thomas's 'ho kyrios mou kai ho theos mou' is addressed directly TO Jesus (eipen auto = 'said to him'), refuting the anti-deity 'exclamation' reading. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2316-theos.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md for the semantic ranges of these terms. Run greek_parser.py --verse on John 20:28 to verify the grammatical construction — the doubled article with personal pronoun 'mou' makes this a personal confession of identity, not a surprised oath. Jesus responds in 20:29 without correction, pronouncing blessing on those who believe — constituting acceptance of the title 'my God.' This occurs just 11 verses after 'my God and your God' (20:17), creating the interpretive tension that prior study trin-05 resolved. If Jesus is not God, he accepts the most explicit deity confession in the NT without demurral — the moral implications are devastating. Verses: John 20:28, John 20:29, John 20:17 Strong's: G2316, G2962

    3. Forgiveness as Divine Prerogative — Mark 2:5-7 and the Delegation Counterargument: The scribes' reasoning in Mark 2:7 is theologically correct — forgiveness of sins against God IS God's exclusive prerogative (Isa 43:25, Dan 9:9, Exo 34:6-7). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G859-aphesis.md, $WORD_STUDIES/H5545-calach.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/WG-forgiveness.md for the forgiveness vocabulary chain. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Mark 2:5, 2:7, and 2:10. The delegation counterargument (Zarley) cites Matt 9:8 and John 20:23 — the research agent should retrieve these in full context and test whether 'authority to men' (plural anthropois) refers to Jesus specifically or humanity generally. The decisive test is whether Jesus forgives sins committed against God rather than against himself — the paralytic never sinned against Jesus personally, yet Jesus declared absolute forgiveness. If Jesus is merely human, he usurped God's prerogative by his own disciples' correct theological reasoning. Luke 7:48-49 repeats the pattern independently. Verses: Mark 2:5-7, Mark 2:10, Luke 7:48-49, Luke 5:20-21, Isa 43:25, Dan 9:9, Exo 34:6-7, Matt 9:8, John 20:23 Strong's: G859, H5545, G1849

    4. The Creation Paradox — 'All Things' Made Through Christ vs. 'Alone/By Myself': Four NT witnesses independently assign creation to Christ: John 1:3 (panta di autou egeneto), Col 1:16 (ta panta di autou kai eis auton ektistai), Heb 1:2 (di hou kai epoiesen tous aionas), 1 Cor 8:6 (di hou ta panta). READ $WORD_STUDIES/H1254-bara.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2936-ktizo.md, $WORD_STUDIES/WG-created.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/H905-bad.md. Yet YHWH declares in Isa 44:24 that he created 'alone' (lebaddi) and 'by myself' (me'itti) — excluding even instrumental agency by a created assistant. Run hebrew_parser.py --verse on Isa 44:24 to verify the emphatic exclusionary language. If Christ is a created being, then: (1) he is part of 'all things' that were made through him — creating himself; (2) YHWH lied about creating alone. Neither is tenable. The resolution: Christ is within YHWH's identity, not a separate created agent. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot on John 1:3 to find creation-exclusivity parallels, and --hybrid-nt on Isa 44:24 to find NT creation-through-Christ passages. Verses: John 1:3, Col 1:16-17, Heb 1:2, 1 Cor 8:6, Isa 44:24, Isa 45:18 Strong's: G2936, H1254, H905, G3956

    5. Savior Exclusivity — YHWH Alone Saves vs. Jesus Saves: Isaiah's savior-exclusivity claims are among the strongest monotheistic declarations in the OT. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4990-soter.md, $WORD_STUDIES/H3467-yasha.md, $WORD_STUDIES/TR-yasha-sozo.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/WG-salvation.md. Isa 43:11 declares 'beside me there is no moshia (savior)' and Isa 45:21-22 says 'there is no God else beside me; a just God and a saviour; there is none beside me.' Yet Acts 4:12 declares salvation only in Jesus' name, and Titus 2:13 identifies Jesus as 'the great God and our Saviour.' Run greek_parser.py --verse on Titus 2:13 and 2 Pet 1:1 to verify the Granville Sharp construction (article-noun-kai-noun). The decisive test: is there a qualitative difference between temporal national deliverance (Judges-era saviors) and eternal salvation from sin? Isa 43:10-13 broadens the context beyond Red Sea to ontological uniqueness. Verses: Isa 43:11, Isa 45:21-22, Acts 4:12, Titus 2:13, 2 Pet 1:1, Luke 2:11, Neh 9:27, Obad 1:21, Isa 43:10 Strong's: G4990, H3467, G4982

    6. Prayer to Jesus and Omnipresence Claims — Divine Attributes in Practice: Three clear NT examples show prayer directed specifically to Jesus: Stephen's dying prayer 'Lord Jesus, receive my spirit' (Acts 7:59), Paul's thrice-repeated prayer to 'the Lord' who responds with 'my grace is sufficient' (2 Cor 12:8-9 — identifiable as Jesus from context), and John's 'Even so, come, Lord Jesus' (Rev 22:20). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md for the semantic range of kyrios applied to Jesus. Additionally, 1 Cor 1:2 defines Christians as those who 'call upon the name of our Lord Jesus Christ' — language Joel 2:32 uses for calling upon YHWH (cf. Rom 10:13 which applies Joel 2:32 to Jesus). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Acts 7:59 and 2 Cor 12:8 to analyze the prayer language. Matt 18:20 and 28:20 claim Jesus' simultaneous presence everywhere believers are — if believers are scattered across the globe, this requires omnipresence. No created being in Scripture claims or exercises omnipresence. If Jesus is not God, prayer to him is idolatrous misdirection, and his omnipresence promises are false. Verses: Acts 7:59, 2 Cor 12:8-9, Rev 22:20, 1 Cor 1:2, Matt 18:20, Matt 28:20, John 14:13-14, John 14:23, Rom 10:13 Strong's: G2962, G1941, G4314

    7. Atonement Sufficiency — Can a Finite Sacrifice Produce Eternal Redemption?: Hebrews constructs a three-link argument chain for atonement sufficiency. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G166-aionios.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G629-apolytrosis.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2435-hilasterion.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G3083-lytron.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G531-aparabatos.md, $WORD_STUDIES/H3724-kopher.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/WG-atonement.md. Link 1: Christ holds priesthood by 'the power of an indestructible (akatalytos, G179) life' (Heb 7:16). Link 2: His priesthood is 'unchangeable' (aparabatos, G531) because 'he ever liveth' (Heb 7:24-25). Link 3: He obtained 'eternal redemption' (aionion lutrosin) through the 'eternal Spirit' (Heb 9:12-14). A created being's existence is by definition contingent — the Creator could un-create it — making its life NOT inherently indestructible. Psa 49:7-8 explicitly states no human can redeem his brother, 'for the redemption of their soul is precious [costly beyond human means].' Run greek_parser.py --verse on Heb 7:16, Heb 9:12, and Heb 9:14 to verify the Greek akatalytos and aionios terms. Acts 20:28 attributes the blood to God directly. If Jesus is not God, the atonement chain breaks at its foundation. Verses: Heb 7:16, Heb 7:24-25, Heb 9:12-14, Heb 10:11-14, Psa 49:7-8, Acts 20:28, Acts 2:24 Strong's: G166, G179, G531, G629, G2435, G3083, H3724

    8. The YHWH-Title Transfer System — OT Divine Titles on Jesus: The NT authors systematically take passages where YHWH is the exclusive subject and apply them to Jesus. READ $WORD_STUDIES/H3068-yhwh.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md. The transfer chains are: Isa 44:6 ('I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God') → Rev 1:17 ('I am the first and the last'); Isa 45:23 ('unto me every knee shall bow') → Phil 2:10-11 ('at the name of Jesus every knee should bow'); Joel 2:32 ('whosoever shall call on the name of the LORD shall be delivered') → Rom 10:13 ('whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved'); Psa 102:25-27 (YHWH as creator) → Heb 1:10-12 (applied to the Son); Isa 40:3 ('prepare ye the way of the LORD') → Matt 3:3 (applied to Jesus). In each case the OT context is exclusively monotheistic — YHWH says 'beside me there is no God.' Delegation cannot explain transferring the title 'there is no God beside me' to a non-God being. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-nt on Isa 44:6, Isa 45:23, and Psa 102:25 to discover additional transfer instances.     9. The Self-Defeating Moral Argument — If Not God, Then the Bible's Greatest Sinner: This focus area synthesizes findings from all six domains into a single devastating reductio ad absurdum. If Jesus is not God: he accepted worship that only God may receive (Domain 1) — violating the first commandment he himself cited in Matt 4:10; he forgave sins committed against God (Domain 2) — blasphemy by the scribes' theologically correct reasoning; he claims credit for creating all things (Domain 3) — either self-creating (impossible) or lying; he accepts the exclusive savior role YHWH reserved for himself (Domain 4) — usurping God's title; he receives prayer and promises simultaneous presence everywhere (Domain 5) — deceiving believers into misdirected prayer; and he offers a sacrifice Psa 49:7-8 says no human can provide (Domain 6) — meaning the atonement fails. The Bible's greatest moral hero becomes simultaneously its greatest violator of the first two commandments. No single coherent non-deity Christology can sustain itself across all six domains without internal contradiction. This is not merely a proof-texting exercise — it is a test of systemic biblical coherence. Verses: Matt 4:10, Mark 2:7, John 1:3, Isa 43:11, Acts 7:59, Psa 49:7-8, Exo 20:3-5 Strong's: G4352, G859, G2936, G4990, G2962

Verses: Isa 42:8, Rev 5:13-14, Matt 9:8, Neh 9:27, Obad 1:21, John 20:23 Strong's: G4352, G1391, H3467

    11. Rev 5 — Co-Worship of God and the Lamb in Identical Formulas: Revelation 5 is the Bible's climactic worship scene. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1391-doxa.md for the glory vocabulary. In Rev 4:11, God on the throne receives 'glory and honour and power.' In Rev 5:12, the Lamb receives 'power, riches, wisdom, strength, honour, glory, and blessing' — a SEVEN-fold expansion. Then in 5:13, every creature attributes 'Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power' to BOTH 'him that sitteth upon the throne, AND unto the Lamb, for ever and ever.' The same formula, the same duration ('for ever and ever'), the same universality ('every creature'). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 5:12 and Rev 5:13 to compare the worship language. If the Lamb is a creature, then every creature in the universe — including the twenty-four elders who 'fell down and worshipped' (5:14) — commits idolatry in heaven. This is not a marginal proof text; it is the final eschatological worship scene of the entire Bible. Verses: Rev 5:8-14, Rev 5:12, Rev 5:13, Rev 4:11 Strong's: G1391, G4352, G5092

    12. Hebrews 1 — The Son's Superiority Argument and OT Application: Hebrews 1 is the densest christological chapter in the NT, making multiple deity claims in rapid succession. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5481-charakter.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G5287-hupostasis.md for the key terms in Heb 1:3 ('express image of his person'). In 1:3, Christ is the 'brightness of his glory' and 'express image of his person' — charaktēr (exact imprint) of hypostasis (substance/being). In 1:8-9, Psa 45:6-7 is applied: 'Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever' — the Father calls the Son 'God.' Verses: Heb 1:2-3, Heb 1:6, Heb 1:8-9, Heb 1:10-12 Strong's: G5481, G5287, G2316, G4352
