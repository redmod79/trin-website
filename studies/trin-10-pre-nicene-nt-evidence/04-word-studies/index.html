    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    Does the New Testament itself — written 200+ years before the Council of Nicaea — teach the deity of Christ, or was this a 4th-century invention? Survey the Christological claims across the earliest NT documents and their dating.

    G2962 — kyrios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2962-kyrios.md

    Occurrences: 748

    kyrios (Lord) — 748 occurrences in 687 NT verses across 25 books. The LXX rendering of YHWH (~7,000 times). Applied to Jesus ~200 times in Paul alone. When Paul calls Jesus 'Lord' (kyrios) and applies OT YHWH-texts to him, the word carries the full weight of the divine name. The Christological title 'Lord' is not merely honorific — it is the divine name transferred.

    Semantic categories: divine authority/sovereignty, YHWH-identification via LXX, Christological title, earthly master (minor sense)

    Key verses: 1 Cor 8:6, Phil 2:11, Rom 10:13, 1 Thess 1:10

    Relevance: Central to the YHWH-text transfer argument. Every time Paul applies a YHWH-text to 'the Lord' (kyrios) Jesus, he is placing Jesus in YHWH's identity position. The 748 NT occurrences and LXX YHWH-mapping make kyrios the primary vehicle for pre-Nicene deity claims.

    G2316 — theos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2316-theos.md

    Occurrences: 1343

    theos (God) — 1,343 tokens in 1,172 NT verses across all 27 books. Directly applied to Christ in multiple passages: John 1:1 (theos en ho logos), John 20:28 (ho theos mou), Titus 2:13 (Granville Sharp), 2 Peter 1:1 (Granville Sharp), Rom 9:5 (God over all), Heb 1:8 (Father addresses Son as theos), 1 John 5:20 (houtos = the true God).

    Semantic categories: the Supreme Deity, deity predication applied to Christ, false gods (rare)

    Key verses: John 1:1, John 20:28, Titus 2:13, Heb 1:8, Rom 9:5

    Relevance: Direct theos-predication of Christ spans multiple independent authors (John, Paul, Peter, Hebrews) across 40+ years (55-95 AD), all predating Nicaea by 200+ years. The explicit application of theos to Christ is not a 4th-century invention.

    G3444 — morphe¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3444-morphe.md

    Occurrences: 3

    morphe (form) — 3 NT occurrences. Denotes the outward expression corresponding to inner essence — not mere appearance (which would be schema). In Phil 2:6, 'the form of God' = the visible manifestation proper to deity; in 2:7, 'the form of a servant' = the manifestation proper to servanthood. Christ did not exchange one morphe for another but ADDED servant-form while continuing to exist (hyparchon, present ptcp) in God-form.

    Semantic categories: essential nature manifested outwardly, Christological pre-existence

    Key verses: Phil 2:6, Phil 2:7

    Relevance: morphe theou establishes Christ's pre-incarnate divine nature in the earliest recoverable Christian hymn (30s-40s AD). The word denotes essential nature, not surface appearance — Christ genuinely possessed deity.

    G725 — harpagmos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G725-harpagmos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    harpagmos (robbery/thing grasped) — hapax legomenon in Phil 2:6. The res rapta reading is favored by the participial clause hyparchon (pre-existing state): Christ already possessed equality with God and did not regard it as something to be exploited. The -mos suffix denotes act/process, but the idiom harpagmon hegeisthai means 'to regard as something to exploit for advantage.'

    Semantic categories: res rapta (thing already possessed), res rapienda (alternative), kenosis theology

    Key verses: Phil 2:6

    Relevance: Establishes that equality with God was Christ's inherent possession, not something he aspired to. This is critical for dating: the pre-Pauline hymn already presupposes pre-existence and divine equality.

    G2758 — kenoo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2758-kenóō.md

    Occurrences: 7

    kenoo (empty/make of no reputation) — 7 occurrences. In Phil 2:7, the self-emptying is defined by the participles: 'taking the form of a servant, being made in the likeness of men.' The kenosis was not subtraction of divine nature but addition of human nature.

    Semantic categories: self-abasement by taking servant form, rendering ineffective/void

    Key verses: Phil 2:7

    Relevance: The kenosis explains the counter-evidence texts (John 14:28, Mark 13:32): during incarnation, Christ voluntarily limited the exercise of certain divine attributes without ceasing to possess divine nature.

    G2320 — theotes¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2320-theotes.md

    Occurrences: 1

    theotes (deity/Godhead) — hapax legomenon in Col 2:9. Derived directly from theos; denotes the very essence, nature, and being of God — not merely divine qualities (which would be theiotes G2305). Paul asserts that the FULL essence of deity (pan to pleroma tes theotetos) dwells in Christ bodily (somatikos).

    Semantic categories: essential deity — the being of God, distinction from theiotes (divine quality)

    Key verses: Col 2:9

    Relevance: The theotes/theiotes distinction is decisive. Paul chose theotes (deity itself) over theiotes (divine quality) to assert that Christ possesses not just divine attributes but the full essence of Godhood. Written c. 60 AD — 265 years before Nicaea.

    G2305 — theiotes¶

    Occurrences: 1

    theiotes (divinity/divine quality) — 1 occurrence in Rom 1:20. From theios (divine, G2304), denoting divine qualities or attributes visible in creation. Distinguished from theotes (G2320, deity/Godhood): theiotes = 'divinity' as a quality; theotes = 'deity' as essence (Trench, Synonyms).

    Semantic categories: divine quality observable in creation, contrast with theotes

    Key verses: Rom 1:20

    Relevance: Establishes the lexical contrast with theotes. What creation reveals (theiotes = divine qualities) is not the same as what Christ embodies (theotes = deity itself). Paul deliberately chose the stronger word for Christ.

    G3056 — logos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3056-logos.md

    Occurrences: 329

    logos (Word) — 329 tokens in 314 NT verses across 24 books. In the Johannine Prologue (John 1:1-18), ho Logos is the pre-existent divine Word who was with God and was God — the vehicle for John's incarnation theology. The Logos was the agent of all creation (John 1:3) and became flesh (John 1:14).

    Semantic categories: the Divine Expression (Johannine Prologue), speech/word, reason/account

    Key verses: John 1:1, John 1:14, Rev 19:13

    Relevance: The Logos Christology of John 1:1-18 is the most explicit deity declaration in the NT. Written c. 90 AD — still 235 years before Nicaea.

    G541 — apaugasma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G541-apaugasma.md

    Occurrences: 1

    apaugasma (radiance/brightness) — hapax in Heb 1:3. From apo + auge (brightness); the Son is the radiance that streams forth from the Father's glory. Active sense (effulgence) favored over passive (reflected light). Implies ontological unity: radiance is inseparable from and coeternal with its light source.

    Semantic categories: radiance from a source, Christological identity

    Key verses: Heb 1:3

    Relevance: The radiance metaphor implies coeternity — as long as the source shines, its radiance exists. The Son is not a secondary or derived light but the very outshining of divine glory.

    G5481 — charakter¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5481-charaktēr.md

    Occurrences: 1

    charakter (exact representation/express image) — hapax in Heb 1:3. From charasso (to engrave); denotes exact reproduction — the precise imprint of God's hypostasis. Stronger than eikon (image): charakter implies point-for-point correspondence between the original and the impression.

    Semantic categories: exact representation of divine being

    Key verses: Heb 1:3

    Relevance: Paired with apaugasma in Heb 1:3 to assert that the Son is both the radiance of God's glory AND the exact stamp of God's being — two hapax terms asserting the Son's full correspondence to the Father.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    proskyneo (worship) — 65 tokens in 54 NT verses. The NT presents a binary: creatures refuse proskyneo (Peter Acts 10:26, Paul Acts 14:15, angel Rev 19:10, 22:8-9), while Christ accepts it (Matt 14:33, 28:9, John 9:38, Heb 1:6, Rev 5:8-14). Zero exceptions in either direction.

    Semantic categories: worship directed at God/Christ, worship refused by creatures

    Key verses: Rev 5:13-14, Heb 1:6, Matt 14:33, John 9:38, Rev 19:10, Rev 22:8-9

    Relevance: The accept/refuse binary is structurally decisive. Christ consistently appears in the God-worthy category. Heb 1:6 is especially significant: the Father COMMANDS angels to worship the Son.

    G1510 — eimi¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1510-eimi.md

    Occurrences: 155

    eimi (I am) — 155 tokens under G1510 specifically; ~2,457 total morphological instances. The absolute ego eimi construction (without predicate) on Jesus's lips parallels the LXX rendering of YHWH's self-identification in Isa 43:10, 43:25, 48:12 and the divine name revelation of Exo 3:14.

    Semantic categories: existential statement, copulative verb, absolute divine self-identification (ego eimi)

    Key verses: John 8:58, John 8:24, John 13:19, John 18:5-6, Mark 14:62

    Relevance: The absolute ego eimi sayings constitute a YHWH-voice register — Jesus uses the exact formula by which YHWH identifies himself in the OT. The stoning response (John 8:59) and prostration response (John 18:6) confirm the divine-name claim was recognized.

    G1520 — heis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1520-heîs.md

    Occurrences: 353

    heis (one) — 353 occurrences. The Greek numeral used in 1 Cor 8:6 for the Shema redistribution: heis theos (one God, the Father) and heis kyrios (one Lord, Jesus Christ). Also in John 10:30: ego kai ho pater hen esmen ('I and the Father are one' — hen neuter = one essence).

    Semantic categories: numeral, Shema unity, ontological oneness

    Key verses: 1 Cor 8:6, John 10:30, Deut 6:4

    Relevance: The Shema's heis/echad is the monotheistic confession Paul reformulates to include Christ. The fact that Paul places Christ in the Shema's divine-identity statement (1 Cor 8:6, c. 55 AD) is the earliest datable evidence for Christ's inclusion in the Godhead.

    G3439 — monogenes¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3439-monogenḗs.md

    Occurrences: 9

    monogenes (only-begotten/unique) — 9 occurrences. Denotes uniqueness, not createdness. Applied to Christ in John 1:14, 1:18, 3:16, 3:18, 1 John 4:9 to express the Son's one-of-a-kind relationship with the Father. Isaac is called monogenes (Heb 11:17) though Abraham had other sons — the word means 'unique,' not 'only child born.'

    Semantic categories: unique divine sonship (Christological), only child (Synoptic), typological

    Key verses: John 1:14, John 1:18, John 3:16

    Relevance: monogenes establishes Christ's unique, unrepeatable relationship to the Father — not 'first-created' but 'one-of-a-kind Son.'

    G4416 — prototokos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4416-prototokos.md

    Occurrences: 9

    prototokos (firstborn) — 9 occurrences. A sovereignty title, not a createdness marker. Col 1:15's 'firstborn of all creation' is followed by 'for by him ALL things were created' (1:16) — a partitive reading is self-contradictory. Parallels Psalm 89:27 where David is called 'firstborn' though he was Jesse's youngest son.

    Semantic categories: Christological preeminence over creation, firstborn from the dead, literal firstborn

    Key verses: Col 1:15, Col 1:18, Rev 1:5

    Relevance: Addresses the objection that 'firstborn of creation' means Christ was created. The context (Col 1:16-17) and OT parallels (Psa 89:27) establish this as a rank/sovereignty title.

    G4138 — pleroma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4138-pleroma.md

    Occurrences: 17

    pleroma (fullness) — 17 occurrences. In Col 1:19 and 2:9, the 'fullness' that dwells in Christ is the totality of God's being — specified in 2:9 as theotes (deity). The divine pleroma is not merely an attribute Christ possesses but the sum total of deity permanently inhabiting him.

    Semantic categories: divine fullness dwelling in Christ, temporal completion, ethnic/salvific completion

    Key verses: Col 1:19, Col 2:9, Eph 1:23

    Relevance: The pleroma + theotes combination in Col 2:9 is the most ontologically explicit deity claim in Paul's letters.

    G2730 — katoikeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2730-katoikeo.md

    Occurrences: 48

    katoikeo (dwell permanently) — 48 tokens in 43 NT verses. The kata- prefix intensifies oikeo (to dwell), producing 'settled, permanent residence.' In Col 2:9, the present tense katoikei indicates that deity CONTINUES to dwell in Christ permanently — not a temporary visitation.

    Semantic categories: permanent dwelling, divine permanent indwelling in Christ

    Key verses: Col 2:9, Eph 3:17

    Relevance: The present tense in Col 2:9 (katoikei) means the fullness of deity is not a past event or temporary state but an ongoing, permanent reality in Christ.

    G228 — alethinos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G228-alethinos.md

    Occurrences: 27

    alethinos (true/genuine) — 27 tokens in 25 NT verses. Heavily concentrated in Johannine literature (80%). Used for 'the only true God' (John 17:3) applied to the Father, AND 'the true God' (1 John 5:20) applied to Christ. The term contrasts genuine with false/counterfeit, not Father with Son.

    Semantic categories: genuine/authentic deity, true vs. false gods

    Key verses: John 17:3, 1 John 5:20

    Relevance: The same author (John) applies alethinos theos to both Father (17:3) and Son (1 John 5:20), demonstrating that 'true God' language does not exclude Christ from deity.

    G4990 — soter¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4990-sōtḗr.md

    Occurrences: 24

    soter (Savior) — 24 occurrences. Applied to both God the Father and Jesus Christ. In the OT, YHWH alone is Savior (Isa 43:11 'beside me there is no savior'). The Christological usage identifies Jesus with the saving function the OT reserves exclusively for YHWH. Granville Sharp texts (Titus 2:13, 2 Peter 1:1) unite theos and soter in one referent.

    Semantic categories: God the Father as Savior, Jesus Christ as Savior

    Key verses: Luke 2:11, Titus 2:13, 2 Peter 1:1

    Relevance: If YHWH alone is Savior (Isa 43:11) and Jesus is called Savior, this constitutes an implicit deity claim. The Granville Sharp constructions make it explicit.

    G2470 — isos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2470-ísos.md

    Occurrences: 7

    isos (equal) — 7 occurrences. In John 5:18, Jesus's opponents understand his claims as 'making himself equal (ison) with God' — isos asserts sameness of nature/status. In Phil 2:6, Christ existed in a condition of 'equality with God' (isa theo).

    Semantic categories: ontological equality (divine nature), quantitative equivalence

    Key verses: John 5:18, Phil 2:6

    Relevance: isos establishes ontological equality between Christ and God — not merely functional similarity but sameness of nature.

    G5293 — hupotasso¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5293-hupotasso.md

    Occurrences: 49

    hupotasso (submit/subject) — 49 occurrences. In 1 Cor 15:28, the Son's future subjection to the Father is an eschatological action within the economy of redemption, not an ontological statement. The same Paul who writes this also writes the Shema redistribution (1 Cor 8:6) and YHWH-text transfers.

    Semantic categories: voluntary submission, eschatological arrangement

    Key verses: 1 Cor 15:28

    Relevance: Addresses the subordination counter-evidence. Functional subordination within the divine economy is compatible with ontological deity.

    G2578 — kampto¶

    Occurrences: 4

    kampto (bend/bow) — 4 NT occurrences: Rom 11:4, 14:11, Eph 3:14, Phil 2:10. From Isa 45:23 (LXX) where YHWH declares that every knee shall bow to him. Paul applies this to Christ in Phil 2:10 (en to onomati Iesou pan gony kampse) and Rom 14:11.

    Semantic categories: bowing the knee in worship, YHWH-text transfer vocabulary

    Key verses: Phil 2:10, Rom 14:11

    Relevance: kampto connects the Isa 45:23 YHWH-text directly to the Phil 2:10-11 and Rom 14:11 Christ-applications. Every knee bowing was YHWH's exclusive claim — now applied to Jesus.

    G5251 — hyperupsoo¶

    Occurrences: 1

    hyperupsoo (highly exalt / super-exalt) — hapax legomenon in Phil 2:9. The hyper- prefix intensifies hupsoo (to exalt), producing 'to exalt to the highest degree.' God 'super-exalted' Christ and gave him 'the name above every name' — a unique, superlative exaltation.

    Semantic categories: supreme exaltation, the name above every name

    Key verses: Phil 2:9

    Relevance: The hapax hyperupsoo conveys a unique, unprecedented level of exaltation. The 'name above every name' is widely understood as YHWH's own name — the name transferred to Christ.

    H259 — echad¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H259-echad.md

    Occurrences: 980

    echad (one/united) — 980 tokens in 739 OT verses. The Shema's echad in Deut 6:4 ('YHWH our God is one YHWH') is the monotheistic confession that Paul reformulates in 1 Cor 8:6. Strong's glosses the core sense as 'properly, united.' The word can denote composite unity (Gen 2:24, 'one flesh') as well as absolute singularity.

    Semantic categories: numeral (one), composite unity, Shema monotheistic confession

    Key verses: Deut 6:4, Gen 2:24

    Relevance: The Shema's echad is the background for Paul's heis redistribution in 1 Cor 8:6. Christ is placed within the 'one' of Israel's monotheistic confession.

    H3068 — YHWH¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3068-yhwh.md

    Occurrences: 6534

    YHWH (the LORD) — 6,534 OT tokens in 5,520 verses across 36 books. The divine name derived from hayah (to be). The LXX renders it as kyrios, which becomes the primary title for Christ in the NT. Paul's systematic transfer of YHWH-texts to Jesus (Rom 10:13/Joel 2:32, Rom 14:11/Isa 45:23, 1 Cor 1:31/Jer 9:24, 1 Cor 2:16/Isa 40:13) constitutes an identification of Jesus with YHWH.

    Semantic categories: the divine name, covenantal God of Israel, LXX kyrios rendering

    Key verses: Exo 3:14, Deut 6:4, Isa 44:6, Isa 45:23, Joel 2:32

    Relevance: The YHWH-to-kyrios-to-Jesus chain is the backbone of NT deity Christology. Paul takes passages where YHWH is the subject and applies them to Jesus without qualification — the earliest datable evidence of deity identification.
