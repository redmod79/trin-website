    Foxe on Servetus and the Genetic Fallacy¶

    Ref Score Content Summary
    FBOM 121.3 0.809 FULL TEXT: "It has long been the delight of both infidels and some professed Christians, when they wish to bring odium upon the opinions of Calvin, to refer to his agency in the death of Michael Servetus. This action is used on all occasions by those who have been unable to overthrow his opinions, as a conclusive argument against his whole system. 'Calvin burnt Servetus!—Calvin burnt Servetus!' is a good proof with a certain class of reasoners, that the doctrine of the Trinity is not true-that divine sovereignty is a fiction-that there is no such thing as an original, total depravity of the human character-and that it is a libel upon the divine character to say that God has, from all eternity, elected a certain number to be saved, while the rest of mankind are left to suffer eternal perdition."
    FBOM 121.4 0.661 "All the other reformers then living approved of Calvin's conduct. Even the gentle and amiable Melancthon"
    FBOM 121.5 0.754 Calvin: "I desire that the severity of the punishment should be remitted"
    FBOM 121.6 0.750 Calvin "himself once banished from Geneva by these very magistrates"
    FBOM 121.7 0.638 "Calvin acted contrary to the benignant spirit of the Gospel"
