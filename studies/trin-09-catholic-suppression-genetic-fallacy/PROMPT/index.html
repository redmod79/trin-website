    EGW/Pioneer Study: Catholic Suppression, Protestant Retention, and the Genetic Fallacy¶

    Question¶

    Investigate the historical record of the Roman Catholic Church's suppression of Bible translation, persecution of theological dissenters, and enforcement of doctrine through political power. Then examine: did the Protestant Reformers who broke from Rome independently retain the deity of Christ? Were anti-Trinitarians also persecuted by Protestants? Does institutional enforcement tell us whether a doctrine is true or false?

    Prior Research Summary (from 00-references.md)¶

    Six prior EGW/pioneer studies and seven prior Bible studies contain directly relevant findings:

    Key established facts from prior studies: 1. pioneer-trinity-development: SDA pioneers were explicitly anti-Trinitarian on creedal grounds (associated Trinity with Roman Catholic creedalism). EGW's shift to Trinitarianism was driven by publications (PP 1890, DA 1898, SpTB 7 1905), NOT by institutional pressure. Uriah Smith died in 1903 still semi-Arian and was never disciplined. The 1931 Fundamental Beliefs adopted Trinitarian articles 16 years after EGW's death -- persuasion, not enforcement. 2. 1260-year-papal-activities: EGW identifies six categories of papal activity during 538-1798: blasphemous claims, systematic persecution ("instruments of torture to compel assent" GC 569.2), suppression of Scripture ("sought to hide from the people the word of truth" GC 267.2), attempted law-change, universal temporal authority, and wilderness hiding of the true church. Council of Toulouse 1229 prohibited laity from possessing Scripture. Casualties estimated at 50-100 million (OFH1 116.3). 3. luther-prophecy-evolution: Luther's justification-by-faith breakthrough PRECEDED and ENABLED his prophetic identification of the papacy. The Reformation consensus was "virtually unanimous" that the papacy is "the predicted Antichrist" (PFF2 529.2) -- yet these same Reformers retained the deity of Christ. Luther broke from Rome on soteriology, ecclesiology, Mariology, purgatory, indulgences, papal authority while retaining Christ's deity. 4. christ-preexistence-authority: EGW's mature theology consistently affirms Christ's inherent, eternal, underived deity ("In Christ is life, original, unborrowed, underived" DA 530.3). Her Christological vocabulary DEVELOPED over time -- early hierarchical language gave way to explicit co-equality. 5. dispensationalism-origin-acceptance: Documents a proven Jesuit-to-Protestant transmission chain (Ribera -> Lacunza -> Irving -> Darby -> Scofield). PFF4 1227.2: "Protestant Futurism is the direct descendant -- doubtless unwittingly -- of the Jesuit Futurist counterinterpretation." This cuts BOTH ways on the genetic fallacy: transmission from Rome can happen, but origin does not determine truth value. 6. trin-08-monotheism-deity-synthesis (Bible study): Seven independent lines of evidence across four NT authors converge on including Christ within YHWH's identity -- the BIBLICAL evidence the deity of Christ rests on, regardless of who historically enforced or suppressed the doctrine.

    External corpus leads (Secrets Unsealed): - Bohr's list of doctrines Protestantism retained from Rome includes Sunday, eternal hell, immortal soul -- but notably does NOT include the Trinity (TPP 55). Verify whether EGW/pioneers explicitly name the Trinity as a Roman inheritance or treat it differently. - Thomas Aquinas quotation on state-enforced heresy execution (PRS Lesson 1 p. 36; GGPC Lesson 2 p. 20). Verify EGW/pioneer references to Aquinas. - Catholic admission that Protestant Sunday-keeping is "homage they pay, in spite of themselves, to the authority of the Church" (RSS Lesson 18 p. 370, citing Monsignor Segur 1868).

    13. Protestant persecution of anti-Trinitarians (Servetus, Legate, Wightman)

    17. Council of Nicaea -- what it actually decided and what the sources say

    18. WHAT: Document what EGW/pioneers (especially Froom, Schaff, and the historical sources) say about the Council of Nicaea (325 AD). What did it decide? Was it merely a political imposition by Constantine?
    19. WHY: Test search found PFF1 367.1 (Froom: Nicaea "the outstanding event of the fourth century," 318 bishops, convened by Constantine who "was present in person"), PFF1 387.3 (Constantine "did adhere to the authority of Scripture" and "Scripture was accepted as the authority by which both parties measured"), SCHAFF 14029 ("its bloodless intellectual victory over a dangerous error is of far greater consequence... than all the bloody victories of Constantine"), HODGKIN 254/5768 (Constantine at Nicaea). Key historical nuance: Nicaea was convened BY an emperor but the decision was based on SCRIPTURE debates between bishops.

    20. HOW: Search "Nicaea council bishops creed Constantine Arius Athanasius" --top 15. Search --book PFF1 --top 10 for "Nicaea council." Search --book SCHAFF --top 15 for "Nicene creed Arius." Search for whether any EGW/pioneer source claims Nicaea was purely a political imposition.

    21. EGW's specific list of Roman errors retained by Protestantism -- is the Trinity on it?

    25. The SDA pioneer anti-Trinitarian-to-Trinitarian transition as counter-example

    26. WHAT: The SDA denomination shifted from anti-Trinitarianism to Trinitarianism between 1888-1931 WITHOUT enforcement, persecution, or coercion. Anti-Trinitarians (Smith) were never disciplined. The transition was driven by EGW's publications and Bible study.
    27. WHY: pioneer-trinity-development study established this comprehensively.     29. The genetic fallacy and argumentum ad verecundiam -- logical analysis

    30. WHAT: The argument "the Catholic Church enforced the Trinity, therefore the Trinity is false" commits the genetic fallacy (judging truth by origin rather than evidence). The argument "Newton and Jefferson rejected the Trinity, therefore it is false" commits argumentum ad verecundiam (appeal to irrelevant authority). Document what EGW/pioneer sources say that bears on these logical errors.
    31. WHY: FBOM 121.3 is the single most important hit: Foxe EXPLICITLY identifies and rejects this reasoning: "'Calvin burnt Servetus!' is a good proof with a certain class of reasoners, that the doctrine of the Trinity is not true." Foxe calls this argument's users "infidels and some professed Christians" who "have been unable to overthrow [Calvin's] opinions" and use the burning "as a conclusive argument against his whole system." SCHAFF 21549 notes Unitarianism grew in Calvinistic churches -- showing anti-Trinitarianism has its own institutional history. PFF4 117.2 (Priestley: "humanitarian" Unitarian who "disbelieved the divinity as well as the deity of Christ"). SCHAFF 14089 (Milton and Newton "cannot properly be termed Arians" but had views "akin to that of Arius" -- their positions were neither pure nor authoritative on theology).

    32. HOW: Search "truth determined by evidence not origin who taught it" --top 10. Search "Bible test doctrine not authority men tradition" --top 10. Retrieve and document FBOM 121.3-121.5 in full (Foxe's complete treatment of the Servetus burning and the genetic fallacy). Note the dispensationalism study's parallel: PFF4 1227.2 shows genetic fallacy cuts both ways.

    33. The distinction between Catholic corruption and doctrinal truth-value

    34. WHAT: EGW and the pioneers distinguish between (a) the Catholic SYSTEM being corrupt and (b) whether every doctrine the Catholic Church held is automatically false. The fact that Rome held to the Trinity does not make the Trinity false, just as the fact that Rome held to Christ's resurrection does not make the resurrection false.
    35. WHY: GC 148.4 ("The Reformation did not end with Luther... new light has been continually shining upon the Scriptures") shows EGW's view that the Reformation was about CONTINUING to recover truth, not about rejecting everything Rome held. GC 524.2 (EGW calls denial of deity of Christ a "dangerous error" -- she would not call it "dangerous" if she thought it was merely a Roman imposition). FABER 1464 (reformers "did not scruple to derive from [Rome] their line of episcopal and sacerdotal ordination" -- they distinguished institutional corruption from institutional nothingness). GUINNESS2 878 (Reformers' convictions "largely derived from their study and interpretation of the prophetic Scriptures" -- evidence-driven, not reaction-driven).

    36. HOW: Search "Rome errors truth mixed corruption some truth" --top 10. Search "Reformation continued new light truth unfolding" --book GC --top 10. Search "dangerous error denies deity Christ" --book GC --top 5. Document the principle that EGW evaluates doctrines by SCRIPTURE, not by who held them.

      • WHY: SCHAFF 14089 found: "John Milton and Isaac Newton cannot properly be termed Arians. Their view of the relation of the Son to the Father was akin to that of Arius, but their spirit and their system of ideas were totally different." PFF4 117.2 (Priestley: "humanitarian Unitarian... disbelieved the divinity as well as the deity of Christ" but "acknowledged miracles... and accepted the validity of prophecy" -- showing even prominent Unitarians mixed valid and invalid positions). SCHAFF 21549 (Unitarianism arose in Calvinistic churches, not from independent Bible study).
      • HOW: Search "Newton Milton Arian view Trinity" --top 10. Search "Priestley Unitarian Christ divinity" --book PFF4 --top 10. Search "intellectual authority reason replace Scripture" --top 10.
