# The Trinity Re-evaluated: A Biblical Investigation

A 10-study series examining whether the Bible teaches that Jesus Christ is God, that the Holy Spirit is a personal agent, and that these truths are consistent with Old Testament monotheism. Each study uses original-language word studies, Greek and Hebrew grammar analysis, cross-testament parallels, and contextual exegesis to let the text speak for itself.

---

## Origin

This series was prompted by a forum debate raising common anti-Trinity arguments: that the Trinity was a 4th-century political invention, that key Bible verses show Jesus is subordinate to or separate from God, that the ransom sacrifice requires Jesus to be merely human, and that the Holy Spirit is an impersonal force. Rather than trading proof-texts, we investigated each claim systematically through the biblical evidence.

## How to Read This Series

**Start with Study 07** if you want the strongest single argument. It shows that removing Jesus' deity creates six simultaneous contradictions within the Bible's own framework — worship, forgiveness, creation, savior exclusivity, prayer, and atonement all break.

**Start with Study 01** if you want to work through the disputed verses in order.

**Start with Study 09** if the historical arguments (Nicaea, Catholic persecution, Newton/Jefferson) are your primary concern.

Each study has a **plain-English summary** (the default page) and a **full technical conclusion** with Greek/Hebrew analysis, word studies, and verse-by-verse examination for those who want to go deeper.

## Methodology

Every study follows the same pipeline:

1. **Web research** for scholarly commentary and peer-reviewed analysis
2. **Prior study search** across 640+ existing biblical studies in the library
3. **Tool-driven scoping** using Nave's Topical Dictionary, Strong's Concordance, and semantic search
4. **Word study generation** for every critical Hebrew/Greek term (30+ new word studies created for this series)
5. **Research** gathering all verse text, Greek/Hebrew parsing, cross-testament parallels, and contextual analysis
6. **Structured analysis** with every claim cross-checked against raw tool output by a Python validation script

No claim in these studies relies on theological tradition or training knowledge. Every assertion traces back to a specific verse, a specific Greek or Hebrew word, or a specific grammatical construction verified by parsing tools.
