# Bible Study: When Jesus says 'Why do you call me good? No one is good except God alone' (Mark 10:18) and 'I ascend to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God' (John 20:17), is he denying his own deity?

## Question
When Jesus says 'Why do you call me good? No one is good except God alone' (Mark 10:18) and 'I ascend to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God' (John 20:17), is he denying his own deity?

## Prior Research Summary
Six prior studies found with directly relevant findings: trin-01 (firstborn/created being — titles that appear to diminish Jesus are actually preeminence claims), trin-02 (subordination — incarnational/kenotic framework explains 'lesser' language), trin-03 (only true God — monos/heis theos does NOT exclude Jesus; Shema distribution in 1 Cor 8:6), trin-04 (ransom — incarnation mechanism explains how divine Son genuinely uses 'my God'), jesus-never-said-i-am-god (Mark 10:18 as Socratic probe; Mark 2:7 parallel), cry-of-dereliction (Psalm 22 'my God' cry reflects incarnational relationship). Web research found scholarly consensus reading Mark 10:18 as implicit deity claim (Hampton/Gundry, Gathercole, Bratcher/Nida, Lyons, BibleHub commentators); Calvin dissents (doctrinal authority reading). EGW reads Mark 10:18 as refusing casual flattery while asserting equality with Father.
