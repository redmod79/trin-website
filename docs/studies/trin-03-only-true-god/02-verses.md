# Verses

## Question
When Jesus says 'the only true God' (John 17:3) and Paul says 'one God, the Father' (1 Corinthians 8:6), do these passages exclude Jesus from deity? Examine the full chapter context of John 17 and the Shema background of 1 Corinthians 8:6.

### The High Priestly Prayer — 'the only true God' and pre-existent glory

> John 17:1	These words spake Jesus, and lifted up his eyes to heaven, and said, Father, the hour is come; glorify thy Son, that thy Son also may glorify thee:
John 17:2	As thou hast given him power over all flesh, that he should give eternal life to as many as thou hast given him.
John 17:3	And this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent.
John 17:4	I have glorified thee on the earth: I have finished the work which thou gavest me to do.
John 17:5	And now, O Father, glorify thou me with thine own self with the glory which I had with thee before the world was.

**Context:** Jesus's prayer to the Father on the night before his crucifixion (the High Priestly Prayer). John 17 is the most sustained prayer of Jesus in the Gospels. The chapter context includes deity claims (v.5 pre-existent glory, v.10 mutual ownership, v.24 pre-creation love).

**Notes:** V.3: monos alethinos theos — the crux text. The kai conjunction connects 'the only true God' with 'Jesus Christ whom thou hast sent' as joint objects of life-giving knowledge (both in accusative). V.5: eichon (imperfect active indicative 1st person — continuous past possession) + para soi (with you, dative of personal proximity) — Jesus claims he personally and continuously possessed glory alongside the Father before the world existed. The pre-verbal para seatō (with yourself) is an aorist imperative request for restoration of shared divine presence.

### Divine unity as the model for believer unity — 'that they may be one, as we are one'

> John 17:21	That they all may be one; as thou, Father, [art] in me, and I in thee, that they also may be one in us: that the world may believe that thou hast sent me.
John 17:22	And the glory which thou gavest me I have given them; that they may be one, even as we are one:
John 17:23	I in them, and thou in me, that they may be made perfect in one; and that the world may know that thou hast sent me, and hast loved them, as thou hast loved me.

**Context:** Jesus prays for the unity of all believers, using his own unity with the Father as the standard. The Father-Son unity is the archetype; believer unity is the derivative copy.

**Notes:** The unity formula: kathos su pater en emoi kago en soi (as you Father in me and I in you). The mutual indwelling (perichoresis) language parallels John 10:30 (hen esmen, we are one — neuter hen indicating unity of essence/purpose, not numerical identity). The fact that Father-Son unity is the MODEL for believer unity means believers approximate but do not achieve the ontological depth of divine unity.

### Pre-creation love and glory — 'thou lovedst me before the foundation of the world'

> John 17:24	Father, I will that they also, whom thou hast given me, be with me where I am; that they may behold my glory, which thou hast given me: for thou lovedst me before the foundation of the world.

**Context:** Jesus asserts a personal love-relationship with the Father that existed before creation (pro kataboles kosmou). This is not merely a divine plan — it is a personal, relational claim.

**Notes:** pro kataboles kosmou (before the foundation of the world) — this phrase appears also in Eph 1:4 and 1 Pet 1:20. Combined with John 17:5's pre-existent glory, this establishes that the 'only true God' of v.3 had a Son with whom he shared glory and love before any created thing existed.

### The Shema — OT background for 1 Corinthians 8:6

> Deuteronomy 6:4	Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God [is] one LORD:

**Context:** The foundational monotheistic confession of Israel. The Hebrew reads: shema yisrael YHWH elohenu YHWH echad. The LXX renders: kyrios ho theos hemon kyrios heis estin. Paul's 1 Cor 8:6 distributes heis between theos and kyrios.

### Equal honor — the Son does what the Father does, all must honor the Son as the Father

> John 5:17	But Jesus answered them, My Father worketh hitherto, and I work.
John 5:18	Therefore the Jews sought the more to kill him, because he not only had broken the sabbath, but said also that God was his Father, making himself equal with God.
John 5:19	Then answered Jesus and said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, The Son can do nothing of himself, but what he seeth the Father do: for what things soever he doeth, these also doeth the Son likewise.
John 5:20	For the Father loveth the Son, and sheweth him all things that himself doeth: and he will shew him greater works than these, that ye may marvel.
John 5:21	For as the Father raiseth up the dead, and quickeneth [them]; even so the Son quickeneth whom he will.
John 5:22	For the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son:
John 5:23	That all [men] should honour the Son, even as they honour the Father. He that honoureth not the Son honoureth not the Father which hath sent him.

**Context:** Jesus's response to persecution for healing on the Sabbath. The Jews understand his 'my Father' language as making himself equal with God (v.18). Jesus then claims divine prerogatives: life-giving (v.21), all judgment (v.22), and equal honor (v.23).

**Notes:** V.23 Greek: ἵνα πάντες τιμῶσι τὸν Υἱὸν καθὼς τιμῶσι τὸν Πατέρα — 'that all should honor the Son just as (kathos) they honor the Father.' The kathos (G2531) makes the honor quantitatively and qualitatively identical. This is incompatible with reading 17:3 as excluding the Son from deity — the same Gospel demands equal honor for the Son.

### 'I and my Father are one' — the Jews understand this as a deity claim

> John 10:30	I and [my] Father are one.
John 10:31	Then the Jews took up stones again to stone him.
John 10:32	Jesus answered them, Many good works have I shewed you from my Father; for which of those works do ye stone me?
John 10:33	The Jews answered him, saying, For a good work we stone thee not; but for blasphemy; and because that thou, being a man, makest thyself God.

**Context:** In the context of the Feast of Dedication. The Jews explicitly state the charge: 'thou, being a man, makest thyself God' (v.33). Jesus's hen esmen ('we are one,' neuter hen indicating unity of nature/action) triggers the blasphemy charge.

**Notes:** ego kai ho pater hen esmen — 'I and the Father one are.' hen (G1520 neuter) indicates unity of essence/purpose rather than personal identity (which would require the masculine heis). The stoning response (v.31) and blasphemy charge (v.33) confirm the Jews understood this as a deity claim. This is the same author who wrote 17:3.

> Philippians 2:10	That at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of [things] in heaven, and [things] in earth, and [things] under the earth;
Philippians 2:11	And [that] every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ [is] Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

**Context:** Application of Isaiah 45:23 (YHWH's exclusive oath) to Jesus.

**Notes:** The YHWH-exclusive 'every knee shall bow' oath of Isa 45:23 is applied to Jesus. If kyrios for Jesus derived only from Ps 110:1's adon (Perriman's argument), Paul would not apply a YHWH-oath text to him. This is the decisive counter to Perriman.

### 'Whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved' — Joel 2:32 applied to Jesus

> Romans 10:13	For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved.

**Context:** Paul quotes Joel 2:32, which reads 'whoever calls on the name of YHWH.' Paul applies it to Jesus (see v.9-12 context: 'if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus').

**Notes:** Joel 2:32: 'whoever calls on the name of YHWH shall be delivered.' Paul applies this to Jesus in the context of confessing 'Jesus is Lord' (v.9). This means Paul identifies Jesus with YHWH — the kyrios who saves is Jesus.

### 'Whosoever shall call on the name of the LORD shall be delivered'

> Joel 2:32	And it shall come to pass, [that] whosoever shall call on the name of the LORD shall be delivered: for in mount Zion and in Jerusalem shall be deliverance, as the LORD hath said, and in the remnant whom the LORD shall call.

**Context:** Joel's prophecy about calling on YHWH's name for salvation. Paul applies this to Jesus in Romans 10:13.

**Notes:** The Hebrew has YHWH (the divine name). The LXX renders kyrios. Paul applies this kyrios text to Jesus, confirming that the NT's kyrios for Jesus derives from YHWH, not merely from Psalm 110:1's adon.

### 'The Lord GOD, and his Spirit, hath sent me' — three agents in one clause

> Isaiah 48:16	Come ye near unto me, hear ye this; I have not spoken in secret from the beginning; from the time that it was, there [am] I: and now the Lord GOD, and his Spirit, hath sent me.

**Context:** A mysterious OT passage with three distinct agents: the speaker ('me'), 'the Lord GOD,' and 'his Spirit.'

**Notes:** Three distinct agents appear: (1) the speaker who has been present 'from the beginning,' (2) Adonai YHWH, and (3) his Spirit — all in one clause. This is OT evidence for plurality within YHWH, relevant to understanding how 'the only true God' can include the Son.

### 'Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever' — elohim addressed to the king

> Psalm 45:6	Thy throne, O God, [is] for ever and ever: the sceptre of thy kingdom [is] a right sceptre.
Psalm 45:7	Thou lovest righteousness, and hatest wickedness: therefore God, thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.

**Context:** A royal psalm. The king is addressed as elohim. Cited in Hebrews 1:8-9 as addressed to the Son.

**Notes:** The structure is remarkable: 'God, YOUR God, has anointed you' — the king is called God, yet has a God. Hebrews 1:8 applies this to the Son without qualification, confirming the Son is both called God and has God (the Father).

### 'The LORD rained from the LORD' — two YHWH references

> Genesis 19:24	Then the LORD rained upon Sodom and upon Gomorrah brimstone and fire from the LORD out of heaven;

**Context:** The destruction of Sodom. 'YHWH rained... from YHWH out of heaven' — two distinct YHWH references.

**Notes:** YHWH on earth rains fire from YHWH in heaven — suggesting two persons both bearing the name YHWH. This is OT evidence for plurality within YHWH.

### 'Isaiah saw his glory and spoke of him' — YHWH's glory attributed to Christ

> John 12:41	These things said Esaias, when he saw his glory, and spake of him.

**Context:** John attributes Isaiah's vision of YHWH's glory (Isaiah 6) to Christ. 'He saw HIS (Jesus's) glory.'

**Notes:** Isaiah 6:1-5 describes Isaiah seeing 'the Lord (Adonai) sitting upon a throne, high and lifted up' — a YHWH vision. John says Isaiah 'saw HIS (autou — referring to Jesus from the preceding context) glory.' John identifies the YHWH of Isaiah 6 with Jesus.

### 'The church of God, which he hath purchased with his own blood'

> Acts 20:28	Take heed therefore unto yourselves, and to all the flock, over the which the Holy Ghost hath made you overseers, to feed the church of God, which he hath purchased with his own blood.

**Context:** Paul's farewell to the Ephesian elders. 'The church of GOD, which HE purchased with HIS OWN blood' — God's blood, which can only be Christ's blood.

**Notes:** The phrase 'the church of God' (tou theou) with 'his own blood' (tou haimatos tou idiou) implies that God purchased the church with his own blood — which is Christ's act on the cross. This implicitly identifies Christ with God.

### Identical worship given to the Lamb and to the one on the throne

> Revelation 5:12	Saying with a loud voice, Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing.
Revelation 5:13	And every creature which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as are in the sea, and all that are in them, heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, [be] unto him that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever.
Revelation 5:14	And the four beasts said, Amen. And the four [and] twenty elders fell down and worshipped him that liveth for ever and ever.

**Context:** Heavenly worship scene. The Lamb receives the same sevenfold doxology as the one on the throne — and they share the worship.

**Notes:** V.13: 'unto him that sitteth upon the throne AND unto the Lamb' — the same worship formula. If worship belongs to God alone (Matt 4:10), and the Lamb receives identical worship alongside God, the Lamb shares in deity.

### 'The throne of God and of the Lamb' — shared throne

> Revelation 22:1	And he shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb.
Revelation 22:2	In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, [was there] the tree of life, which bare twelve [manner of] fruits, [and] yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree [were] for the healing of the nations.
Revelation 22:3	And there shall be no more curse: but the throne of God and of the Lamb shall be in it; and his servants shall serve him:

**Context:** New Jerusalem. God and the Lamb share a single throne — not two thrones. 'His servants shall serve HIM' (singular).

**Notes:** ho thronos tou theou kai tou arniou — 'the throne of God and of the Lamb' — one throne, two persons. The singular 'him' (auto) in 'his servants shall serve him' indicates God and the Lamb are worshipped as one.

### 'All power is given unto me' + trinitarian baptismal formula

> Matthew 28:18	And Jesus came and spake unto them, saying, All power is given unto me in heaven and in earth.
Matthew 28:19	Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost:

**Context:** The Great Commission. Jesus claims all authority and commands baptism in the singular 'name' (onoma) of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

**Notes:** eis to onoma (singular 'name') tou patros kai tou huiou kai tou hagiou pneumatos — one name shared by three persons. The singular 'name' covering Father, Son, and Spirit implies shared identity/authority.

### Trinitarian benediction — grace of Christ, love of God, communion of Spirit

> 2 Corinthians 13:14	The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Ghost, [be] with you all. Amen.

**Context:** Paul's closing benediction with a triadic formula naming Christ, God, and the Spirit in coordinate relationship.

**Notes:** Christ is named first, coordinate with God the Father and the Holy Spirit. This triadic structure parallels 1 Cor 12:4-6 and Matt 28:19.

### 'That Rock was Christ' — Paul identifies Christ with YHWH's OT presence

> 1 Corinthians 10:4	And did all drink the same spiritual drink: for they drank of that spiritual Rock that followed them: and that Rock was Christ.

**Context:** Paul identifies Christ with the supernatural Rock that provided water in the wilderness — an act attributed to YHWH in the OT (Exodus 17:6; Numbers 20:8-11).

**Notes:** Paul identifies the Rock (an OT YHWH manifestation) as Christ. This is the same Paul who wrote 'one God, the Father... one Lord, Jesus Christ' in 1 Cor 8:6. Paul clearly did not understand 8:6 as excluding Christ from YHWH.

### Trinitarian pattern — same Spirit, same Lord, same God

> 1 Corinthians 12:4	Now there are diversities of gifts, but the same Spirit.
1 Corinthians 12:5	And there are differences of administrations, but the same Lord.
1 Corinthians 12:6	And there are diversities of operations, but it is the same God which worketh all in all.

**Context:** Paul's discussion of spiritual gifts. The triadic formula — Spirit, Lord, God — parallels 1 Cor 8:6 and 2 Cor 13:14.

**Notes:** Spirit → Lord (Jesus) → God (Father) — three persons in coordinate relationship. The 'same' (auto) repeated for each indicates parity in the source of spiritual gifts.

### 'One Lord, one faith, one baptism, one God and Father of all'

> Ephesians 4:4	[There is] one body, and one Spirit, even as ye are called in one hope of your calling;
Ephesians 4:5	One Lord, one faith, one baptism,
Ephesians 4:6	One God and Father of all, who [is] above all, and through all, and in you all.

**Context:** Paul's unity confession in Ephesians. Echoes 1 Cor 8:6 with the triad: one Spirit, one Lord, one God.

**Notes:** The heis (one) is distributed across Spirit, Lord (Jesus), and God (Father) — the same pattern as 1 Cor 8:6 but expanded to include the Spirit explicitly.

### 'The Word was made flesh, and we beheld his glory'

> John 1:14	And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth.

**Context:** The incarnation of the divine Logos. The glory (doxa) witnessed is the glory of the monogenes — the unique one from the Father.

**Notes:** ho logos sarx egeneto — the Word 'became' (egeneto, aorist of ginomai) flesh. The same egeneto that describes creation in v.3 now describes incarnation. doxan hos monogenous para patros — 'glory as of the unique one from the Father.' The incarnation does not diminish the Word's deity (v.1) but reveals it in flesh.

### 'The only begotten Son/God, in the bosom of the Father'

> John 1:18	No man hath seen God at any time; the only begotten Son, which is in the bosom of the Father, he hath declared [him].

**Context:** The prologue's conclusion. The textual variant monogenes theos ('the only-begotten God') vs. monogenes huios ('the only-begotten Son') is significant for Christology.

**Notes:** The critical text reads monogenes theos ('the unique God') rather than monogenes huios ('the unique Son'). If the reading monogenes theos is original, then the prologue explicitly calls Jesus 'God' — matching the theos of 1:1c. The phrase eis ton kolpon tou patros ('into the bosom of the Father') indicates the most intimate personal communion.
