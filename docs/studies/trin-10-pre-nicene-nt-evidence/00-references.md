## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (Phase 0) found extensive scholarly material. Key findings:

### Scholarly Consensus on Dating
- **Larry Hurtado** (*Lord Jesus Christ*, 2003): "Exalted views of Jesus emerge well within the first decades of Christianity." Earliest Christianity practiced "functionally binitarian worship." Christ-devotion was a "big bang" — "explosively rapid" — adopted within months of the crucifixion.
- **Richard Bauckham** (*Jesus and the God of Israel*, 2008): "The highest possible Christology — the inclusion of Jesus in the unique divine identity — was central to the faith of the early church even before any of the New Testament writings were written, since it occurs in all of them."
- **Michael Kruger**: 1 Cor 8:6 and Phil 2:6-11 contain "earlier Christian tradition that significantly predates Paul's own letters" — meaning high Christology is earlier than the 50s AD.
- **Bart Ehrman** (agnostic critic): Even he states that "everyone at the council already fully believed Christ was God. The question was: in what sense?" He explicitly says Nicaea was "NOT when church Fathers decided that Jesus was divine."

### YHWH-Text Transfer (Hurtado, 2019)
Paul deliberately applied OT YHWH texts to Jesus:
- Rom 10:13 ← Joel 2:32 (calling on the name of YHWH)
- Rom 14:11 ← Isa 45:23 (every knee bows to YHWH)
- 1 Cor 1:31 ← Jer 9:23-24 (boasting in YHWH)
- 1 Cor 2:16 ← Isa 40:13 (the mind of YHWH)
- 1 Cor 10:26 ← Psa 24:1 (the earth is YHWH's)
This was "intentional and fueled by powerful convictions about Jesus' exalted status."

### Pre-Pauline Material
- **Phil 2:6-11** (Christ Hymn): Scholarly consensus holds this reflects pre-Pauline liturgical material. Three-stage Christology: pre-existence → incarnation → exaltation. Pushes high Christology to the 30s-40s AD.
- **1 Cor 8:6** (Christian Shema): Paul "divided the Shema between God and Christ." One of the earliest data points for christologically reformulated monotheism.
- **1 Cor 16:22** (Maranatha): Aramaic prayer "Our Lord, come" from earliest Palestinian Jewish-Christian community. Paul left untranslated — Gentile readers already knew it. Pushes Christ-devotion to the 30s-40s AD in Jerusalem.

### Explicit Deity Texts
- **John 20:28**: Thomas's "My Lord and my God" — "the highest Christological confession in any Gospel." Greek uses nominative with article (ho theos mou), direct address.
- **Romans 9:5**: "Christ, who is over all, God blessed for ever" — Metzger and Moo hold reading A (Christ = "God over all") makes best sense of Paul's syntax.
- **Titus 2:13**: Granville Sharp's rule makes "the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ" a single-referent identification.

### Extra-Biblical Evidence
- **Pliny the Younger** (c. 112 AD): Hostile Roman governor reports Christians "recited an antiphonal ode to Christ as to a god" — non-Christian testimony, 200+ years before Nicaea.
- **Ignatius of Antioch** (c. 108-117 AD): Repeatedly and casually calls Jesus "our God" as established confession, not novel teaching. Direct disciple of apostles.
- **Justin Martyr** (c. 155 AD): Christ "being the First-Begotten Word of God, IS EVEN GOD."

### NT Document Dating Framework
- Earliest: 1 Thessalonians c. 50 AD
- Paul's undisputed letters: 50s AD
- Synoptics: 65-85 AD
- John/Revelation: 90-100 AD
- Latest NT document: c. 120-130 AD
- Pre-Pauline traditions: 30s-40s AD
- Council of Nicaea: 325 AD
- Minimum gap: 195 years; maximum gap: 295 years
