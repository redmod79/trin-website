## Question
Does the New Testament itself — written 200+ years before the Council of Nicaea — teach the deity of Christ, or was this a 4th-century invention? Survey the Christological claims across the earliest NT documents and their dating.

## Prior Research Summary
Nine prior studies in the Trinity series (trin-01 through trin-08) plus jesus-never-said-i-am-god have extensively documented seven converging lines of deity evidence: Shema redistribution (1 Cor 8:6), YHWH-title transfer (Isa 45:23→Phil 2:10-11, Joel 2:32→Rom 10:13, etc.), creation within YHWH's 'alone' (Isa 44:24 + John 1:3/Col 1:16/Heb 1:2), worship co-reception across the creator-creature boundary, divine prerogative exercise, grammatically precise deity predication, and Spirit-YHWH identification. The capstone study (trin-08) synthesized these across four independent NT authors. THIS study (trin-10) adds the CHRONOLOGICAL dimension — mapping each Christological claim to its approximate composition date to demonstrate the entire NT predates Nicaea by 200+ years, and pre-Pauline traditions push evidence to within a decade of the crucifixion. Extensive web research (Phase 0) confirms scholarly consensus (Hurtado, Bauckham, Kruger, even Ehrman) that belief in Christ's deity was pre-Nicene. External corpus findings from EGW, Schaff, Froom, Gibbon, and Eusebius provide additional corroboration.
