# Cross-Testament Parallels

- John 14:16 → John 15:26 (nt, vocabulary + structural — both use parakletos + ekeinos + personal verb)
- John 14:16 → 1 John 2:1 (nt, vocabulary — Jesus as first parakletos, Spirit as allos parakletos)
- 1 Corinthians 12:11 → 1 Corinthians 12:6 (nt, structural — Spirit's energei/bouletai parallels God's energei ta panta)
- Romans 8:26 → Romans 8:34 (nt, structural — Spirit intercedes (v.26-27) as Christ intercedes (v.34))
- Acts 5:3-4 → Acts 5:9 (nt, structural — lying to Spirit = lying to God (v.3-4), tempting the Spirit (v.9))
- Hebrews 9:14 → Romans 16:26 (nt, vocabulary — aionios applied to both Spirit (Heb 9:14) and God (Rom 16:26))
- Matthew 28:19 → 2 Corinthians 13:14 (nt, structural — triadic formulas coordinating Father, Son, Spirit)
- Matthew 28:19 → 1 Peter 1:2 (nt, structural — triadic salutation coordinating all three persons)
- Isaiah 63:10 → Ephesians 4:30 (nt, vocabulary + thematic — OT atsab (grieve) parallels NT lupeo (grieve) applied to the Spirit)
- Isaiah 48:16 → John 14:26 (nt, thematic — OT three-agent sending parallels NT Father-sending-Spirit)
- Acts 28:25 → Isaiah 6:8-10 (ot, quotation — Paul attributes YHWH's speech in Isa 6 to the Holy Spirit)
- Hebrews 3:7 → Psalm 95:7-11 (ot, quotation — Hebrews attributes YHWH's Psalm 95 speech to 'the Holy Ghost saith')
- Hebrews 10:15 → Jeremiah 31:33-34 (ot, quotation — Hebrews attributes YHWH's new covenant promise to the Spirit's testimony)
- Revelation 13:12 → John 16:14 (nt, structural parody — false prophet directs worship to beast as Spirit glorifies Christ)
- Revelation 13:15 → John 6:63 (nt, structural parody — false prophet gives pneuma to image as Spirit gives life)
- Revelation 13:16 → Ephesians 4:30 (nt, structural parody — false prophet marks foreheads as Spirit seals believers)
- 1 Thessalonians 5:19 → Deuteronomy 4:24 (ot, thematic counter — 'quench' the Spirit (fire) parallels God as 'consuming fire' — both use fire imagery for personal beings)
- Acts 2:17 → Jeremiah 23:24 (ot, thematic counter — Spirit 'poured out' parallels God who 'fills heaven and earth' — both use filling/pervasion language for personal beings)
- 1 Corinthians 6:19 → Ephesians 3:17 (nt, thematic counter — Spirit 'dwells in' believers parallels Christ 'dwelling in hearts' — dwelling language for personal beings)
