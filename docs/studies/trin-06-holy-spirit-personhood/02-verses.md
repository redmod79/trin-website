# Verses

## Question
Is the Holy Spirit a personal agent who thinks, wills, speaks, and can be sinned against — or an impersonal divine power/force? Examine the Greek grammatical evidence, personal-action verbs attributed to the Spirit, sin-against-the-Spirit passages, and triadic formulas.

### Allos parakletos — another Comforter of the same kind

> And I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may abide with you for ever; [Even] the Spirit of truth; whom the world cannot receive, because it seeth him not, neither knoweth him: but ye know him; for he dwelleth with you, and shall be in you.

**Context:** Jesus' Farewell Discourse (John 14-16). Jesus is preparing the disciples for his departure and promising a replacement personal presence — 'another' (allos) Comforter of the same kind as himself.

**Notes:** Greek: allos (G243, Acc Sg M) parakletos (G3875, Acc Sg M) — 'another Comforter.' In v.17, pneuma (N-ASN neuter) is called 'the Spirit of truth' and the relative pronoun ho (R-ASN) agrees with the neuter pneuma. The pronoun auto (P-ASN neuter) in v.17 also agrees with pneuma grammatically. This is the verse where the allos/heteros distinction matters: allos = another of same kind (Jesus = first parakletos per 1 John 2:1).

---

### Ekeinos (masculine) for the Spirit — the grammatical debate pivot

> But the Comforter, [which is] the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance, whatsoever I have said unto you.

**Context:** Continuation of Farewell Discourse. Jesus identifies the Comforter as 'the Holy Ghost' and describes teaching and remembrance — cognitive personal verbs.

- Παράκλητος (G3875) — N-NSM — Nom Sg M | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN] Nom Sg N | Ἅγιον ἅγιος G40 [A-NSN] Nom Sg N | ὃ ὅς G3739 [R-ASN] Acc Sg N | πέμψει πέμπω G3992 [V-FAI-3S] | Πατὴρ πατήρ G3962 [N-NSM] | ὀνόματί ὄνομα G3686 [N-DSN] | ἐκεῖνος ἐκεῖνος G1565 [D-NSM] Nom Sg M | διδάξει διδάσκω G1321 [V-FAI-3S] | ὑπομνήσει ὑπομιμνῄσκω G5279 [V-FAI-3S]

**Notes:** CRITICAL GRAMMATICAL DATA: ho Parakletos (N-NSM, masculine) is in apposition with to Pneuma to Hagion (N-NSN/A-NSN, neuter). Then ekeinos (D-NSM, masculine nominative singular) is the subject of didaxei (V-FAI-3S, 'will teach') and hupomnesei (V-FAI-3S, 'will remind'). Wallace argues ekeinos tracks Parakletos (masculine) as antecedent. Clouzet argues it tracks Pneuma despite the gender mismatch. The relative pronoun ho (R-ASN, neuter) that introduces the Father-sending clause agrees with pneuma (neuter), showing John is aware of the grammatical gender — then switches to masculine ekeinos for the subject of the personal verbs.

### Spirit of truth testifies — ekeinos with martureo

> But when the Comforter is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, [even] the Spirit of truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me: And ye also shall bear witness, because ye have been with me from the beginning.

**Context:** Jesus explains the Spirit's testimonial role — proceeding from the Father and testifying of Christ — coordinated alongside apostolic witness.

- Παράκλητος (G3875) — N-NSM — Nom Sg M | ὃν ὅς G3739 [R-ASM] Acc Sg M | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN] Nom Sg N | ὃ ὅς G3739 [R-NSN] Nom Sg N | ἐκπορεύεται ἐκπορεύομαι G1607 [V-PNI-3S] | ἐκεῖνος ἐκεῖνος G1565 [D-NSM] Nom Sg M | μαρτυρήσει μαρτυρέω G3140 [V-FAI-3S]

**Notes:** Structure: ho Parakletos (N-NSM) ... to Pneuma tes aletheias (N-NSN) ho (R-NSN, neuter relative) ... ekeinos (D-NSM, masculine) marturese (V-FAI-3S 'will testify'). Again, the relative pronoun ho agreeing with neuter pneuma appears, then ekeinos (masculine) serves as the subject of the personal verb martureo. The Spirit is coordinated with the apostles ('ye also shall bear witness') — both personal witnesses.

### The Spirit convicts the world — judicial personal action

> Nevertheless I tell you the truth; It is expedient for you that I go away: for if I go not away, the Comforter will not come unto you; but if I depart, I will send him unto you. And when he is come, he will reprove the world of sin, and of righteousness, and of judgment: Of sin, because they believe not on me; Of righteousness, because I go to my Father, and ye see me no more; Of judgment, because the prince of this world is judged.

**Context:** Jesus explains why his departure is necessary — the Paraclete's coming requires it. The Spirit's work is judicial: convicting (elegcho) the world concerning three categories.

**Notes:** In v.7: ho Parakletos (N-NSM) is subject. pempso auton (P-ASM, 'I will send him') — masculine accusative pronoun for the Paraclete. In v.8: ekeinos (D-NSM) elegxei (V-FAI-3S, 'will convict') ton kosmon. The verb elegcho means to convict, reprove, expose — a judicial act requiring a personal prosecutor. Three categories: sin (unbelief), righteousness (Christ's vindication), judgment (Satan's condemnation).

### Six personal verbs with ekeinos — densest personhood cluster

> Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth: for he shall not speak of himself; but whatsoever he shall hear, [that] shall he speak: and he will shew you things to come. He shall glorify me: for he shall receive of mine, and shall shew [it] unto you. All things that the Father hath are mine: therefore said I, that he shall take of mine, and shall shew [it] unto you.

**Context:** The most verb-dense Spirit passage in the NT. Six distinct personal verbs attributed to the Spirit within three verses.

- ἐκεῖνος (G1565) — D-NSM — Nom Sg M | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN] Nom Sg N | ὁδηγήσει ὁδηγέω G3594 [V-FAI-3S] | λαλήσει λαλέω G2980 [V-FAI-3S] | ἑαυτοῦ ἑαυτοῦ G1438 [F-3GSM] Gen Sg M | ἀκούει ἀκούω G191 [V-PAI-3S] | ἀναγγελεῖ ἀναγγέλλω G312 [V-FAI-3S]

**Notes:** v.13: ekeinos (D-NSM) is subject for: hodegese (G3594, 'will guide'), lalesei (G2980, 'will speak'), akouei (G191, 'hears'), lalesei again ('will speak'), anangelei (G312, 'will declare'). v.14: ekeinos (D-NSM) again as subject: doxasei (G1392, 'will glorify'), lempsetai (G2983, Fut Mid 'will receive'), anangelei ('will declare'). v.15: lambanei (G2983, 'receives'), anangelei ('will declare'). heautou (F-3GSM, masculine reflexive) in 'aph heautou' = 'from himself.' The reflexive pronoun is masculine, tracking the masculine ekeinos. Total personal verbs: guide, speak, hear, declare, glorify, receive — all requiring a personal subject.

### Lying to the Spirit = lying to God — direct deity identification

> But Peter said, Ananias, why hath Satan filled thine heart to lie to the Holy Ghost, and to keep back [part] of the price of the land? Whiles it remained, was it not thine own? and after it was sold, was it not in thine own power? why hast thou conceived this thing in thine heart? thou hast not lied unto men, but unto God.

**Context:** Ananias and Sapphira incident. Peter accuses Ananias of lying to the Holy Spirit (v.3) and then immediately equates this with lying to God (v.4).

- ψεύσασθαί (G5574) — V-ADN — Aor Mid Inf | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-ASN] | Ἅγιον ἅγιος G40 [A-ASN]

**Notes:** v.3: pseudomai (G5574, Aor Mid Inf) se to Pneuma to Hagion — 'to lie to (accusative) the Holy Spirit.' v.4: ouk epseuso (G5574, Aor Mid Ind 2S) anthropois alla to Theo (N-DSM, 'to God'). The shift from 'lied to the Holy Spirit' to 'lied to God' without any qualification is a direct identification. The verb pseudomai with the Spirit as object requires a personal being who can be the target of deception.

### Spirit speaks direct commands to Philip

> Then the Spirit said unto Philip, Go near, and join thyself to this chariot.

**Context:** The Ethiopian eunuch narrative. The Spirit gives Philip a direct, specific command.

**Notes:** eipen (G3004, V-2AAI-3S 'said') to Pneuma (N-NSN) to Philippo (N-DSM) — 'the Spirit said to Philip.' The verb lego/eipen (to say/speak) with the Spirit as grammatical subject — the Spirit issues a personal command. The command is specific and situational, not a general principle.

### Spirit uses first-person 'I' — ego apestalka

> While Peter thought on the vision, the Spirit said unto him, Behold, three men seek thee. Arise therefore, and get thee down, and go with them, doubting nothing: for I have sent them.

**Context:** Cornelius narrative. The Spirit speaks to Peter using first-person pronouns.

- εἶπεν (G3004) — V-2AAI-3S — | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN]
- ἐγὼ (G1473) — P-1NS — Nom Sg | ἀπέσταλκα ἀποστέλλω G649 [V-RAI-1S] Perf Act Ind 1st person

**Notes:** v.19: eipen to Pneuma (N-NSN 'the Spirit said'). v.20: ego (P-1NS, first-person 'I') apestalka (G649, V-RAI-1S Perf Act Ind 1st person 'I have sent') autous (them). The Spirit uses the first-person pronoun ego and the first-person verb form — 'I have sent them.' An impersonal force does not say 'I.'

### Spirit speaks in first person: 'Separate me Barnabas and Saul'

> As they ministered to the Lord, and fasted, the Holy Ghost said, Separate me Barnabas and Saul for the work whereunto I have called them.

**Context:** The church at Antioch worshiping and fasting. The Holy Spirit speaks to the assembly with first-person pronouns.

- εἶπεν (G3004) — V-2AAI-3S — | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN] | μοι ἐγώ G1473 [P-1DS] Dat Sg | προσκέκλημαι προσκαλέω G4341 [V-RNI-1S] Perf M/P Ind 1st person

**Notes:** eipen (V-2AAI-3S 'said') to Pneuma to Hagion (N-NSN/A-NSN). Then first-person: Aphorisate (V-AAM-2P 'Separate') de moi (P-1DS, dative 'me') — the Spirit says 'Separate FOR ME.' proskeklemai (V-RNI-1S, Perf M/P Ind 1st person 'I have called') autous — 'I have called them.' Two first-person forms (moi and proskeklemai) with the Spirit as speaker. This is decisive personal speech.

### Spirit exercises joint decision-making with the apostles

> For it seemed good to the Holy Ghost, and to us, to lay upon you no greater burden than these necessary things;

**Context:** Jerusalem Council decree. The apostles coordinate the Spirit as a co-deliberator alongside themselves.

**Notes:** edoxen (G1380, V-AAI-3S 'it seemed good') to Pneumati to Hagio (N-DSN/A-DSN, dative) kai hemin (P-1DP, 'and to us'). The Spirit is placed alongside the apostles as a co-deliberator using the same dative construction — 'to the Spirit... and to us.' This grammatical coordination presupposes equal personal agency.

### Spirit forbids and does not permit — volitional overruling

> Now when they had gone throughout Phrygia and the region of Galatia, and were forbidden of the Holy Ghost to preach the word in Asia, After they were come to Mysia, they assayed to go into Bithynia: but the Spirit suffered them not.

**Context:** Paul's second missionary journey. The Holy Spirit actively overrules human plans.

**Notes:** v.6: koluthentes (G2967, V-APP-NPM Aor Pass Ptcp 'having been forbidden') hupo tou Hagiou Pneumatos (by the Holy Spirit) — the Spirit as agent of prohibition (hupo + genitive = personal agent). v.7: ouk eiasen (G1439, V-AAI-3S 'did not permit') autous to Pneuma Iesou ('the Spirit of Jesus') — the Spirit exercises volitional authority to override apostolic plans.

### Spirit = YHWH — Paul attributes Isaiah 6:9-10 to the Holy Spirit

> And when they agreed not among themselves, they departed, after that Paul had spoken one word, Well spake the Holy Ghost by Esaias the prophet unto our fathers, Saying, Go unto this people, and say, Hearing ye shall hear, and shall not understand; and seeing ye shall see, and not perceive: For the heart of this people is waxed gross, and their ears are dull of hearing, and their eyes have they closed; lest they should see with [their] eyes, and hear with [their] ears, and understand with [their] heart, and should be converted, and I should heal them.

**Context:** Paul's final appeal to the Roman Jews. He attributes the words of Isaiah 6:9-10, spoken by YHWH in Isaiah's throne-room vision, to the Holy Spirit.

- Πνεῦμα (G4151) — N-NSN — | ἐλάλησεν λαλέω G2980 [V-AAI-3S] Aor Act Ind

**Notes:** to Pneuma to Hagion (N-NSN) elalesen (G2980, V-AAI-3S 'spoke') dia Esaiou — 'the Holy Spirit spoke through Isaiah.' In the original context (Isa 6:8-10), it is YHWH (Adonai) who speaks. Paul directly identifies the Holy Spirit as the YHWH who spoke in Isaiah's vision. The verb laleo ('spoke') with Spirit as subject — personal speech attribution.

### Spirit bears witness with our spirits — personal co-testimony

> The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God:

**Context:** Romans 8 — the Spirit's ministry to believers. The Spirit co-testifies with the believer's spirit about adoption.

- Πνεῦμα (G4151) — N-NSN — | συνμαρτυρεῖ συμμαρτυρέω G4828 [V-PAI-3S] Pres Act Ind

**Notes:** auto to Pneuma (P-NSN + N-NSN) sunmarturei (G4828, V-PAI-3S Pres Act Ind 'bears witness with') to pneumati hemon. The sym- prefix ('with') in symmartureo indicates joint, coordinated testimony between two distinct witnesses. The Spirit testifies alongside the human spirit — a personal act of co-witness.

### Spirit intercedes and has phronema (mind) — cognitive faculties

> Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered. And he that searcheth the hearts knoweth what [is] the mind of the Spirit, because he maketh intercession for the saints according to [the will of] God.

**Context:** Romans 8 — the Spirit's intercessory ministry. The Spirit has both cognitive and emotional capacity: a mind (phronema) and intercession with groanings.

- Πνεῦμα (G4151) — N-NSN — | ὑπερεντυγχάνει ὑπερεντυγχάνω G5241 [V-PAI-3S] Pres Act Ind
- ἐραυνῶν (G2045) — V-PAP-NSM — | οἶδεν οἶδα G1492 [V-RAI-3S] | φρόνημα φρόνημα G5427 [N-NSN] | Πνεύματος πνεῦμα G4151 [N-GSN] | ἐντυγχάνει ἐντυγχάνω G1793 [V-PAI-3S]

**Notes:** v.26: auto to Pneuma (N-NSN) huperentugchanei (G5241, V-PAI-3S, hapax legomenon 'intercedes on behalf of') stenagmois alaletois ('with unutterable groanings'). v.27: to phronema (G5427, N-NSN 'the mind/mindset') tou Pneumatos (G4151, N-GSN). Also entugchanei (G1793, V-PAI-3S 'intercedes') huper hagion kata theon. The Spirit possesses a phronema — a settled disposition, mindset, way of thinking. Phronema occurs only 6 times in NT, all in Romans 8. It is attributed to both 'the flesh' (8:6-7) and 'the Spirit' (8:6, 8:27). Impersonal forces do not have a phronema.

### Spirit searches and knows — cognitive verbs requiring personal subject

> But God hath revealed [them] unto us by his Spirit: for the Spirit searcheth all things, yea, the deep things of God. For what man knoweth the things of a man, save the spirit of man which is in him? even so the things of God knoweth no man, but the Spirit of God.

**Context:** Paul's argument about divine revelation. The Spirit is the agent through whom God reveals hidden wisdom.

- Πνεῦμα (G4151) — N-NSN — Nom Sg N | ἐραυνᾷ ἐραυνάω G2045 [V-PAI-3S] Pres Act Ind
- οἶδεν (G1492) — V-RAI-3S — Perf Act Ind | πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN] | ἔγνωκεν γινώσκω G1097 [V-RAI-3S] Perf Act Ind | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN]

**Notes:** v.10: to Pneuma (N-NSN) panta erauna (G2045, V-PAI-3S 'searches all things') kai ta bathe tou Theou ('even the deep things of God'). v.11: to Pneuma tou Theou (N-NSN) — paralleled with 'the spirit of man which is in him.' Paul's analogy: just as a person's own spirit knows their inner thoughts, so God's Spirit knows God's inner thoughts. The verb eraunao means to investigate thoroughly — a cognitive act. The verb oiden (G1492, V-RAI-3S 'knows') is used for the human spirit's self-knowledge. Paul attributes the same type of knowledge to the Spirit of God — personal cognitive agency.

### Spirit distributes gifts as he wills — clearest volitional attribution

> But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Spirit, dividing to every man severally as he will.

**Context:** Paul's discussion of spiritual gifts. The Spirit is the sovereign distributor of gifts — exercising personal volition.

- ἐνεργεῖ (G1754) — V-PAI-3S — Pres Act Ind | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-NSN] Nom Sg N | διαιροῦν διαιρέω G1244 [V-PAP-NSN] Pres Act Ptcp Nom Sg N | βούλεται βούλομαι G1014 [V-PNI-3S] Pres M/P Ind

**Notes:** to hen kai to auto Pneuma (N-NSN 'the one and the same Spirit'): energei (G1754, V-PAI-3S 'energizes/works'), diairoun (G1244, V-PAP-NSN Pres Act Ptcp 'distributing'), kathos bouletai (G1014, V-PNI-3S Pres M/P Ind 'as he wills'). boulomai denotes deliberate, reasoned decision — not mere wish but settled resolve of a rational agent. The middle voice (bouletai) emphasizes the Spirit's own deliberate personal choice. Three verbs: energizes, distributes, wills — all requiring a volitional subject.

### Do not grieve the Holy Spirit — emotional capacity requiring personhood

> And grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption.

**Context:** Ephesians 4:25-32 ethical instruction. Paul inserts this command about the Spirit amidst behavioral exhortations — grieving is a personal emotional response.

- λυπεῖτε (G3076) — V-PAM-2P — Pres Act Impv | Πνεῦμα πνεῦμα G4151 [N-ASN] | Ἅγιον ἅγιος G40 [A-ASN] | ἐσφραγίσθητε σφραγίζω G4972 [V-API-2P] Aor Pass Ind

**Notes:** me (negative particle) lupeite (G3076, V-PAM-2P Pres Act Impv 'grieve') to Pneuma to Hagion tou Theou. The construction me + present imperative = prohibitive present imperative: 'stop grieving' or 'do not continue grieving' — implying an ongoing action being prohibited. lupeo means to cause grief, pain, sadness — an emotional response that requires a personal subject capable of feeling. The sealing language (esphragisthete, Aor Pass Ind 'you were sealed') connects the Spirit's personal role to the believer's security.

### Blasphemy against the Spirit — unforgivable personal offense

> Wherefore I say unto you, All manner of sin and blasphemy shall be forgiven unto men: but the blasphemy [against] the [Holy] Ghost shall not be forgiven unto men. And whosoever speaketh a word against the Son of man, it shall be forgiven him: but whosoever speaketh against the Holy Ghost, it shall not be forgiven him, neither in this world, neither in the [world] to come.

**Context:** Matthew 12:22-37. The Pharisees attribute Jesus' Spirit-empowered exorcism to Beelzebub. Jesus warns that blasphemy against the Spirit is unforgivable.

- Πνεύματος (G4151) — N-GSN — | βλασφημία βλασφημία G988 [N-NSF]

**Notes:** v.31: he de tou Pneumatos blasphemia (G988, N-NSF + G4151 N-GSN) ouk aphethesetai — 'the blasphemy of/against the Spirit shall not be forgiven.' The genitive tou Pneumatos is objective genitive — blasphemy directed against the Spirit. v.32: kata tou Pneumatos tou Hagiou (genitive) — 'against the Holy Spirit.' Blasphemeo (G987) fundamentally means to speak against, revile, defame — it requires a personal object. You cannot blaspheme electricity or gravity. The context makes the sin specific: attributing the Spirit's work to Satan.

### Always resisting the Holy Spirit — personal opposition language

> Ye stiffnecked and uncircumcised in heart and ears, ye do always resist the Holy Ghost: as your fathers [did], so [do] ye.

**Context:** Stephen's speech before the Sanhedrin. He accuses them of perpetuating their fathers' pattern of resisting the Spirit.

The Spirit is in the dative as the indirect object of opposition. The historical pattern ('as your fathers') presupposes the Spirit was personally active throughout Israel's history.

### Insulting the Spirit of grace — treating a person with contempt

> Of how much sorer punishment, suppose ye, shall he be thought worthy, who hath trodden under foot the Son of God, and hath counted the blood of the covenant, wherewith he was sanctified, an unholy thing, and hath done despite unto the Spirit of grace?

**Context:** Hebrews 10:26-31 warning against deliberate sin after receiving knowledge of truth. Three offenses listed in escalating order.

**Notes:** Three parallel offenses: (1) katapatesas (trampling) the Son of God, (2) hegosamenos (counting) the blood unclean, (3) enubrisas (G1796, V-AAP-NSM Aor Act Ptcp 'having insulted') to Pneuma tes charitos (N-ASN + N-GSF 'the Spirit of grace'). enubrizo means to insult, treat with contempt, outrage — it requires a personal victim. The Spirit is coordinated with the Son of God and the covenant blood in a three-part offense structure.

### The Holy Ghost saith — Spirit attributed YHWH's Psalm 95 speech

> Wherefore (as the Holy Ghost saith, To day if ye will hear his voice, Harden not your hearts, as in the provocation, in the day of temptation in the wilderness: When your fathers tempted me, proved me, and saw my works forty years. Wherefore I was grieved with that generation, and said, They do alway err in [their] heart; and they have not known my ways. So I sware in my wrath, They shall not enter into my rest.)

**Notes:** legei (G3004, V-PAI-3S 'says') to Pneuma to Hagion (N-NSN/A-NSN) — 'the Holy Spirit says.' The present tense legei implies ongoing address (not just past speech). The quoted text has first-person divine speech: 'they tempted me... I was grieved... I sware in my wrath.' The Hebrews author attributes this YHWH speech to the Holy Spirit — direct Spirit = YHWH identification. Also note autou (P-GSM, masculine genitive 'his') in 'his voice' referring to the Spirit.

### The Holy Ghost is a witness — Spirit attributed Jeremiah 31 speech

> [Whereof] the Holy Ghost also is a witness to us: for after that he had said before, This [is] the covenant that I will make with them after those days, saith the Lord, I will put my laws into their hearts, and in their minds will I write them; And their sins and iniquities will I remember no more.

**Context:** Hebrews attributes the new covenant promise of Jeremiah 31:33-34 to the testimony of the Holy Spirit.

**Notes:** Marturei (G3140, V-PAI-3S 'bears witness/testifies') hemin kai to Pneuma to Hagion — 'the Holy Spirit also testifies to us.' The Spirit is described as a martureo-ing agent — testimony is a personal, judicial act. The quoted text is from Jeremiah 31:33-34 where YHWH speaks. The Spirit is both testifier (personal act) and identified with the YHWH who made the covenant promise.

### Through the eternal Spirit — Spirit's agency in the atonement

> How much more shall the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without spot to God, purge your conscience from dead works to serve the living God?

**Context:** Hebrews 9:11-15. The superiority of Christ's sacrifice — offered through (dia) the eternal Spirit.

- Πνεύματος (G4151) — N-GSN — Gen Sg N | αἰωνίου αἰώνιος G166 [A-GSN] Gen Sg N | προσήνεγκεν προσφέρω G4374 [V-AAI-3S]

**Notes:** dia (PREP) Pneumatos (G4151, N-GSN genitive) aioniou (G166, A-GSN 'eternal'). dia + genitive = through/by means of — indicating agency. The Spirit is the agent through whom Christ offered himself. The adjective aionios ('eternal') is the same word applied to God (Rom 16:26, 'the everlasting God') and to eternal life. If the Spirit is eternal, the Spirit possesses a divine attribute. This makes the Spirit an active participant in the atonement, not a passive instrument.

### The Lord is that Spirit — direct identification with kyrios/YHWH

> Now the Lord is that Spirit: and where the Spirit of the Lord [is], there [is] liberty.

**Context:** Paul's discussion of the veil of Moses and the new covenant ministry. He directly identifies the Lord with the Spirit.

**Notes:** ho Kurios (G2962, N-NSM 'the Lord') to Pneuma (G4151, N-NSN) estin (V-PAI-3S 'is'). Direct predication: 'the Lord IS the Spirit.' In LXX context, kyrios = YHWH. Paul then says 'the Spirit of the Lord' (to Pneuma Kuriou) — showing the Spirit is both identified with YHWH and distinguished from YHWH in an economic sense. This is a direct Spirit-deity text.

### Triadic formula — singular name governing three persons

> Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost:

**Context:** The Great Commission. Jesus commands baptism in the singular name (onoma) of three persons.

### Paidion (neuter) with masculine pronoun — gender override parallel

> And when they were departed, behold, the angel of the Lord appeareth to Joseph in a dream, saying, Arise, and take the young child and his mother, and flee into Egypt, and be thou there until I bring thee word: for Herod will seek the young child to destroy him.

- παιδίον (G3813) — N-ASN — Acc Sg N (neuter) | αὐτοῦ αὐτός G846 [P-GSM] Gen Sg M (masculine — gender override) | αὐτό αὐτός G846 [P-ASN] Acc Sg N (neuter — standard agreement)

> By pureness, by knowledge, by longsuffering, by kindness, by the Holy Ghost, by love unfeigned,

**Context:** Paul lists his commendations. The Holy Ghost appears in a list of virtues/qualities.

**Notes:** en hagnotteti, en gnosei, en makrothumia, en chrestoteti, en Pneumati Hagio, en agape anupokrito. Anti-personhood argument: Spirit listed among impersonal qualities (purity, knowledge, etc.). Counter: Paul's list mixes personal and impersonal items — this is an epistolary convention, not an ontological category. 'In the Holy Spirit' describes the sphere of Paul's ministry, not an attribute of Paul.

### God is a consuming fire — counter to Spirit-as-fire objection

> For the LORD thy God [is] a consuming fire, [even] a jealous God.

**Context:** Moses' warning against idolatry. God is described as a consuming fire.

**Notes:** YHWH elohekha esh okhlah (H784/H398 'fire consuming'). God is called fire without negating his personhood. If fire imagery for God does not disprove God's personhood, fire/quenching imagery for the Spirit does not disprove the Spirit's personhood. This is the parallel-treatment test.

### Do not I fill heaven and earth? — God fills, yet is personal

> Can any hide himself in secret places that I shall not see him? saith the LORD. Do not I fill heaven and earth? saith the LORD.

**Context:** YHWH's omnipresence declaration. God 'fills' heaven and earth.

**Notes:** anokhi male (H4390 'I fill'). God fills heaven and earth — the same filling/pervasion language used for the Spirit. If God's filling heaven and earth does not negate his personhood, the Spirit's filling believers does not negate the Spirit's personhood.

### Christ dwell in your hearts — personal being uses dwelling language

> That Christ may dwell in your hearts by faith; that ye, being rooted and grounded in love,

**Context:** Paul's prayer for the Ephesians. Christ is described as dwelling in hearts.

**Notes:** katoikesai (G2730 'to dwell') ton Christon en tais kardiais humon. Christ dwells in believers' hearts — the same 'dwelling/indwelling' language used for the Spirit (1 Cor 6:19). Christ's indwelling does not negate his personhood. Therefore the Spirit's indwelling does not negate the Spirit's personhood.

### The Holy Ghost made you overseers — Spirit appoints church leaders

> Take heed therefore unto yourselves, and to all the flock, over the which the Holy Ghost hath made you overseers, to feed the church of God, which he hath purchased with his own blood.

**Context:** Paul's farewell to the Ephesian elders. The Spirit is credited with appointing church overseers.

**Notes:** to Pneuma to Hagion etheto (G5087, V-2AMI-3S 'has placed/appointed') humas episkopous ('you as overseers'). The Spirit appoints leaders — an authoritative, personal act of governance.

### Take not thy holy spirit from me — personal relational presence

> Cast me not away from thy presence; and take not thy holy spirit from me.

**Context:** David's penitential psalm after his sin with Bathsheba. He pleads for the Spirit's continued presence.

**Notes:** ruach qodshekha (H7307/H6944 + 2ms 'your holy spirit') al-tiqqach (H3947 'do not take') mimmenni ('from me'). Parallel with 'thy presence' (panekha) — the Spirit = God's personal presence. The withdrawal of the Spirit is the withdrawal of a person, not the deactivation of a force.

### The everlasting God — same aionios used for the Spirit in Heb 9:14

> But now is made manifest, and by the scriptures of the prophets, according to the commandment of the everlasting God, made known to all nations for the obedience of faith:

**Context:** Paul's doxology. God is called 'everlasting' (aionios) — the same adjective applied to the Spirit in Hebrews 9:14.

**Notes:** tou aioniou (G166) Theou — 'the eternal God.' Same adjective aionios applied to God here and to the Spirit (pneumatos aioniou) in Heb 9:14. If the Spirit shares the same eternal attribute as God, the Spirit shares a divine characteristic.

### A sin unto death — connected to unpardonable sin against the Spirit

> If any man see his brother sin a sin [which is] not unto death, he shall ask, and he shall give him life for them that sin not unto death. There is a sin unto death: I do not say that he shall pray for it.

**Context:** John's discussion of sin and prayer. The 'sin unto death' likely connects to the blasphemy against the Spirit.

**Notes:** hamartia pros thanaton ('sin unto death'). Connected to the unforgivable sin of Matt 12:31-32 and Heb 10:26-29 — sins against the Holy Spirit that reach a point of no return.

### Full OT context: Angel, Holy Spirit, YHWH — three agents

> In all their affliction he was afflicted, and the angel of his presence saved them: in his love and in his pity he redeemed them; and he bare them, and carried them all the days of old. But they rebelled, and vexed his holy Spirit: therefore he was turned to be their enemy, [and] he fought against them. Then he remembered the days of old, Moses, [and] his people, [saying], Where [is] he that brought them up out of the sea with the shepherd of his flock? where [is] he that put his holy Spirit within him?

**Context:** Isaiah's recounting of God's dealings with Israel — three agents appear: YHWH, the Angel of his presence, and his Holy Spirit.

**Notes:** v.9: malakh panav ('angel of his presence/face') saved them. v.10: itsbu (H6087 Piel 'they vexed') et-ruach qodsho ('his holy spirit'). v.11: 'where is he that put his holy spirit within him?' Three distinct agents: YHWH who loves and redeems, the Angel of his presence who saves, and his Holy Spirit who can be vexed and who was put within Moses/Israel. The Spirit is possessed by YHWH ('his') yet distinct from YHWH — personal and relational.

### Not by might nor by power but by my Spirit — YHWH's possessive

> Then he answered and spake unto me, saying, This [is] the word of the LORD unto Zerubbabel, saying, Not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit, saith the LORD of hosts.

**Context:** Zechariah's vision of the lampstand. YHWH declares his Spirit as the means of accomplishment.

**Notes:** lo ve-chayil ve-lo ve-koach ki im-be-ruchi (H7307 + 1us 'by my spirit') neum YHWH tsevaot. YHWH uses the first-person possessive 'my Spirit' — relational language implying distinct-yet-owned agency. The Spirit contrasts with impersonal might (chayil) and power (koach) — the Spirit is the personal alternative to mere force.

### He shall glorify me — Spirit glorifies Christ (false prophet parallel)

> He shall glorify me: for he shall receive of mine, and shall shew [it] unto you.

**Context:** Continuation of John 16:13-15 — the Spirit's Christ-glorifying ministry, paralleled by the false prophet's beast-glorifying role.

**Notes:** ekeinos (D-NSM) eme doxasei (G1392 V-FAI-3S 'will glorify me'). The Spirit glorifies Christ. The false prophet (Rev 13:12) causes worship of the beast. The parody requires a personal original.

### Sealed with the Holy Spirit — Spirit seals (false prophet parallel)

> In whom ye also [trusted], after that ye heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation: in whom also after that ye believed, ye were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise,

**Context:** The Spirit's sealing work — marking believers as God's own. Paralleled by the false prophet's marking in Rev 13:16.

**Notes:** esphragisthete (G4972 'you were sealed') to Pneumati tes epangelias. The Spirit seals believers' foreheads (cf. Rev 7:3, Ezek 9:4). The false prophet marks foreheads with the beast's number (Rev 13:16). The parody of sealing with marking presupposes a personal Spirit whose work can be counterfeited.

### Triadic gift distribution — Spirit, Lord, God working in parallel

> Now there are diversities of gifts, but the same Spirit. And there are differences of administrations, but the same Lord. And there are diversities of operations, but it is the same God which worketh all in all. But the manifestation of the Spirit is given to every man to profit withal. For to one is given by the Spirit the word of wisdom; to another the word of knowledge by the same Spirit; To another faith by the same Spirit; to another the gifts of healing by the same Spirit; To another the working of miracles; to another prophecy; to another discerning of spirits; to another [divers] kinds of tongues; to another the interpretation of tongues: But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Spirit, dividing to every man severally as he will.

**Context:** Paul's full discussion of spiritual gifts — the Spirit distributes as a sovereign personal agent.

**Notes:** The triadic structure (vv.4-6) and the Spirit's sovereign distribution (v.11 bouletai) combine in this passage. The Spirit is the organizing principle of the church's gifting — a role that requires intelligence, purpose, and will.

### Blasphemy against the Holy Ghost — Markan parallel

> But he that shall blaspheme against the Holy Ghost hath never forgiveness, but is in danger of eternal damnation:

**Context:** Mark's parallel to Matthew 12:31-32. Blasphemy against the Spirit has no forgiveness — ever.

**Notes:** blasphemese (G987, V-AAS-3S 'shall blaspheme') eis to Pneuma to Hagion — 'against the Holy Spirit.' Mark uses eis (into/against) rather than Matthew's kata (against). 'Never forgiveness' (ouk echei aphesin eis ton aiona) — the most absolute formulation.

---

### Blasphemy against the Holy Ghost — Lukan parallel

> And whosoever shall speak a word against the Son of man, it shall be forgiven him: but unto him that blasphemeth against the Holy Ghost it shall not be forgiven.

**Context:** Luke's parallel to Matthew 12:31-32. Same distinction between forgivable speech against the Son and unforgivable blasphemy against the Spirit.

**Notes:** to eis to Hagion Pneuma blasphemesanti (G987, V-AAP-DSM 'the one having blasphemed') ouk aphethesetai. Lukan form confirms the three-gospel testimony to the unforgivable nature of anti-Spirit blasphemy.
