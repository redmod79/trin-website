- Question: "When Jesus says 'the only true God' (John 17:3) and Paul says 'one God, the Father' (1 Corinthians 8:6), do these passages exclude Jesus from deity?"

### The Grammatical Debate (ekeinos/parakletos/pneuma)

**Wallace position (scholarly mainstream):** Masculine pronouns (ekeinos) in John 14-16 follow standard grammatical agreement with the masculine antecedent parakletos, not a deliberate gender override for the neuter pneuma. Grammar alone neither proves nor disproves personhood.

**Clouzet position (JATS, Adventist):** John deliberately broke gender agreement (masculine ekeinos for neuter pneuma) as an indication of personhood. The gender override is linguistically noteworthy.

**Bowman's contribution:** Neuter grammatical gender is irrelevant to personhood — the neuter paidion ("child") refers to the person Jesus 11 times in the NT (Matt 2:8, 9, 11, 13, 14, 20; Luke 2:17, 27, 40). In Matt 2:13, masculine auto appears for neuter paidion.

**Ministry Magazine (2012):** "The gender of parakletos, as well as that of pneuma, is nothing more than a linguistic accident." Warns against deriving theological conclusions from grammatical gender. Genuine evidence for personhood lies in functional descriptions.

### The Functional/Semantic Evidence

**Thoennes (Crossway):** Catalogs personal actions: comforts (John 14:16), teaches (John 14:26), speaks (Acts 8:29; 13:2), makes decisions (Acts 15:28), grieves (Eph 4:30), overrules (Acts 16:6-7), searches (1 Cor 2:10-11), distributes gifts (1 Cor 12:11), intercedes (Rom 8:26-27), assures (Rom 8:16), testifies (John 15:26).

**Bible.org:** Three marks of personhood — intellect (1 Cor 2:10-11), emotion (Eph 4:30), volition (1 Cor 12:11).

**Lectionary Greek:** In 1 Cor 12:11, bouletai (present middle indicative of boulomai, "as he wills") locates personal will in the Spirit. Combined with energei (energizes) and diairei (distributes) — all three verbs require a volitional subject.

### Anti-Personhood Arguments (from BeliefMap)

Eight arguments identified for engagement:
1. Pneuma is neuter — counter: neuter gender describes personal agents (demons as "spirits")
2. Liquid/metaphorical language — "poured out," "fills," "baptized in" — counter: same metaphors apply to Father/Son
3. "Quenching" the Spirit (1 Thess 5:19) — counter: metaphorical for suppressing influence
4. Impersonal representations — dove, fire, wind, oil — counter: God = fire (Exo 3:2-4), Christ = bread, vine, light
5. Absence from Father-Son salutation formulas — counter: present in triadic formulas elsewhere
6. No proper name — counter: YHWH shared by all (2 Cor 3:17)
7. List context (2 Cor 6:6) — Spirit listed among impersonal virtues — counter: mixed category lists common
8. Testimony pattern (1 John 5:8) — Spirit grouped with water and blood — counter: all three "testify," a personal act

### OT Evidence (Moskala, JATS 2013)

- Genesis 1:2: Spirit's active engagement in creation
- Isaiah 63:10: "they grieved His Holy Spirit" — capacity to be grieved = personal quality
- Challenges the view that OT believers lacked understanding of Spirit's personal dimensions
