# Bible Study: If Jesus is not God but a created being or merely a perfect human, what logical contradictions arise within the Bible's own framework? Trace the consequences across six domains: (1) worship — Jesus accepts what only God may receive (Ex 20:3-5, Matt 4:10 vs. Matt 14:33, 28:9, John 9:38, 20:28), (2) forgiveness — Jesus exercises what only God can do (Mark 2:5-7), (3) creation — all things made through him yet he is a thing made (John 1:3, Col 1:16), (4) savior exclusivity — YHWH alone is savior yet Jesus saves (Isa 43:11 vs. Titus 2:13, Acts 4:12), (5) prayer/omnipresence — prayer directed to Jesus and his promise to be present everywhere (Acts 7:59, 2 Cor 12:8, Matt 18:20, 28:20), (6) atonement sufficiency — can a finite sacrifice provide eternal redemption (Heb 9:12-14, Heb 7:16, 25)?

2. **Thomas's Confession (John 20:28) — The Explicit Deity Claim Accepted:**
Thomas's 'ho kyrios mou kai ho theos mou' is addressed directly TO Jesus (eipen auto = 'said to him'), refuting the anti-deity 'exclamation' reading. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2316-theos.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G2962-kyrios.md for the semantic ranges of these terms. Run greek_parser.py --verse on John 20:28 to verify the grammatical construction — the doubled article with personal pronoun 'mou' makes this a personal confession of identity, not a surprised oath. Jesus responds in 20:29 without correction, pronouncing blessing on those who believe — constituting acceptance of the title 'my God.' This occurs just 11 verses after 'my God and your God' (20:17), creating the interpretive tension that prior study trin-05 resolved. If Jesus is not God, he accepts the most explicit deity confession in the NT without demurral — the moral implications are devastating.
**Verses:** John 20:28, John 20:29, John 20:17
**Strong's:** G2316, G2962

3. **Forgiveness as Divine Prerogative — Mark 2:5-7 and the Delegation Counterargument:**
The scribes' reasoning in Mark 2:7 is theologically correct — forgiveness of sins against God IS God's exclusive prerogative (Isa 43:25, Dan 9:9, Exo 34:6-7). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G859-aphesis.md, $WORD_STUDIES/H5545-calach.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/WG-forgiveness.md for the forgiveness vocabulary chain. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Mark 2:5, 2:7, and 2:10. The delegation counterargument (Zarley) cites Matt 9:8 and John 20:23 — the research agent should retrieve these in full context and test whether 'authority to men' (plural anthropois) refers to Jesus specifically or humanity generally. The decisive test is whether Jesus forgives sins committed against God rather than against himself — the paralytic never sinned against Jesus personally, yet Jesus declared absolute forgiveness. If Jesus is merely human, he usurped God's prerogative by his own disciples' correct theological reasoning. Luke 7:48-49 repeats the pattern independently.
**Verses:** Mark 2:5-7, Mark 2:10, Luke 7:48-49, Luke 5:20-21, Isa 43:25, Dan 9:9, Exo 34:6-7, Matt 9:8, John 20:23
**Strong's:** G859, H5545, G1849

4. **The Creation Paradox — 'All Things' Made Through Christ vs. 'Alone/By Myself':**
Four NT witnesses independently assign creation to Christ: John 1:3 (panta di autou egeneto), Col 1:16 (ta panta di autou kai eis auton ektistai), Heb 1:2 (di hou kai epoiesen tous aionas), 1 Cor 8:6 (di hou ta panta). READ $WORD_STUDIES/H1254-bara.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2936-ktizo.md, $WORD_STUDIES/WG-created.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/H905-bad.md. Yet YHWH declares in Isa 44:24 that he created 'alone' (lebaddi) and 'by myself' (me'itti) — excluding even instrumental agency by a created assistant. Run hebrew_parser.py --verse on Isa 44:24 to verify the emphatic exclusionary language. If Christ is a created being, then: (1) he is part of 'all things' that were made through him — creating himself; (2) YHWH lied about creating alone. Neither is tenable. The resolution: Christ is within YHWH's identity, not a separate created agent. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot on John 1:3 to find creation-exclusivity parallels, and --hybrid-nt on Isa 44:24 to find NT creation-through-Christ passages.
**Verses:** John 1:3, Col 1:16-17, Heb 1:2, 1 Cor 8:6, Isa 44:24, Isa 45:18
**Strong's:** G2936, H1254, H905, G3956
