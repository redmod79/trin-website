# Verses

## Question
If Jesus is not God but a created being or merely a perfect human, what logical contradictions arise within the Bible's own framework? Trace the consequences across six domains: (1) worship — Jesus accepts what only God may receive (Ex 20:3-5, Matt 4:10 vs. Matt 14:33, 28:9, John 9:38, 20:28), (2) forgiveness — Jesus exercises what only God can do (Mark 2:5-7), (3) creation — all things made through him yet he is a thing made (John 1:3, Col 1:16), (4) savior exclusivity — YHWH alone is savior yet Jesus saves (Isa 43:11 vs. Titus 2:13, Acts 4:12), (5) prayer/omnipresence — prayer directed to Jesus and his promise to be present everywhere (Acts 7:59, 2 Cor 12:8, Matt 18:20, 28:20), (6) atonement sufficiency — can a finite sacrifice provide eternal redemption (Heb 9:12-14, Heb 7:16, 25)?

### First Commandment: Worship Belongs to YHWH Alone

> Thou shalt have no other gods before me. Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness [of any thing] that [is] in heaven above, or that [is] in the earth beneath, or that [is] in the water under the earth: Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them: for I the LORD thy God [am] a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth [generation] of them that hate me;

**Context:** The Ten Commandments at Sinai. The first commandment establishes the absolute exclusivity of worship — no bowing down to or serving anything other than YHWH.

**Notes:** Domain 1 foundation. The Hebrew shachah (H7812, bow down) and abad (H5647, serve) establish the worship prohibition that Jesus himself cites in Matt 4:10. If Jesus accepts the same worship that YHWH commands exclusively for himself, the first commandment is either violated or Jesus is within YHWH's identity.

---

### Serve YHWH Exclusively — Quoted by Jesus in the Temptation

> Thou shalt fear the LORD thy God, and serve him, and shalt swear by his name.

**Context:** Moses' instruction to Israel in the Shema context (Deut 6:4-15). Commands exclusive fear, service, and oath-loyalty to YHWH.

**Notes:** Jesus cites this passage in Matt 4:10 to refuse Satan's worship request. The irony is devastating if Jesus is not God: he cites the exclusive-worship command and then accepts the very worship he said belongs only to God.

---

### Jesus Cites Exclusive Worship Command Against Satan

> Then saith Jesus unto him, Get thee hence, Satan: for it is written, Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve.

**Context:** The wilderness temptation. Satan offers Jesus the kingdoms of the world in exchange for worship. Jesus refuses by citing the exclusive-worship command.

**Notes:** Jesus explicitly affirms that worship belongs to God alone ('him only shalt thou serve'). He then accepts worship from disciples (Matt 14:33, 28:9, 28:17) without any correction. The accept/refuse contrast with this verse is the structural crux of Domain 1.

### Angel Refuses Worship a Second Time — Double Witness

### Peter Refuses Worship from Cornelius

> And as Peter was coming in, Cornelius met him, and fell down at his feet, and worshipped [him]. But Peter took him up, saying, Stand up; I myself also am a man.

**Context:** Cornelius, a devout Roman centurion, meets Peter and falls at his feet in worship. Peter immediately refuses.

**Notes:** Peter's reason for refusing: 'I myself also am a man.' The implication is that a man (human) must not accept worship. If Jesus is merely a man and accepts worship, Peter's refusal is incoherent.

### Jesus Forgives the Sinful Woman — Independent Forgiveness Witness

> And he said unto her, Thy sins are forgiven. And they that sat at meat with him began to say within themselves, Who is this that forgiveth sins also?

**Context:** Simon the Pharisee's house. A sinful woman anoints Jesus' feet. Jesus pronounces forgiveness. The dinner guests question his authority.

**Notes:** Independent witness (not a Synoptic parallel to Mark 2). The guests' question 'Who is this that forgiveth sins also?' shows the same recognition: forgiveness of sins is a divine prerogative. The woman did not sin against Jesus personally, yet Jesus declares absolute forgiveness.

### Parallel Forgiveness Account — Scribes Challenge 'Who Can Forgive Sins But God Alone?'

> And when he saw their faith, he said unto him, Man, thy sins are forgiven thee. And the scribes and the Pharisees began to reason, saying, Who is this which speaketh blasphemies? Who can forgive sins, but God alone?

**Context:** Luke's parallel to Mark 2. The same incident with the same theological challenge from the religious leaders.

**Notes:** Luke adds 'the Pharisees' to the scribes, broadening the witness to the recognition that forgiveness is God's exclusive prerogative. The phrase 'but God alone' (ei me monos ho Theos) is even more emphatic than Mark's 'but God only.'

### YHWH: 'I, Even I, Am He That Blotteth Out Thy Transgressions' — Forgiveness as Divine Prerogative

> I, [even] I, [am] he that blotteth out thy transgressions for mine own sake, and will not remember thy sins.

**Context:** Isaiah 43 — YHWH's self-identification as Israel's creator, redeemer, and exclusive savior. After declaring 'beside me there is no saviour' (43:11), YHWH declares he alone blots out transgressions.

**Notes:** The emphatic doubled 'I, even I' (anoki anoki) mirrors 43:11. Forgiveness is grounded in YHWH's own initiative ('for mine own sake'), not in human delegation. The Hebrew machah (blot out) is exclusively divine in context — no human intermediary is mentioned. This establishes the OT principle that Jesus exercises in Mark 2:5-7.

### 'All Things Were Made by Him' — Creation Domain Crux

> All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made.

**Context:** John 1 prologue. The Word (Logos) who was 'with God' and 'was God' (1:1) is identified as the agent of all creation.

**Notes:** Greek: panta di autou egeneto kai choris autou egeneto oude hen ho gegonen. 'All things' (panta) admits no exceptions. The double negative ('without him was not any thing made') is emphatic: absolutely nothing in creation exists apart from the Word's agency. If the Word is a created being, he is part of 'all things' and therefore made himself — a logical impossibility.

### The Son as Creator and Exact Representation of God's Being

> Hath in these last days spoken unto us by [his] Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the worlds; Who being the brightness of [his] glory, and the express image of his person, and upholding all things by the word of his power, when he had by himself purged our sins, sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high;

**Context:** Hebrews 1 opening — the Son's identity statement. The author establishes the Son as heir, creator, sustainer, and exact divine representation before beginning the angel-superiority argument.

**Notes:** Greek: charakter tes hypostaseos autou (G5481 + G5287) — 'exact imprint of his substance/being.' Charakter means a precise reproduction, like a coin stamped from a die. Hypostasis is the underlying reality or essential being. The Son is not merely similar to God — he is the exact representation of God's essential nature. Combined with 'by whom he made the worlds' (di hou kai epoiesen tous aionas), this places the Son within God's own creative identity.

> Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved.

> To the Lord our God [belong] mercies and forgivenesses, though we have rebelled against him;

**Context:** Daniel's prayer of confession for Israel. He attributes mercies and forgiveness to God as belonging to his character.

**Notes:** Forgiveness (selichot, plural) 'belongs' to God as a divine attribute. This reinforces the scribes' reasoning in Mark 2:7 — forgiveness is God's prerogative.

### YHWH's Self-Description: Forgiving Iniquity, Transgression, and Sin

> And the LORD passed by before him, and proclaimed, The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth, Keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear [the guilty]; visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children, and upon the children's children, unto the third and to the fourth [generation].

**Context:** YHWH's self-revelation to Moses on Sinai after the golden calf incident. This is the most complete divine self-description in the OT.

**Notes:** YHWH's own self-description includes 'forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin.' Forgiveness is part of YHWH's essential character. When Jesus exercises this same forgiveness in Mark 2:5, he acts in YHWH's own capacity.

### 'My Glory Will I Not Give to Another' — Glory Exclusivity

> I [am] the LORD: that [is] my name: and my glory will I not give to another, neither my praise to graven images.

**Context:** Isaiah 42 — YHWH declares his exclusive right to glory and praise.

**Notes:** YHWH says his glory (kavod) will not be given to 'another.' Yet the Lamb receives identical doxa (glory) in Rev 5:12-13. Either the Lamb is 'another' (violating this declaration) or the Lamb is within YHWH's identity. This verse directly tests the 'delegated worship' model.

### 'Before Me There Was No God Formed' — Unique Divine Identity

> Ye [are] my witnesses, saith the LORD, and my servant whom I have chosen: that ye may know and believe me, and understand that I [am] he: before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me.

**Context:** Isaiah 43:10-13 — YHWH's self-identification as the unique, uncreated God. This immediately precedes the savior exclusivity claim in 43:11.

**Notes:** YHWH declares 'before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me.' This eliminates any 'created God.' If Jesus is a created being who exercises divine prerogatives, he is a 'God formed after' YHWH, contradicting this text.

### 'Whatsoever Ye Shall Ask in My Name, That Will I Do' — Jesus Answers Prayer

> And whatsoever ye shall ask in my name, that will I do, that the Father may be glorified in the Son. If ye shall ask any thing in my name, I will do [it].

**Context:** The Upper Room Discourse. Jesus promises to personally answer prayers made in his name.

**Notes:** Jesus does not say 'the Father will do it' — he says 'I will do it' (ego poieso). He claims personal agency in answering prayer. This requires omniscience and omnipotence. No created being can guarantee to answer 'whatsoever ye shall ask' across all believers for all time.

### 'We Will Come and Make Our Abode' — Father and Son Indwelling

> Jesus answered and said unto him, If a man love me, he will keep my words: and my Father will love him, and we will come unto him, and make our abode with him.

**Context:** The Upper Room Discourse. Jesus promises that both he and the Father will indwell believers.

**Notes:** The 'we' (Father and Son) will 'make our abode' with individual believers. This requires simultaneous indwelling in every believer — omnipresence. Jesus places himself alongside the Father in the same indwelling activity.

### Joel 2:32 Applied to Jesus — 'Call Upon the Name of the Lord'

> For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved.

**Context:** Paul's argument for universal salvation by faith. He quotes Joel 2:32 (calling on YHWH) and applies it to Jesus (from context — Rom 10:9-12).

**Notes:** Joel 2:32 in the Hebrew: 'whosoever shall call on the name of YHWH shall be delivered.' Paul applies this to Jesus in the context of confessing 'Jesus is Lord' (10:9). The transfer of YHWH-invocation language to Jesus is one of the clearest YHWH-title transfers in the NT.

### 'God Was Manifest in the Flesh' — Incarnation Statement

> And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, received up into glory.

**Context:** Paul's statement on the 'mystery of godliness' — a creedal summary of Christ's incarnation, ministry, and glorification.

**Notes:** The KJV reads 'God was manifest in the flesh.' While textual variants exist, the creedal statement identifies the incarnate one with God. The six-line hymn traces a trajectory from divine manifestation to heavenly glorification.

### 'I Am the First and the Last... I Am Alive for Evermore' — YHWH Title + Indestructible Life

> And when I saw him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, Fear not; I am the first and the last: [I am] he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death.

**Context:** The risen Christ appears to John on Patmos. Jesus identifies himself with YHWH's exclusive title and declares his indestructible life.

**Notes:** Jesus claims the title 'the first and the last' — which YHWH claims exclusively in Isa 44:6 ('beside me there is no God'). He then adds 'I am alive for evermore' — connecting the YHWH title to the indestructible life of Heb 7:16.

### 'I Am the First and the Last; Beside Me There Is No God' — YHWH Title Cluster

> Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer the LORD of hosts; I [am] the first, and I [am] the last; and beside me [there is] no God.

**Context:** Isaiah 44 — YHWH's self-declaration. The 'first and last' title is embedded in an exclusivity claim.

**Notes:** The title 'first and last' appears in a context that excludes any other deity. When Jesus takes this title in Rev 1:17, he takes a title whose context says no one else can hold it. Delegation cannot explain transferring the title 'there is no God beside me' to a non-God being.

### Co-Worship Formula: Identical Blessing to God and the Lamb

> And every creature which is in heaven, and on the earth, and under the earth, and such as are in the sea, and all that are in them, heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, [be] unto him that sitteth upon the throne, and unto the Lamb for ever and ever.

**Context:** Revelation 5 climax — every creature in the universe directs the same worship formula to both God on the throne and the Lamb.

**Notes:** The worship formula is identical for both recipients: eulogia, time, doxa, kratos. The duration is identical: eis tous aionas ton aionon. The scope is universal: pan ktisma (every creature). If the Lamb is a creature, every creature worships a fellow creature with the same formula they worship God.

### Human 'Saviours' in Temporal Deliverance — Counterargument Passage

> Therefore thou deliveredst them into the hand of their enemies, who vexed them: and in the time of their trouble, when they cried unto thee, thou heardest [them] from heaven; and according to thy manifold mercies thou gavest them saviours, who saved them out of the hand of their enemies.

**Context:** Nehemiah 9 — the Levites' prayer recounting Israel's history. God gave human 'saviours' (moshiim) who delivered Israel from military enemies.

**Notes:** Hebrew moshiim (plural hiphil participle of yasha, H3467) — same root as Isa 43:11 moshia. The anti-deity counterargument cites this to disprove savior exclusivity. However: (1) these are plural temporal military deliverers, not THE savior; (2) context is national deliverance, not eternal salvation from sin; (3) God 'gave them' these saviors — delegated for temporal purposes; (4) Isa 43:10-13 broadens beyond temporal deliverance to ontological uniqueness.

### 'Saviours Shall Come Up on Mount Zion' — Human Savior Usage

> And saviours shall come up on mount Zion to judge the mount of Esau; and the kingdom shall be the LORD'S.

**Context:** Obadiah's prophecy about Edom's judgment. Human 'saviours' will judge Esau, but the kingdom belongs to YHWH.

**Notes:** Same Hebrew root moshiim as Neh 9:27. The conclusion is 'the kingdom shall be the LORD'S' — the human saviors execute judgment under YHWH's sovereignty. This temporal, delegated deliverance is qualitatively different from Jesus' eternal salvation.

### Disciples Given Authority to Forgive — Delegation Counterargument

> Whose soever sins ye remit, they are remitted unto them; [and] whose soever [sins] ye retain, they are retained.

**Context:** Post-resurrection appearance. Jesus breathes on the disciples and grants authority regarding sins.

**Notes:** The delegation counterargument cites this to show forgiveness was given to humans. However: (1) the authority comes FROM Jesus, presupposing Jesus's own divine authority to delegate; (2) the verb forms suggest declarative authority — announcing forgiveness that God has already granted; (3) Jesus himself forgave the paralytic BEFORE any such delegation, exercising inherent authority.

### High Priest Charges Blasphemy at Jesus' Deity Claim

> But he held his peace, and answered nothing. Again the high priest asked him, and said unto him, Art thou the Christ, the Son of the Blessed? And Jesus said, I am: and ye shall see the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven. Then the high priest rent his clothes, and saith, What need we any further witnesses? Ye have heard the blasphemy: what think ye? And they all condemned him to be guilty of death.

**Context:** Jesus' trial before the Sanhedrin. The high priest asks directly whether Jesus is the Christ. Jesus answers 'I am' and adds the Son of Man coming in glory.

**Notes:** The high priest charges blasphemy — contemporaneous proof that Jesus' claims were recognized as deity claims by first-century Jewish authorities. The 'ego eimi' response combined with Daniel 7:13 and Psalm 110:1 allusions constitutes a threefold deity claim.

### YHWH as Creator — Applied to Jesus in Heb 1:10-12

> Of old hast thou laid the foundation of the earth: and the heavens [are] the work of thy hands. They shall perish, but thou shalt endure: yea, all of them shall wax old like a garment; as a vesture shalt thou change them, and they shall be changed: But thou [art] the same, and thy years shall have no end.

**Context:** A psalm addressed to YHWH as creator and eternal being. The psalmist contrasts YHWH's permanence with creation's transience.

**Notes:** In the original psalm, the addressee is unambiguously YHWH — the creator of heaven and earth who endures unchanged while creation perishes. Hebrews 1:10-12 applies this entire passage to the Son. If the Son is a creature, Hebrews misapplies a YHWH-exclusive creation text.

### 'One Mediator... Who Gave Himself a Ransom for All' — Ransom Scope

> For [there is] one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus; Who gave himself a ransom for all, to be testified in due time.

**Context:** Paul's instruction on prayer for all people. He grounds universal prayer in the universal scope of Christ's mediation and ransom.

**Notes:** The ransom is antilytron hyper panton (a ransom for all). The scope exceeds what any finite creature can cover. Psalm 49:7-8 says no man can redeem his brother; here one man's ransom covers all.

### Surplus Vocabulary: The Gift Far Exceeds the Offense

> But not as the offence, so also [is] the free gift. For if through the offence of one many be dead, much more the grace of God, and the gift by grace, [which is] by one man, Jesus Christ, hath abounded unto many. And not as [it was] by one that sinned, [so is] the gift: for the judgment [was] by one to condemnation, but the free gift [is] of many offences unto justification. For if by one man's offence death reigned by one; much more they which receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness shall reign in life by one, Jesus Christ.) Therefore as by the offence of one [judgment came] upon all men to condemnation; even so by the righteousness of one [the free gift came] upon all men unto justification of life. For as by one man's disobedience many were made sinners, so by the obedience of one shall many be made righteous. Moreover the law entered, that the offence might abound. But where sin abounded, grace did much more abound:

**Context:** Romans 5 — Paul's Adam-Christ comparison. The gift in Christ surpasses the damage done by Adam.

**Notes:** Paul's surplus vocabulary (pollo mallon = 'much more'; hyperperisseuo = 'super-abound') formally denies exact equivalence between offense and gift. The gift far exceeds the damage. One man's act affecting all humanity requires a person of infinite worth.

### Baptismal Formula: Singular 'Name' for Father, Son, Spirit

> Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost:

**Context:** The Great Commission. Jesus commands baptism in the singular 'name' (onoma) of Father, Son, and Spirit.

**Notes:** The singular 'name' (to onoma) governing all three persons implies a shared identity. Placing the Son alongside the Father and Spirit under one 'name' in a baptismal formula is a Trinitarian structure that makes no sense if the Son is a creature.

### Messiah Called 'THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS' — YHWH Title on the Branch

> Behold, the days come, saith the LORD, that I will raise unto David a righteous Branch, and a King shall reign and prosper, and shall execute judgment and justice in the earth. In his days Judah shall be saved, and Israel shall dwell safely: and this [is] his name whereby he shall be called, THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS.

**Context:** Jeremiah's messianic prophecy. The coming Davidic king will bear the name 'YHWH our Righteousness.'

**Notes:** The Messiah's name is YHWH Tsidqenu — 'YHWH our Righteousness.' This is the divine name (YHWH, H3068) applied to the messianic king — an OT YHWH-title transfer anticipating the NT pattern.

### 'This Is the True God, and Eternal Life' — Applied to Jesus

> And we know that the Son of God is come, and hath given us an understanding, that we may know him that is true, and we are in him that is true, [even] in his Son Jesus Christ. This is the true God, and eternal life.

**Context:** Near the close of 1 John. John identifies Jesus Christ as 'the true God and eternal life.'

**Notes:** The nearest antecedent of 'This' (houtos) is 'his Son Jesus Christ' — identifying Jesus as 'the true God.' The same author who wrote 'the only true God' (John 17:3) also calls Jesus 'the true God.'
